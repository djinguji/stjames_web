WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. PRAYERS FOR OUR HAITI VOLUNTEER GROUP. On Thursday, September 2 a group of eight volunteers from St. James will be heading to Haiti. They will be serving for a week in Milot at the Sacré Coeur Hospital, run by the CRUDEM Foundation. A couple of the volunteers are nurses and will work with patients. The rest will be helping to restore the hospital chapel. Please keep the group and the people of Haiti in your prayers. You can also join the group for a special send-off blessing at the 5:30pm Mass on Monday, August 30. Thank you for your support.

CABRINI MINISTRY TRAINING offers quality training that includes 5 months of weekly sessions with emphasis on pastoral skills, along with ethics, self-awareness, and more. CMT graduates are ministering in parishes, hospitals, nursing homes, and prisons; giving solace to the elderly, persons with special needs, the homebound, the homeless, the dying, and many more. Plan to attend an Information Meeting on September 13 from 7 – 8:30 pm at Cathedral Place, Rooms 1EW. RSVP (or for more information), Tom Curran, 206-760-0583 Ext 4.

ENGAGING SPIRITUALITY focuses on the link between spirituality and engaged compassion by emphasizing personal and collective prayer, reflection on the writings of spiritual masters, personal experiences with the poor, and contemplative dialogue in a small faith community. This 21-week program taps into ancient and contemporary wisdom and inspires participants to develop spiritual practices that keep them rooted in Gospel justice and grounded in love. Sessions will be held on Thursday evenings, beginning in late October. Learn more about the program at an Information Night on Thursday, September 16, 6:30 pm, in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Mary Brown, 206-335-4541; Robin Lawlor, 206-228-8144.

WINTER SHELTER VOLUNTEER TRAINING. Results from the 2010 One Night Count showed over 2,750 people living on the streets of King County. With difficult economic times in our nation; we are likely to see even more people facing a homelessness crisis. As part of our response to the need in our community, St. James will again be hosting a Winter Shelter from October to April. We are gearing up for our 19th year of operation and will be holding a training for all new and returning volunteers on Sunday, September 19 at 7pm in Cathedral Hall. We are in need of overnight hosts, someone to organize our toiletries donations, and volunteers to make breakfast in the mornings. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.
