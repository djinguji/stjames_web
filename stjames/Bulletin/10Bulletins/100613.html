June 13, 2010

Dear Friends,

I want to take this opportunity to thank each one of you who has contributed to the Annual Catholic Appeal. As always, your generosity is quite amazing. During difficult economic times you have dug deep and taken us closer to achieving our combined Archdiocesan and parish goal of $470,330. I am more grateful than words can express. And to those of you who are in a position to but who have not yet made a gift, I would encourage you to do so. There’s still time!

Deacon David Olsen, who served as our sacristan for three years and who took a regular turn preaching at weekend Masses has been asked by the Archdiocese to assume a new ministry among the Native American communities of Snohomish County. I am happy that he will have this new opportunity for diaconal ministry and I know he will bring many blessings to the people he serves, but I am also very sorry to see him and his wife Reeny leave St. James. Like you, I will miss David’s calming, peaceful presence and his insightful and challenging homilies. This weekend, during the hospitality following the morning Masses, you will not only have a chance to participate in the Health Fair, but also an opportunity to greet David , to sign a parish “card” for him, and to thank him for his ministry here at St. James. David, our prayers accompany you on this new chapter of your journey of faith and service!

And one more thing to share with you: after nearly 20 years serving as my assistant, Kay Kaiser has made the difficult decision to retire. As you can imagine, this is a happy moment for Kay but a difficult moment for me as well as for the parish. There is no way I can begin to calculate how many lives Kay has touched over her years of service here: how many people she has touched by her warmth and kindness, how many people she has patiently helped over the phone and in person, how many ruffled feathers she has smoothed, how many appointments and interviews she has scheduled for me, how many parties and receptions she has planned and hosted, and the list could go on and on. Chances are, you are one of the countless people Kay has helped over the years. If so, I know you will take time to say a special prayer of thanks for her and a prayer, too, that her well-deserved retirement will be everything she hopes for.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
