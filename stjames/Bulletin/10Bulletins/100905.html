WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

ENGAGING SPIRITUALITY focuses on the link between spirituality and engaged compassion by emphasizing personal and collective prayer, reflection on the writings of spiritual masters, personal experiences with the poor, and contemplative dialogue in a small faith community. This 21-week program taps into ancient and contemporary wisdom and inspires participants to develop spiritual practices that keep them rooted in Gospel justice and grounded in love. Engaging Spirituality is a program of JustFaith Ministries, and is being offered for the third year here at St. James Cathedral. Sessions will be held on Thursday evenings, beginning in late October. Learn more about the program at an Information Night on Thursday, September 16, 6:30 pm, in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Mary Brown, 206-335-4541; Robin Lawlor, 206-228-8144.

CABRINI MINISTRY TRAINING. Our goal is to provide parishes and local institutions with capable, confident, faith-filled lay ministers. We offer quality training that includes 5 months of weekly sessions with emphasis on pastoral skills, along with ethics, self-awareness, and more. CMT graduates are ministering in parishes, hospitals, nursing homes, and prisons; giving solace to the elderly, persons with special needs, the homebound, the homeless, the dying, and many more. Plan to attend an Information Meeting on September 13 from 7–8:30pm at Cathedral Place, Rooms 1EW. RSVP (or for more information), Tom Curran, 206-760-0583 Ext 4.

WINTER SHELTER VOLUNTEER TRAINING. Results from the 2010 One Night Count showed over 2,750 people living on the streets of King County. With difficult economic times in our nation; we are likely to see even more people facing homelessness. As part of our response to the need in our community, St. James will again be hosting a Winter Shelter from October to April. We are gearing up for our 19th year of operation and will be holding a training for all new and returning volunteers on Sunday, September 19 at 7pm in Cathedral Hall. We are in need of overnight hosts, someone to organize our toiletries donations, and volunteers to make breakfast in the mornings. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.
