St. James

July 25, 2010

Dear Friends,

I am writing this early in the week and already I am hearing some long-range weather forecasts that are predicting rain. Well, if it comes, we will deal with it but I don’t think it’s going to. When you read this you’ll know whether my optimism was warranted! In any case, I know we’ll have a great celebration. And I know you’ll want to join me in thanking Trent Mummery and Stephen Pace, and their team of more than fifty (!) volunteers, for making this great event possible.

We’re doing a very low-key fundraiser today. The kneelers on the chairs in the Cathedral are now 16 years old and the padding on them is pretty much non-existent. (If you sit in the chairs you don’t need me to tell you this!) It’s going to cost approximately $22,000 to reupholster 600 kneelers—about $40 per kneeler. If you’re in a position to help, we would be most grateful!

It is wonderful to be able to celebrate the feast of St. James with all of you. It was on this day 22 years ago that I was installed as Cathedral pastor by Archbishop Hunthausen, and celebrated the Feast of St. James with this community for the first time. In some ways, that seems like a long, long time ago to me; in other ways, it feels like yesterday! Over these years you have let me lead you in prayer and Eucharist and you have let me be your teacher and your companion on the great journey of faith. People sometimes ask me what my greatest joys are as a priest. I tell them that my greatest joy is being able to gather with all of you Sunday after Sunday to celebrate the Eucharist in our magnificent Cathedral. There are many other joys of priestly ministry, but this one surpasses them all. How grateful I am to be a part of this wonderful community of faith. Happy Feast Day!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

DUWAMISH RIVER ECOJUSTICE BOAT TOUR. In 2001, the Environmental Protection Agency listed the Lower Duwamish River as a federal Superfund site, naming it as one of the most contaminated sites in the United States. The St. James Environmental Justice Group, together with the Duwamish River Cleanup Coalition and several other downtown Seattle faith communities, is sponsoring a prayerful and reflective boat tour of the Lower Duwamish on Saturday, August 14 from noon to 2pm. The boat tour will provide an opportunity to learn about the state of Seattle’s major river and our call to practice environmental justice. $15 per person and reservations are required. Information, Rachael Ross at 206-903-4629.
