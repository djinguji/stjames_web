Bulletin Archive

Easter V

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

PRAY FOR IMMIGRANTS. This weekend, all across the country, people of various faith traditions will be praying for immigrants. Be a part of this powerful weekend of prayer to continue to move fair and just immigration reform forward for our families, our communities, and for our nation. Stop by our Justice for Immigrants table at coffee hour after the 10:00 mass, or attend our Immigration Dialogue today for an informal discussion on immigration issues from 11:15 - 11:45 am in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Look for the “Prayer for Immigrants and Exiles” on our website. Information Christopher Koehler at 206-382-4511.

COMMUNITY SUPPORTED AGRICULTURE. The Environmental Justice Group is pleased to team up with Clean Greens Farm to bring community supported agriculture to St. James. Clean Greens Farm is a local farm that grows chemical- and pesticide-free produce, which will be delivered to St. James for pickup after the weekend masses. Subscribers can sign up for all or part of the growing season, June through October. This is a great way to eat healthy and support the movement for environmentally responsible farming practices. Members of the Environmental Justice Group will be present after masses today with more information and to answer your questions. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

KATHRYN WELD, MEZZO-SOPRANO. Friday, May 7, 8:15 pm Cathedral soloist Kathryn Weld sings sacred songs with texts from the Song of Songs as part of the Cathedral’s Song of Songs festival, with Joseph Adam, piano. Compositions by Bern H. Herbolsheimer, Hindemith, Barber, Wolf, and others. Suggested donation $15, students and seniors pay as able. Information 206-382-4874.

SEATTLE PRO MUSICA. Friday, May 14, 2010 8:15pm & Saturday, May 15, 2010 8:00pm Cathedral Resident Ensemble, Seattle Pro Musica continues the Song of Songs Festival with choral settings of texts from the Song of Songs from all over the world, including world premieres by Ivan Moody and Karen P. RCIA SPONSOR INFORMATION MEETING. We continue to have many new inquirers coming to participate in our Christian Initiation process to learn more about the Catholic Church (RCIA). We need St. James parishioners to be companions with our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. On Wednesday, May 5, 7-9pm, Cathedral Place—second floor, there will be an information meeting to learn more about RCIA and the role of the sponsor. All training and support will be provided and the time commitment is reasonable-a minimum of 2 sessions a month (Wednesday evenings). The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

YOUNG ADULTS (21 - THIRTY-SOMETHING) “FIRST-THURSDAY” SOCIAL… The Capitol Club at 414 East Pine, Seattle continues to be a favorite of ours, so we will gather there for fun conversation, food and drink Thursday, May 6 at 7:00pm. The menu is "Tapas Style" with flavors from the Mediterranean. Enjoy great food in a comfortable environment! Serving casual class to the Hill since 1996, the Capitol Club is a favorite of those who enjoy a fun evening out – so bring your friends. Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP will be Wednesday May 12. We will visit both the Washington State History Museum and the Museum of Glass in Tacoma. The search for Sasquatch, folk art treasures, icons of Washington, and, of course, watching artists transform molten glass into art in the Hot Shop will be just a few of the exhibits we’ll see. In between we’ll lunch at the Renaissance Café. Please join us! Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners. Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

DETENTION MINISTRY. “I was in prison and you visited me.” These words of Jesus call us to reach out with compassion to those in detention in our local prisons and jails. Tom Wagner, the Assistant Director of Pastoral Care Services for the Archdiocese, will be speaking on the joys and challenges of detention ministry. Come, learn more, and explore whether you are being called to this important ministry. Thursday, May 13, 7:00 pm, in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

WORLDWIDE MARRIAGE ENCOUNTER. It takes much effort just to make ends meet in our world today. Do you wish you could talk about something besides all the stresses in your lives? A Worldwide Marriage Encounter Weekend gives you the time and tools to revitalize romance, deepen communication, and nourish the spirituality in your marriage. Coming weekends are June 4-6, Tacoma area and June 11-13, Seattle area. Information John and Martha Ryland (Tacoma), 253-859-9174, jmryland@comcast.net or Rory and Dawn Myers (Seattle), 206-772-2344, seattlewwme@yahoo.com.

CANTILENA, A MEDIEVAL OPEN SING. Sunday, May 16, 2010 7:00pm Medieval Women’s Choir director Margriet Tindemans will direct several settings of texts from the Song of Songs, mostly in Latin. Scores in modern notation will be provided. Space is limited in the beautiful Chapel at St. James Cathedral. Registration and information 206-264-4822 or www.medievalwomenschoir.org.

PEOPLE WITHOUT HOMES: FROM AWARENESS TO ACTION. Since its inception last year, the St. James Housing Advocacy Committee has worked to increase action within the parish to end homelessness. On Thursday, May 20 we are hosting a presentation by Laura Clark and Rich Gamble at 7pm in Cathedral Hall. Laura will share her experience of living through homelessness and the knowledge she has gained through two decades of work in human services. Rich, a Catholic Community Services employee, will help us learn how our efforts and advocacy can make a difference. Join us as we increase our resolve and deepen our awareness of how to effectively end homelessness in our community. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

Thomas and Shira Kammen. Ms. Kammen will be playing medieval fiddle and harp. Information 206-264-4822, www.medievalwomenschoir.org.

POST-TRAUMATIC STRESS DISORDER (PTSD)—BEYOND RETURNING HOME. Tuesday May 25, 7:00pm, Pastoral Outreach Center. Matthew Jakupcak, PhD and Michelle Upham, MSW, Deployment Health Clinic of the VA Puget Sound Health Care System, will discuss the challenges Iraq/Afghanistan veterans face in reintegrating their personal and professional lives following a combat tour. Anyone interested in or touched by PTSD is invited to come and learn about the physical and mental stressors associated with combat. Sponsored by the Health and Healing Ministry.

SPRING BLOOD DRIVE THANK YOU. There were 40 participants in the Spring Blood Drive, for a total potential benefit of up to 81 different patients. This is a powerful support for our community and the help provided to those patients will echo through the relief of families, friends, and care providers. The Puget Sound Blood Center and the St. James Health and Healing Ministry extend a grateful thank you to all who participated.

ART OF THE SONG OF  SONGS. Now through May 31, the Cathedral Chapel is home to an exhibition by local artists entitled The Pleasures of the Garden. The works depict gardens, fruit, flowers, music, light, and shadow — imagery inspired by the Old Testament Song of Songs. Click here for more information about the Song of Songs Festival.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Brendan in Bothell, St. Philip in Woodland, and St. Joseph the Worker Mission in Yacolt.
