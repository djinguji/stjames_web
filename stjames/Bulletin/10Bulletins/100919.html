WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

STOP BY THE CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE after Mass today for a wide selection of spiritual reading, as well as our own Cathedral items—terrific CDs from our music program, postcards, notecards, and more!

CHILDREN'S FAITH FORMATION. Sunday School registration will be TODAY in Cathedral Hall after the 8:00am, 10:00am, and 12:00 Noon Masses. This ministry at St. James offers faith formation opportunities to children from Pre-School (4-year olds) through 12th grade. In addition, this ministry is seeking volunteers who can give part of their Sunday mornings to help make a difference in the faith lives of young 4-6 year olds and 6th grade youth as catechists or catechist assistants. CATHEDRAL “VISION” SURVEY. This past week we sent out a short “20 Questions” survey to 1,000 parish households. If you received one, please do consider taking just a few moments to fill it out and return it. Your input is important! The results of the survey will help our Pastoral Vision Council to create a new vision statement for the parish.

THE YOUTH MUSIC PROGRAM is now registering students for the fall semester. Students in grade 1-4 may participate at any time, without audition, in one of our two training choirs which rehearse from 4:30-6:00 pm every Thursday. Exceptionally talented young singers may audition for the advanced Schola Cantorum which rehearses Thursdays from 4:30-6:30. Scholarships are available. Information, please contact the Music Office at 206-382-4874.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can find out more information at RCIA website. It is not too late to join our new inquiry session on Wednesday evenings. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH. What does the Catholic Church believe and what difference does it make it my life? Using the Bible, The Catechism of the Catholic Church, and Church documents, this adult enrichment program will explore the Catholic interpretation of scripture, key theological concepts, and the history of the Church. This is a wonderful opportunity for an in-depth look at Catholic teaching and its implications for our everyday lives. Classes this year include Prayer, Mary and the Saints, Catholic Social Teaching, Letters of St. Paul, Trinity, History of the Church in the USA and the Creed. You can come to one or all classes. Classes take place on 10/17/10, 10/24/10, 1/9/11, 1/23/11, 3/6/11, and 3/13/11 at St. Peter’s in Beacon Hill from 1:30-5:30 PM. Cost: $5 per class. (Cosponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

THE ARCHBISHOP THOMAS J. MURPHY MILLENIUM ORGAN turns ten! St. James Cathedral celebrates the 10th anniversary of the Rosales east apse pipe organ with a year-long series of programs featuring our two Cathedral organists and special guests. The first program on October 15, at 8:00 pm, features Thomas Joyce, newly-appointed Assistant Organist at Grace Cathedral, San Francisco. Dr. Joyce was assistant organist at St. Mark’s Cathedral in Seattle for the past seven years. His program will feature works for cello and organ, organ duet, and the premiere of his Magnificat and Nunc Dimittis with the Women’s Schola of Seattle Pro Musica, a Cathedral Resident Ensemble. Suggested donation is $15, students and seniors pay as able. Information 206-382-4874 or Cathedral music website.

WINTER SHELTER VOLUNTEER TRAINING. Results from the 2010 One Night Count showed over 2,750 people living on the streets of King County. With difficult economic times in our nation; we are likely to see even more people facing homelessness. As part of our response to the need in our community, St. James will again be hosting a Winter Shelter from October to April. We are gearing up for our 19th year of operation and will be holding a training for all new and returning volunteers TONIGHT, Sunday, September 19 at 7pm in Cathedral Hall. We are in need of overnight hosts, someone to organize our toiletries donations, and volunteers to make breakfast in the mornings. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

Dear Friends,

The announcement this past week that Pope Benedict has appointed Bishop J. Peter Sartain, formerly Bishop of Joliet, Illinois, as our new Archbishop is wonderful news for this local church. I know you will want to join me in praying for him as he prepares to assume the awesome responsibilities that will be his. Pray, too, for Archbishop Brunett as he prepares to step aside after leading this church for nearly 13 years.

May the Holy Spirit continue to guide our Church during these days of transition!

Father Michael G. Ryan
