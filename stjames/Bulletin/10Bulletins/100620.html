Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last week, we had visitors from Charlotte, NC; St. Petersburg, FL; and Chesapeake, VA, among other places. The winner of our drawing was from North Las Vegas, NV.

PRIESTS’ DAYS. The priests of the Archdiocese will be in Ocean Shores for their annual gathering during the coming week. From Monday, June 21 through Thursday, June 24, there will be one Mass in the Cathedral each day at 5:30pm. There will be no 8:15am Mass during this time, but the Office of Morning Prayer will be prayed in the Chapel each day at 8:15am. The regular Mass schedule will resume on Friday, June 25.

ST. JAMES CATHEDRAL YOUTH MUSIC PROGRAM CHOIR CAMP 2010. August 9-13, and Sunday morning, August 15, open to young singers grades 4-8, in the Archdiocese of Seattle. This week-long camp, led by some of the region’s finest Catholic music educators, offers excellent training in liturgical music. Students learn musical skills, note reading, singing techniques and participate in daily Morning Prayer in the Cathedral. For information and auditions, call the Cathedral Music Office at 206-382-4874.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL: “LETTERS TO GOD” Join us on Tuesday, July 13 in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm. Tyler Doherty is an extraordinary 8 year old boy. Surrounded by a loving family and community, and armed with the courage of his faith, he faces his daily battle against cancer. To Tyler, God is a friend, a teacher and the ultimate pen pal. Tyler’s prayers take the form of letters which he composes and mails on a daily basis. These letters transforms many other lives in unexpected ways. Rated PG. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, July 14 at 7:00 pm. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514, or go visit our webpage.

TOURS OF THE CATHEDRAL. During the summer months, June through August, we offer tours of the Cathedral every Wednesday at 1:00pm. Gather in the west vestibule; no reservations required. This is a great opportunity to show your Cathedral to out of town guests!

PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30 pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

WANTED: RCIA SPONSORS FOR YOUNG ADULTS. We have several young adults, including one married couple, in RCIA who are still in need of sponsors. If you are interested, please contact Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

AIRLINE MILES NEEDED FOR VOLUNTEER TRIP TO HAITI. A group of St. James volunteers is preparing for a service trip to a hospital in Haiti at the end of August. We are looking for donations of Alaska and/or American Airlines miles to help volunteers offset the cost of the trip. Please consider helping us provide service in Haiti by donating 60,000 miles from either airline. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

DONATION COLLECTION FOR THE YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT. On July 18, teens from St. James and St. Mary’s will head to Burlington, WA to participate in the Youth Migrant Project. The teens will volunteer in migrant daycare centers, run a reading camp, experience farm work, and have time to reflect on their experiences. On Sunday, June 27 and July 11, the teens will be collecting donations for the migrant families at Coffee Hour. They will also be selling tostadas on Sunday, July 11 to raise money for needed supplies and trip costs. Please stop by with your donations of rice, beans, flour, sugar, blankets, casual coats, school supplies, and/or monetary contributions. For a complete list of needed items. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Thomas More in Lynnwood and St. Aloysius in Buckley.
