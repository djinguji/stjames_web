WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. The winner of our drawing was from Binghamton, NY.

RELIEF FOR FLOOD VICTIMS IN PAKISTAN. The recent flooding in Pakistan, the worst in the nation’s history, has already killed 1,500 people and has displaced 16 million more. Catholic Relief Services is on the ground, rebuilding bridges and bringing relief to the people of Pakistan. At the conclusion of Masses this weekend, the ushers will be on hand to receive any offering you would like to make to the flood relief efforts. Please make checks payable to CRS. The regular St. Vincent de Paul collection has been postponed to next week, August 22.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can find out more information at RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin on Wednesday evening, September 7. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

CATHOLIC RELIEF SERVICES UPDATE: HAITI EARTHQUAKE – SIX MONTHS LATER. In the six months following the earthquake in Haiti, CRS has been working to assist 900,000 made vulnerable by the devastation. Relief has come in the form of programs in food relief, emergency shelters, water provisions and sanitation, health consultations and emergency operations, seed fairs for agriculture stimulation, protection through safe zones for the vulnerable and cash-for-work opportunities. For more information please visit the CRS website. The Missions Office reports that $2,219,000 has been contributed to CRS by Archdiocese of Seattle parishes and schools, and businesses and others in western Washington – a record response from here for disaster relief and redevelopment.

WELCOME, PARENTS WITH YOUNG CHILDREN. We support all of our parishioners who are attending Mass with young children and we welcome them! If you find that your child is getting restless, please feel free to step into the west vestibule or the cathedral chapel, where we have provided a sound system so that you can continue to participate in the Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship.

Please note that babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.
