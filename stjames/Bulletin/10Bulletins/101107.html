WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

HOTEL ON THE CORNER OF BITTER AND SWEET. Join us for a discussion on Thursday, November 11, at 7:00 pm for a lively discussion of this book by Jamie Ford. When the Panama Hotel is reopened in Seattle’s International District, Henry Lee is reminded of Keiko Okabe. He befriended the Japanese American girl at Rainier Elementary in the 1940s, just before she and her family were taken to the interment camps. We will be meeting in the Mother Mary Rose Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Christopher Koehler, 206-382-4511.

THE ST. JAMES YOUTH MUSIC PROGRAM is seeking young singers ages 6-9 for participation in our two training choirs. Taught by some of Seattle's finest music educators, our classes teach singing technique, music fundamentals such as rhythm and pitch, and active & engaged participation in the liturgy through singing. Enroll now for participation in Archbishop Sartain's installation services, and holiday liturgies including Readings and Carols and Christmas Eve Vigil Mass.

MONTEVERDI 1610 VESPERS. Friday & Saturday, December 3 & 4, 8:00 pm Commemorating the 400th year of the publication of this collection, St. James Cathedral with Pacific Musicworks and the Early Music Guild present Seattle’s first period performance of this immortal masterpiece. Stephen Stubbs directs an all-star ensemble of vocal soloists and instrumentalists with special guests from Seattle Baroque Orchestra and Italy’s Concerto Palatino. Parishioners may obtain reserved passes by contacting the Cathedral Music Office, 206-382-4874.

Information and registration, Christopher Koehler at 206-382-45151.
