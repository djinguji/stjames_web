JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Beginning this week, our newly Elect are wearing brown robes while attending Mass and other liturgies during Lent. This helps them to know, in a concrete way, that they have entered a new and significant stage in their final preparations for Easter. It also anticipates another change that will take place when they emerge from the baptismal waters at the Easter Vigil and change to white robes. Seek them out in our midst and, if possible, introduce yourself to them after Mass. Let them know you are praying for them and looking forward to Easter when they will join us at the Eucharistic table.

LENTEN PRAYER PARTNERS FOR THE ELECT. This is the time to “adopt” one of our Elect as they prepare to celebrate the sacraments of Baptism, Confirmation and Eucharist. You are asked to pray every day for him/her asking for God’s blessings and guidance. Also, notes and cards will be collected during Holy Week to be given to the Elect before Easter. Prayer cards are available at the place of prayer located in the north aisle of the west nave of the Cathedral (where you can also learn more about each of them in our Book of the Elect), in the bookstore, and in Cathedral Hall during Sunday morning hospitality. You can also meet our Elect. Your prayers make a difference!!! Information Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

OPERATION RICE BOWL

In Luke’s story of the temptation of Jesus we see how we can be tempted by wealth, power, and esteem. Lent calls us to focus on our faith life by living it in deeper ways. As we begin our journey with Catholic Relief Services’ Operation Rice Bowl, let us pray and sacrifice so that through our faith life we can make a difference in the lives of our brothers and sisters in need.

This week, Rice Bowl focuses on the country of Lesotho in southern Africa, where AIDS is a major threat to life. CRS programs in Lesotho focus on orphans, education, training, nutrition, and healthcare. For more information about Lesotho, refer to your Rice Bowl calendar or visit CRS online.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Holy Redeemer in Vancouver and Sacred Heart in LaConner and Winlock.
