OPERATION RICE BOWL

Jesus’ transfiguration was a moment of hope for the disciples, giving them the strength to endure the future sacrifices they would make as they formed the early Church. As we continue our journey with Catholic Relief Services’ Operation Rice Bowl, let us be mindful that our Lenten sacrifices help to give hope to our brothers and sisters in need.

This week, Rice Bowl focuses on Bolivia, where many work in farming and agriculture. Thanks to CRS programs in Bolivia, farmers are forming cooperatives, learning to diversify their crops, increasing their crop yield, and preserving the environment. For more information on Bolivia, refer to your Rice Bowl calendar or visit CRS online.

JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Lent is a particularly special time for the Elect who have been journeying towards baptism, Eucharist and confirmation at the Easter Vigil. As part of their preparation, the Church celebrates anointing with the oil of the Catechumen, blessings and exorcisms. Beginning next weekend, we will join in prayer for our Elect as they participate in the ancient ceremonies called the Scrutinies that symbolize the spiritual purification taking place in their lives as they prepare for baptism. Through these Scrutinies, blessings and anointings, the Elect are encouraged to reflect more deeply on their own spiritual challenges and weaknesses. At the same time, these rites offer powerful prayers of faith, hope and strength on their behalf, calling upon God’s grace and mercy to penetrate their entire lives. Please continue to keep them in your prayers.
