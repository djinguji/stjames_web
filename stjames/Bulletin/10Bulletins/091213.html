Advent III

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church's liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. Our own Cathedral Choir of St. James has a brand-new CD, French Cathedral Music for Choir and Two Organs. This magnificent new recording makes the perfect Christmas gift! Also: Cathedral Christmas cards featuring the art of St. James Cathedral.

ST. GEORGE SCHOOL BAKE SALE. Come support one of our Rainbow Schools, learn more about St. George School and enjoy some great baked goodies. TODAY at coffee hour.

ART IN THE CATHEDRAL CHAPEL. Stop by the Cathedral Chapel to view an exhibit of watercolors of the Holy Land by Cathedral parishioner Patrick White. Many of these original works are for sale, and all proceeds benefit the Vatican University in Bethlehem for scholarships for Christian and Muslim students, particularly from the refugee camps. The exhibit will remain in the Chapel through Sunday, December 20.

GIVING TREE GIFT COLLECTION. Stop by Cathedral Hall after any of the Sunday Masses on December 13 (including the 5:30 Mass) to drop off your Giving Tree gifts. This year we are partnering with Carson House, Elderhealth Northwest, King County Juvenile Detention Center, First Nations Housing and Recovery Project, Operation Nightwatch and Frederic Ozanam House. Thank you for participating and remember all gifts are due by December 13. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

YOUNG ADULTS “WARM HEARTS AND SOLES”. 6th annual Advent sock drive for homeless men continues today, Sunday, December 13 and each Sunday in Advent. Your generosity so far is warming our hearts, but we still have a way to go to reach our goal of 2010 pairs for delivery in January 2010 to warm the soles of those in need. Let’s keep it up by dropping off new socks at the Young Adult table in Cathedral Hall after 8:00am, 10:00am, and 12:00 Sunday Masses, or during the week at 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Such a worthwhile project – let’s keep those hearts and soles warm this winter! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

JESUS: THE INCARNATION OF PRAYER (An Advent Bible Study) Tuesday, December 15 from 1-3 pm or 7-9 pm in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor. “The drama of prayer is fully revealed to us in the Word who became flesh and dwells among us.” Join us as we contemplate Jesus in prayer and what it reveals to us about our own prayer lives. PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30 pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place leave a message with John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

ST. JAMES IN THE NEWS

Father Ryan has had an article published in the December 14 issue of America, the national Catholic weekly magazine. You can find the article online or you can link to it from the “Pastor’s Desk” page. The article deals with the forthcoming controversial new translations of the Roman Missal (the prayers of the Mass).

IMMIGRATION: CREATING CARING COMMUNITIES. Join us in examining the complexity of immigration including economic and legislative issues, faithful citizenship, and the call to act with justice. Reflect and pray, explore scripture & Catholic Social Teaching, examine history and recent trends, review U.S. Bishops’ recommendations, participate in an interactive story sharing, and receive practical tools & resources. Tuesday, January 5, 7-9pm, second floor, Cathedral Place. Presented by the Intercommunity Peace & Justice Center. Sponsored by Social Justice and Outreach, and the ESL Program. Please RSVP to Christopher Koehler at 206-382-4511.
