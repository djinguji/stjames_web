WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

RESPECT LIFE MONTH

“There can be no compromise with the standard
Jesus set and continually calls us to:
the measure of love is to love without measure.”
United States Conference of Catholic Bishops

October is Respect Life Month. Each year, the Church asks us to pray for a greater respect for human life, from conception to natural death, during the month of October. Each Thursday during the month of October, we’ll pray a special Rosary for Life following the 5:30pm evening Mass. All are welcome!

For more information on the Church’s teaching on the “seamless garment” of life issues, visit here.

THE LAST SENIOR TRIP OF 2010. Wednesday, October 13, will take us to the Seattle Art Museum. Led by a docent, we will tour the work of Pablo Picasso, arguably the most radical and influential artist of the 20th century. The exhibition presents iconic works from virtually every phase of Picasso’s legendary career, documenting the full range of his unceasing inventiveness and prodigious creative process. Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place, contact John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minster 206-274-3173.

THE ARCHBISHOP THOMAS J. MURPHY MILLENIUM ORGAN turns ten! St. James Cathedral celebrates the 10th anniversary of the Rosales east apse pipe organ with a year-long series of programs featuring our two Cathedral organists and special guests. The first program on October 15, at 8:00 pm, features Thomas Joyce, newly-appointed Assistant Organist at Grace Cathedral, San Francisco. Dr. Joyce was assistant organist at St. Mark’s Cathedral in Seattle for the past seven years. His program will feature works for cello and organ, organ duet, and the premiere of his Magnificat and Nunc Dimittis with the Women’s Schola of Seattle Pro Musica, a Cathedral Resident Ensemble. Suggested donation is $15, students and seniors pay as able. Information 206-382-4874 or Cathedral music website.

WELCOME, PARENTS WITH YOUNG CHILDREN. We support all of our parishioners who are attending Mass with young children and we welcome those children into our midst. If you find that your child needs a quiet place away from the stimulation of the Mass, please feel free to step into the west vestibule or the cathedral chapel, where we have provided a sound system so that you can continue to participate in the Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. Please note that babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.
