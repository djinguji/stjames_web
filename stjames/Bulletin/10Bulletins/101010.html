THE SPIRIT CATCHES YOU AND YOU FALL DOWN. Join us on Thursday, October 14, at 7:00 pm for a lively discussion of this book by Anne Fadiman. Lia Lee is 3 months old when she has her first epileptic seizure. Her Hmong family believe her soul has been frightened out of her body. THE ARCHBISHOP THOMAS J. MURPHY MILLENIUM ORGAN turns ten! St. James Cathedral celebrates the 10th anniversary of the Rosales east apse pipe organ with a year-long series of programs featuring our two Cathedral organists and special guests. The first program on October 15, at 8:00 pm, features Thomas Joyce, newly-appointed Assistant Organist at Grace Cathedral, San Francisco. Dr. Joyce was assistant organist at St. Mark’s Cathedral in Seattle for the past seven years. His program will feature works for cello and organ, organ duet, and the premiere of his Magnificat and Nunc Dimittis with the Women’s Schola of Seattle Pro Musica, a Cathedral Resident Ensemble. Suggested donation is $15, students and seniors pay as able. Information 206-382-4874 or Cathedral music website.

PRAYER, MARY AND THE SAINTS: FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH SERIES. Next Sunday, October 17, 1:30-5:30pm, St. Peter’s Parish Hall in Beacon Hill. Participants will learn the four types of prayer and learn the difference between public and private prayer and why both are important in the life of a Catholic. We will look at the Psalms and the Lord’s Prayer in particular. In addition, participants will explore what we learn about Mary through each of the Gospels and look at various images of Mary that have emerged through the centuries; summarize the doctrines of the Immaculate Conception and the Assumption; and look at cultural images of Mary. We will explain the Church’s response to apparitions and other private revelations, and learn the origins, uses and meanings of the Rosary. We will explore the historical development of sainthood; the process of Canonization; and the importance of being given a Saint’s name at Baptism. This is a wonderful opportunity for an in-depth look at Catholic teaching and its implications for our everyday lives. Cost: $5 per class. (Cosponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

October is Respect Life Month. Each year, the Church asks us to pray for a greater respect for human life, from conception to natural death, during the month of October. Each Thursday during the month of October, we’ll pray a special Rosary for Life following the 5:30pm evening Mass. All are welcome!

For more information on the Church’s teaching on the “seamless garment” of life issues, visit here.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct debit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. Please drop the completed card in the collection basket or put it in the mail. If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.
