August 22, 2010

Dear Friends,

Let me share some good news with you. Our little campaign to raise funds to re-pad and reupholster the kneelers on the Cathedral chairs was more than successful. As of this writing, thanks to the generosity of more than 230 of you, and thanks to two very large gifts, we exceeded our goal by several thousand dollars! The work will be done sometime in the fall, certainly before Christmas. I’m going to get down on my knees to thank God for the wonderful way you came through on this—and, no, I won’t be waiting till the new kneelers are installed!

Speaking of generosity: last week we took up a spontaneous collection for the relief of the 20 million flood victims in Pakistan. You contributed more than $8,800 as you left the Cathedral. An amazing response, to say the least, and an eloquent testimonial to the depth of your concern for people in need. The money has been forwarded to Catholic Relief Services where it will make a difference for a lot of suffering people. Thank you for always being there when it counts.

In mid-September we will be saying good-bye to Jennifer Ibach who has served with Patty Bowman for the past three years in our social outreach ministries. Jennifer has done a marvelous job in coordinating our Winter Shelter as well as our Volunteer Chore Ministry. She has also done wonders with the Youth Migrant Project, each year getting more young people involved. She has more than made her mark in the area of social advocacy, too. Jennifer has decided to complete her graduate program in theology. I know you will join me in wishing her well in this and in thanking her for her leadership, enthusiasm, and inspiration. We will miss you, Jenn!

Lastly, in July we welcomed Peggy Behnken as our receptionist and office manager. We are delighted to have her. If you have occasion to visit or call the parish office, I know you will be pleased with the welcome you receive and that you will enjoy getting to know her. Welcome to St. James, Peggy!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can find out more information at RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin on Wednesday evening, September 7. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

WELCOME, PARENTS WITH YOUNG CHILDREN. We support all of our parishioners who are attending Mass with young children and we welcome them! If you find that your child is getting restless, please feel free to step into the west vestibule or the cathedral chapel, where we have provided a sound system so that you can continue to participate in the Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship.

Please note that babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct debit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. Please drop the completed card in the collection basket or put it in the mail. If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.
