OPERATION RICE BOWL

Today we hear the call of Moses to lead his people from oppression to freedom. God promises to be with them as they journey to a new life. Our participation in Rice Bowl unites us in prayer, fasting, and giving with the millions of people throughout the world who are oppressed by poverty, injustice, war, disease, and lack of education and other opportunities.

This week, Rice Bowl focuses on Afghanistan, where safety concerns, distance, and resources can all be barriers to quality education. To help address these issues, CRS aids in the operation of community-based schools for boys, girls, and young women. Villagers work together to support the schools, teachers, students, and one another through these community programs. For more information on Afghanistan, refer to your Rice Bowl calendar or visit CRS online.

JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

This weekend we celebrate the first of the three Scrutinies with the retelling of the story of the Samaritan woman at the well. This Gospel challenges our Elect to be honest about their personal lives just as the woman was with her encounter with Christ. In acknowledging their sinfulness and need for healing, they come to know more fully the mercy and loving acceptance of Christ in their lives. To prepare for their Scrutiny, each is asked to reflect upon what he/she most thirst for in life, and then to bring that to this ancient rite for God’s grace. Continue to enter into these solemn rites in prayer with our Elect as they prepare for baptism.

March 7, 2010

Dear Friends,

A lot has happened in the month since my last letter. For one thing, we had three amazing nights of Great Music for Great Cathedrals. Even more amazing is that 367 parishioners pitched in to make it happen! To each of them, my congratulations and sincere thanks for bringing an incredible experience of beauty to our city and to our parish. Great Music we will not see again, but beautiful music we shall surely hear and beautiful things we shall most definitely see. So stay tuned!

Record numbers of people filled the Cathedral for the Masses of Ash Wednesday. The good weather no doubt helped, but I think there was more: a realization that Lent is the perfect time for soul-searching and for reflecting on Christ’s suffering, death, and resurrection and how they can come alive for each of us and give meaning to everything we do. May our Lenten journey bring us closer to Christ and to each other.

Fourteen of our parishioners participated in the Rite of Election on the day following Ash Wednesday. They are no longer catechumens now, but “Elect.” They have been chosen for Baptism and the sacraments of Confirmation and Eucharist, and these days of Lent will be especially important for them as they prepare for those powerfully transforming sacramental celebrations at the Easter Vigil. Please pray for them, and, if possible, please consider becoming a “Prayer Partner” with one or another of them during the remainder of Lent. You can do this by visiting the special place of prayer in the Cathedral’s north aisle or you can even do it by going online to the Cathedral’s website. We make it easy!

Catholics Come Home, the television advertising campaign that is currently being run throughout Western Washington, is, I’m sure, getting some people to think about their faith and their relationship to the Church. I hope you’ll keep these people in your prayers and, should you meet one at Mass or at the coffee hour, extend a word of welcome. You might also enjoy visiting the Cathedral’s website and viewing a very engaging brief video of some of our parishioners who tell of their return to the Church at St. James. It’s a quick ‘watch’ and a worthwhile one.

I usually try to get away for a retreat during Lent and this year is no exception. I’m making a return visit to Downside Abbey in the west of England to spend some days with the Benedictine monks there. Please keep me in your prayers!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
