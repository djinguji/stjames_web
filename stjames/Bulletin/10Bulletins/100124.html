WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church's liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

Dear Friends,

Your response to the collection last weekend for earthquake relief in Haiti was nothing short of magnificent. Over $30,000 was donated in that spontaneous collection following the Masses! You hardly need me to tell you – but I’ll do it anyway – that this is an absolutely typical and true-to-form response from this parish. And it will make a real difference in the lives of the Haitian people who are suffering so mightily.

If you were not able to be here last Sunday and would like to contribute to this effort, you may do so by using one of the special red envelopes in the pews and chairs. If you write a check, please make it payable to Catholic Relief Services, or if you make it payable to the Cathedral, write “Haiti relief” on the memo line. Thank you.

CELEBRATING CATHOLIC SCHOOLS—THE RAINBOW SCHOOLS COALITION. On Sunday, January 31 we will celebrate Catholic School education, and in a particular way the Cathedral children who attend one of the five Rainbow Schools. Our parish is a contributing member of the Rainbow Schools Program whereby children from our parish can attend at “parishioner” tuition rates. Approximately 60 cathedral children are enrolled in one of these fine schools. Representatives of Sts. Edward, George, Paul, Joseph and Therese Catholics Schools will be available after the 10:00 and 12:00 Masses during hospitality. Children are encouraged to come to Mass in their uniforms. In addition, our parish has established the Cathedral Scholarship Fund to assist families in meeting the financial obligation involved in sending their elementary school aged children to any on of the Rainbow Schools. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.
