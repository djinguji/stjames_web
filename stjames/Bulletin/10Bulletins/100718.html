FLOWERS IN THE CATHEDRAL. According to a pious legend, lavender received its sweet scent when Mary washed the Christ Child's clothes and hung them to dry on a lavender bush. When the sun and wind had dried the clothes, they smelled sweet and clean. If you have been inspired to give thanks with flowers, please contact Peggy Behnken at 206-654-4650 for more information.

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. Beautiful statues of St. Francis for your garden or backyard—in stock for a limited time only! We also have plenty of great reading for the summertime, and a variety of books related to our patron, James, and the camino to his shrine at Santiago de Compostela.

CHORAL MUSIC NEXT SUNDAY. At both the 10:00 and the 12:00 Noon masses on July 25th, the Cathedral Soloists and the full Cathedral Choir of St. James will celebrate the Jubilee feast of St. James with choral music in honor of our patron.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session began Wednesday, July 14 at 7:00 pm. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514, or go visit our webpage.

SUMMER CHOIR AND YOU! You are needed in this year’s St. James Summer Choir. Any one—every one—is welcome to join in this Cathedral tradition. You do not have to be a gifted singer, just someone who loves to join with others to support the congregation and to sing praises at one or all Sunday noon masses in August. Rehearsals are the Sundays of August at 11:15am in Cathedral Place, Choir Room. Information Cathedral Music website, 206-382-4874.

DUWAMISH RIVER ECOJUSTICE BOAT TOUR. In 2001, the Environmental Protection Agency listed the Lower Duwamish River as a federal Superfund site, naming it as one of the most contaminated sites in the United States. The St. James Environmental Justice Group, together with the Duwamish River Cleanup Coalition and several other downtown Seattle faith communities, is sponsoring a prayerful and reflective boat tour of the Lower Duwamish on Saturday, August 14 from noon to 2pm. The boat tour will provide an opportunity to learn about the state of Seattle’s major river and our call to practice environmental justice. $15 per person and reservations are required. Information, Rachael Ross at 206-903-4629.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL—UNDER THE STARS! Join us on Tuesday, August 10 at 7 PM in Archbishop Murphy courtyard for the showing of “Up”. This film (which won an Academy Award for best animated picture) tells the story of a grumpy widower who decides to relocate his home to South America with the help of a thousand balloons. This touching tale offers lessons on love, loss, marriage and perseverance. Rated PG. Bring your lawn chairs, blankets and your favorite movie treats for an evening under the stars. (weather permitting—otherwise in Cathedral Place). Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.
