WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. During the month of October, all proceeds will benefit the Children’s Religious Education Program. If you are interested in becoming a Cathedral barista, please call Sean and Katherine Berry at 206-571-6868. Training is provided!

PRAYER, MARY AND THE SAINTS: FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH SERIES TODAY. Sunday, October 17, 1:30-5:30pm, St. Peter’s Parish Hall in Beacon Hill. Participants will learn the four types of prayer and learn the difference between public and private prayer and why both are important in the life of a Catholic. In addition, participants will explore what we learn about Mary through each of the Gospels and look at various images of Mary that have emerged through the centuries. We will explore the historical development of sainthood. This is a wonderful opportunity for an in-depth look at Catholic teaching and its implications for our everyday lives. Cost: $5 per class. (Cosponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

YOUNG ADULTS (21 - 30-SOMETHING) 3RD SUNDAY. Scripture sharing and informal social is today, Sunday, October 17 starting at 4:00pm. Do you ever wonder how the Sunday readings relate to our everyday lives? Enjoy fellowship, wine, and cheese from 4:00 to 4:30 followed by sharing how the Scripture readings for the day speak to you in your everyday life. The study will finish in time to attend 5:30 Mass. Young adults over the age of 21, single or married, gather in the Fireside Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Newcomers and those not new to the parish are all welcome! Information, Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

FALL BLOOD DRIVE THANK YOU. Thirty-seven donors registered at the Fall Blood Drive, and contributed the most units of blood ever donated for a blood drive at St. James. These donors will provide a total potential benefit of up to 99 different patients. This is a powerful support for our community. The help provided to those patients will echo through the relief of families, friends, and care providers. The Puget Sound Blood Center and the St. James Health and Healing Ministry extend a grateful thank you to all who participated.

RESPECT LIFE MONTH

“There can be no compromise with the standard
Jesus set and continually calls us to:
the measure of love is to love without measure.”
United States Conference of Catholic Bishops

October is Respect Life Month. Each year, the Church asks us to pray for a greater respect for human life, from conception to natural death, during the month of October. Each Thursday during the month of October, we’ll pray a special Rosary for Life following the 5:30pm evening Mass. All are welcome!

PREGNANCY SUPPORT MINISTRY. St. James works in cooperation with Catholic Community Services to provide assistance to pregnant women in need. Parish-based volunteers provide practical assistance such as transportation, babysitting, and cooking meals. We are seeking new volunteers for this vital ministry in the service of life. An information session will be held Wednesday, October 27 at 1:00pm in the Fireside Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

WINTER SHELTER VOLUNTEERS NEEDED. With difficult economic times in our nation, we are seeing even more people facing the crisis of homelessness. This past weekend, St. James began its 19th season of hosting our winter shelter. We continue to need volunteers who can commit to serving as overnight hosts once a month from October through April. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

WINTER SHELTER DONATIONS. Our shelter guests need new socks and underwear, as well as disposable razors, shaving cream, toothbrushes, mouth wash, deodorant, and other travel size toiletries. Please bring your donations to the Cathedral Hall after Sunday Mass or drop them off at the parish office M-F 9am-5pm.

VAN DRIVERS NEEDED. Van drivers who assist elderly parishioners attend Mass are urgently needed. The commitment is for one Sunday per month. Be a part of this rewarding ministry! Information John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin November 3 on Wednesday evenings. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. Also check out our webpage.

PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30 pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

FLORAL ARRANGEMENTS AT THE ALTAR. Floral offerings are a venerable custom in the Church, and a wonderful way to remember a deceased loved one, celebrate an anniversary, or give thanks for an answered prayer. Information Peggy Behnken, 206-654-4650.

NEED PASTORAL CARE? The Cathedral provides a variety of such ministries. Whether you are sick or homebound and in need of communion, or if you need the sacrament of the Anointing of the Sick, we can help! Information John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Luke in Shoreline and St. Jude in Redmond.
