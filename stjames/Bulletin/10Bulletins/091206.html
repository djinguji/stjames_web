YOUNG LIVES ON HOLD: THE COLLEGE DREAMS OF UNDOCUMENTED STUDENTS. Each year, 65,000 undocumented immigrant youth legally graduate from the U.S. public school system. They graduate into a world where all steps towards furthering their future are blocked. Ineligible for legal employment and financial aid to put toward a higher education, they live under the threat of deportation to a foreign country they’ve never known. Dr. Roberto Gonzales, a University of Washington professor, offers a resolution in his report on this unique population, including the bipartisan DREAM Act and its benefit to the U.S. economy. Read the full report online. Information, Christopher Koehler at 206-382-4511.

FUNERAL PLANNING. Many people have questions regarding funerals—questions ranging from the Church’s position regarding cremation and burial to how and when funerals are scheduled and celebrated in the Cathedral. Since the time of the death of a loved one is likely to be very emotionally draining, it is clearly not the best time for dealing with such questions. In an effort to help parishioners be prepared ahead of time, the Cathedral offers a simple guide, available at our parish website. This information is also in a booklet that may also be obtained by calling the parish office.

  • 20 – 20 pound turkeys – a gift from Mrs. H.

  • 40 pounds of fresh carrots and 20 gallons of milk purchased with cash gifts
  • Gallons of creamy gravy prepared by Janet, our Tuesday Head Cook
  • Day-old bread, onions, apples, and celery gleaned by Bob, Peter, Mike, Amy and Alex, from PCC and Madison Markets
  • 55 pumpkin pies assembled and baked on Wednesday by Carol, our Monday Head Cook, and 6 volunteers
  • 8 more freshly baked pies from other thoughtful volunteers
  • Plus 50 volunteers who worked steadily 3 shifts from 8:30am to 6:30pm to prep, serve and cleanup

Thank you to all who participated in making this a beautiful meal!

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s highly acclaimed journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct deposit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. If you're not sure whether you're registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.
