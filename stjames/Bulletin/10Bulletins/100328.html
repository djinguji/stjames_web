Bulletin Archive

Palm Sunday

WELCOME—AND WELCOME BACK! If you are a visitor or a newcomer, we want you to know how welcome you are at St. James Cathedral. You’re invited to visit a welcome table at the coffee hours in Cathedral Hall. You’ll have a chance to ask questions and find out more about St. James Cathedral. If you’re a Catholic who’s been away from the Church, there’s an opportunity to talk to a member of our “Welcome Back” team in the Cathedral Chapel.

PALM SUNDAY DECOR. A special thank you to David Helgeson, senior gardener at the Volunteer Park Conservatory, for the gift of the large, decorative date palm branches. If you would like to help beautify the Cathedral this Easter, please use one of the goldenrod envelopes in the pews.

ASSISTIVE LISTENING SYSTEM. If you have trouble hearing or understanding the spoken word in the Cathedral, please ask an usher about trying out an assistive listening device. The listening devices are especially designed for those who have difficulty hearing in the resonant acoustic of the Cathedral.

BAPTISM AND FIRST COMMUNION FOR OLDER CHILDREN. If your child would like to be baptized, or was baptized into another Christian Church and would like to be received into the Catholic Church, or if your child was baptized Catholic but not catechized, call for details about the RCIA process for children. New inquiry classes will start April 11. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? Our Welcome Back program provides a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are offered on Mondays, April 12 through July12, 7:00-9:00pm on 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

ENGAGING OUR CONFLICTS: AN EXPLORATION OF NONVIOLENT PEACEMAKING. This eight-week series explores the power and potential of nonviolent peacemaking in our lives and in the world. Participants will learn about nonviolence and how to apply it in real issues. Topics to be explored include: the dynamics of nonviolent peacemaking, interpersonal nonviolence, and grappling with structural, institutional and cultural violence. Monday evenings, April 12 through June 7. Cost per student $25 for materials. Registration required. Information, or to register, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

VAN DRIVER NEEDED. Want to make someone’s day? Help our senior parishioners attend Mass by driving one of our vans one Sunday per month. Information John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

WALKING PROGRAM. The Health and Healing Ministry begins the second year of its popular walking program this April. We will begin where we ended last October. Pick up forms to record your steps this Sunday, March 28, in Cathedral Hall. Information, John Simpson, 206-274-3173.

CATHEDRAL SCHOLARSHIP FUND FOR RAINBOW SCHOOLS STUDENTS. Our parish has established a scholarship fund to assist families in meeting the financial obligation involved in sending their elementary school age children to any one of the Rainbow Schools (Sts. George, Edward, Paul, Theresa, and Joseph). Applications are now available for the 2010-2011 school year. Applications are due May 1. Families are required to be registered in our parish, have a current Sacrificial Giving card on file (whether they are able to make a monetary pledge or not), and are active in the parish in some capacity. Information, Theresa Van de Ven, 206-382-5822.

CONSIDER THE RAINBOW SCHOOLS. Are you a parent trying to find a Catholic school for your child? If you have the sincere desire for a Catholic education for your child, it is available! Father Felino Paulino, pastor, invites you to make an appointment to visit St. Edward, St. George, and/or St. Paul Schools. See what is going on in the hallways and classrooms. Perhaps one of these fine schools would be a good choice for your child. Information, St. Edward, 206-725-1774; St George, 206-762-0656; St. Paul, 206-725-0780.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, April 21. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Our Elect are now entering their final week of preparation for the Easter Vigil, where they will be baptized, confirmed and receive Eucharist for the first time as one with us in the faith. They will participate with us in our Holy Week liturgies, being dismissed for the last time on Holy Thursday evening. They will also participate in a daylong retreat on Holy Saturday in their final preparation rites prior to the celebration of the Vigil of all vigils! You are encouraged to join us at the Easter Vigil to celebrate this most important day of their lives where we, the baptized, will also renew our baptismal promises as we celebrate the resurrection of our Lord.

PRAYER CARDS FOR THE ELECT. Thank you for keeping our Elect in your prayers during this season of Lent. Now is the time to turn in your personalized cards or notes for our Elect that will be distributed to them on Holy Saturday. You can drop off your cards in the baskets located at the place of prayer in the Cathedral, in the Hall during hospitality on Sunday mornings, or in the Bookstore during its regular hours. They need to be turned in no later than Holy Thursday evening. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

OPERATION RICE BOWL

This week we walk with Jesus through his passion, death, and resurrection. We begin to experience the sacrifice he made for us and how we are also called to give our life for others.
