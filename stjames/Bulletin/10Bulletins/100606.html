WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are from Matilda and Jose Banez in thanksgiving on their 55th wedding anniversary. Flowers are also from the Saturday wedding of Ciara Hugie and Conor Cook.

PRAYER FOR THE EARTH WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. On Friday, June 11 at 6:30 pm in the Cathedral, you are invited to join in an ecumenical prayer in response to the disastrous oil spill in the Gulf. With readings, song, and silence, we will pray for those working on the scene, and for a greater respect for all of creation.

SEE THE REAL FACE OF IMMIGRATION. Join us on Tuesday, June 8 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor, for a showing of Under the Same Moon followed by discussion on immigration through the lens of Catholic Social Teaching, which tells us that human life is sacred and the dignity of the human person is the foundation of a moral vision for society, and that we are one human family whatever our national, racial, ethnic, economic, and ideological differences. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP will be Wednesday, June 16. First we will visit the Botanical Gardens in Bellevue: a lovely display of cultivated gardens and serene woodlands. Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners. Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

ST. JAMES CATHEDRAL YOUTH MUSIC PROGRAM CHOIR CAMP 2010. August 9-13, and Sunday morning, August 15, open to young singers grades 4-8, in the Archdiocese of Seattle. This week-long camp, led by some of the region’s finest Catholic music educators, offers excellent training in liturgical music. Students learn musical skills, note reading, singing techniques and participate in daily Morning Prayer in the Cathedral. For information and auditions call the Cathedral Music Office at 206-382-4874.

TOURS OF THE CATHEDRAL. During the summer months, June through August, we offer tours of the Cathedral every Wednesday at 1:00pm. Gather in the west vestibule; no reservations required. This is a great opportunity to show your Cathedral to out of town guests!

WELCOME, PARENTS WITH YOUNG CHILDREN. We support all of our parishioners who are attending Mass with young children and we welcome those children into our midst. If you find that your child needs a quiet place away from the stimulation of the Mass, please feel free to step into the west vestibule or the cathedral chapel, where we have provided a sound system so that you can continue to participate in the Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. Please note that babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). Registration cards can be found at the Welcome Table at coffee hours. Please drop the completed card in the collection basket or put it in the mail. If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.

Volunteer Opportunities at St. James

ALL PARISH CHOIR. Join the Cathedral Choir and be part of the All Parish Choir on Sunday June 13, at the 10:00am Mass. All ages and voices are welcome. Gather in the Chapel at 9:00am. Rehearse in the East Apse at 9:05am. Don’t miss this yearly opportunity to sing parish favorites with the musicians of the Cathedral. Information, 206-382-4874.

HELP A SENIOR IN NEED. Over 150 people in our greater community are on a waiting list for help with transportation, shopping, laundry, housework, and more. Age, illness, economics, and disabilities can all be barriers to accomplishing these daily tasks. By volunteering just a few hours a month with Catholic Community Services’ Volunteer Chore Services program, you can make a difference in someone’s life. Interested in joining this rewarding ministry? Come to an orientation on the 2nd floor of the Pastoral Outreach Center (907 Columbia) on Wednesday, June 16 at 6:30pm. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

HELP CREATE A COMMUNITY OF COMPASSION. To register, contact Stephanie Pietras at 206-324-1520 ext. 226 or stephanie@multifaith.org. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.
