September 26, 2010

Dear Friends,

I’m happy to be able to report to you that the 14th annual Archbishop Hunthausen Golf Tournament at Inglewood Country Club was a great success. More than 110 golfers participated this year and had a terrific time. The weather could have cooperated a bit more but this is Seattle, after all! The dinner after the tournament—which has become a wonderful social event for a lot of parishioners— drew more than 180 people. Heartfelt thanks to Maria Laughlin and Jeff Meder who worked so hard to make it a great event. The funds raised that day will help support the Cathedral Kitchen which, as you must know, is in greater demand than ever during these days of slow economic recovery.

It is a pleasure to be able to welcome Suzanne Lee to our Cathedral staff. Suzanne joined us late in August and is working closely with Patty Bowman in our social outreach ministries. She and her husband Brian Lynch have been parishioners here for several years and, while relatively new to ministry here at the Cathedral, she is anything but new to church ministry. For four years Suzanne served as a pastoral minister at Our Lady of Fatima parish in Magnolia and, before that, she served on the staff of Catholic Community Services as Director of Advocacy. When you have had an opportunity to meet her and work with her you will know why I am delighted that she has joined our ministerial team. Welcome, Suzanne!

The other day, Patty Bowman and I received a very nice letter from some 15 people of the SHARE Safe Haven Shelter who have been using the Cathedral Hall for overnight shelter all summer long. Since you are really the ones who have offered hospitality to them, I thought I would quote their letter in part: “The first and most important thing that we, the residents of the Safe Haven Shelter can say is Thank You. We greatly appreciate the time that we stayed in the Cathedral Hall. If not for your kindness, we would have had to sleep outside. Not only did we feel safe and secure, but you made us feel welcome in a very large way with just your acceptance of us in the first place. From leaving us doughnuts, to learning our names, the people at St. James made us feel welcome. We are grateful.”

Lastly, in case you notice that I’m not here the next couple of Sundays it’s only because it’s time for some vacation. I’ll be traveling in France with my sisters and brother-in-law, celebrating my sister Judy’s 50th jubilee as a Sister of the Holy Names. Should be fun. Au revoir!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

THE ARCHBISHOP THOMAS J. MURPHY MILLENIUM ORGAN turns ten! St. James Cathedral celebrates the 10th anniversary of the Rosales east apse pipe organ with a year-long series of programs featuring our two Cathedral organists and special guests. The first program on October 15, at 8:00 pm, features Thomas Joyce, newly-appointed Assistant Organist at Grace Cathedral, San Francisco. Dr. Joyce was assistant organist at St. Mark’s Cathedral in Seattle for the past seven years. His program will feature works for cello and organ, organ duet, and the premiere of his Magnificat and Nunc Dimittis with the Women’s Schola of Seattle Pro Musica, a Cathedral Resident Ensemble. Suggested donation is $15, students and seniors pay as able. Information 206-382-4874 or Cathedral music website.

RESPECT LIFE MONTH

“There can be no compromise with the standard
Jesus set and continually calls us to:
the measure of love is to love without measure.”
United States Conference of Catholic Bishops

October is Respect Life Month. Each year, the Church asks us to pray for a greater respect for human life, from conception to natural death, during the month of October. We begin our observance on Friday, October 1, with Exposition of the Blessed Sacrament following the 8:15am Mass and a Holy Hour for Life at 12:00 Noon. Each Thursday during the month of October, we’ll pray a special Rosary for Life following the 5:30pm evening Mass. All are welcome!

For more information on the Church’s teaching on the “seamless garment” of life issues, visit here.
