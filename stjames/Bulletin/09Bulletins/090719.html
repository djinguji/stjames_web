From the Pastor’s Desk

July 19, 2009

Dear Friends,

For some of you, this will be the first time you will have heard of the death of Deacon Joe Curtis. Joe died very suddenly and unexpectedly in the wee hours of last Tuesday morning. I had visited him late Sunday afternoon over at Exeter House and would never have guessed that his death was so near. He was looking forward to returning to his home at Faerland Terrace and, even more importantly, to his community here at St. James.

For personal reasons, Joe’s family was very eager to have his funeral as soon as possible. It was celebrated this past Thursday afternoon. Many of you learned about it in time to attend but many others of you, unfortunately, did not. For that reason, I intend to offer the 10:00 Mass next Sunday, the feast of St. James, for the happy repose of his soul.

Joe Curtis joined our staff as minister with the homeless back in the late 1980’s – just a year or so after I arrived at St. James. His name quickly became synonymous with the Cathedral’s outreach to poor and homeless people in downtown Seattle. More importantly, his presence became a gift to countless people who call the streets of our city home. Joe was a founder of the Matt Talbot Center, he led Bible study sessions at the Downtown Emergency Service Center, for years he coordinated the parish’s involvement in Operation Nightwatch, and he ran the clothing room at the Lazarus Day Center. Very fittingly, in 2003 he was honored by Operation Nightwatch as Hero of the Homeless.

And, of course, Joe served as Deacon and preached here at St. James. All those years he did what deacons are ordained to do: he helped awaken us to what it means to find the face of Christ in the least of our sisters and brothers. In so many ways, he was “in our midst as one who serves.”

We will miss Joe but we will see him again when the mercy of God gathers us together gain in the joy of God’s kingdom! Please pray for him as well as for his wife, Martha, and his daughters Ann and Mary and their families.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates. If you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct debit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.
