Easter Sunday

There was no bulletin published for Easter Sunday. The following letter was published in the Order of Celebration.

Easter Sunday, 2009

Dear Friends,

It is a joy to be able to welcome you to St. James Cathedral on this Easter of 2009. I do so in the name of our Archbishop, Alexander J. Brunett, as well as in my own.

Of all the times we gather as Christian believers, none is more important than this one. Easter is the greatest of all of our feasts. It celebrates the Mystery that is the very foundation of our Christian faith, as St. Paul wrote, "If Christ has not risen, our faith is in vain." (I Cor. 15:14)

But Christ has risen and your presence here in the Cathedral both affirms and celebrates that most startling and enduring of all realities. While we do not see him, we know he lives and that he is in our midst breathing life and hope into us, walking alongside us on our pilgrimage of faith, our journey toward the Kingdom.

I have no way of knowing what is in your heart as you come here on this Easter Sunday of 2009. For some, I am sure, there is joy and a keen sense of hope for the future as Spring with all its promise is bursting forth all around us; for others there may be the pain that comes from the death of a loved one. For all of us there is the stress and uncertainty brought about by the current economic meltdown out nation and our world are experiencing, and there is also the painful paradox of living in a world that, while incredibly beautiful, is also hopelessly violent. Death and life are never very far apart in our lives or in our world, are they? But death is not the whole story. Easter proclaims for all to hear the one thing that really matters, the one thing we need to know above all else: that death does not have the last word. Life does!

My prayer today is that you will be renewed in faith and hope as you join in worship here at St. James Cathedral.

If you are a visitor, please know that you are warmly welcomed here. I hope you will find something today that will touch your heart, awaken your spirit, and make you want to return to this holy place again and again. If you are part of the regular family of St. James, I take this opportunity to thank you for all the ways you make the Risen Lord Jesus present in this parish and in this city: through your prayer, your faith, and your love that overflows in such generous and unselfish service, especially to the poor.

May the blessings of the Risen Lord Jesus be your in abundance.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Pastor of St. James Cathedral
