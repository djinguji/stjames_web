Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • The Third Sunday of Advent is also called “Gaudete” Sunday, from the words of St. Paul which we sing at the beginning of Mass today: “Rejoice! Again I say, rejoice! The Lord is near” (Philippians 4: 4). How does Paul’s urging to rejoice resonate with you today? What gives you cause for rejoicing in these Advent days?
  • In today’s second reading, St. Paul gives the Thessalonians some simple rules for living in expectation of Christ’s coming: “Do not quench the Spirit… Test everything; retain what is good.” Where do you hear the Spirit speaking? Are there ways in which, whether as individuals or as a society, we seem to “quench” the Spirit’s flame?
