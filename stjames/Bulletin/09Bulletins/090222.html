Bulletin - February 22, 2009

FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

February 22, 2009

Dear Friends,

The Cathedral choirs are getting a break today. I think you’ll agree that they’ve more than earned one after doing Great Music for Great Cathedrals three nights in a row—not to mention all the rehearsal time beforehand. So the pressure is on us this weekend. We get to be the choir. I have no doubt that we will more than measure up.

Speaking of measuring up: you have done that and more in the recent “Snow Angel” campaign to make up for the shortfall we experienced during the “Winter of Our Discontent.” You may remember that the collection shortfall from the Advent and Christmas snow and ice storms was $25,000. We have more than made up for that, thanks to your incredible response to my invitation to become “snow angels.” As of this writing, 489 of you have sprouted angel wings, contributing $41,018! That’s nothing short of remarkable. The funds donated over and above the goal will be used to support the Cathedral Kitchen.

Even though I would like to have thanked each of you individually for your gift, I’m trusting you will agree with me that saving postage was even a higher value. Please know, however, how deeply grateful I am. Your generosity never ceases to amaze and inspire me.

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

THE NEW AMERICAN. Share the journey of a new American, from the turmoil of the old country to the promise of America. This live performance combined with film and audio, traces the journey of an Irish immigrant. It includes the steamship passage of 1910, the path through Ellis Island, and working in the sweatshops of Manhattan. Stay for the post-performance discussion on Catholic Social Teaching and current immigration issues. Friday, March 13 at 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Presented by Living Voices, St. James Cathedral Outreach Ministries and the ESL Program. Information, Christopher Koehler, 206-382-4511.

ST. JAMES ESL BOOK CLUB. Join us on Tuesday, March 31 in the Pastoral Outreach Center to discuss Jasmine by Bharati Mukherjee. Acclaimed by the Los Angeles Times Book Review as “artful and arresting” and “breathtaking,” the book tells the riveting story of a young woman’s journey and transformation from rural India to Iowa. Jasmine is a quick read, and a thought-provoking story of what it means to be American. Now available in the Cathedral Bookstore. Information, Christopher Koehler, 206-382-4511.

  • St. Paul often uses unexpected comparisons to help us understand the mystery of Jesus Christ. Today’s reading is a case in point. Jesus, he says, is God’s “yes.” What does this simple word “yes” say to you today?
  • St. Paul also uses an unusual comparison drawn from the language of finance. God will be faithful to his promise to bring us to eternal life. How do we know? Because he has “put his seal on us” and given us the Holy Spirit “as a first installment”! What do you imagine the “second installment” will be?
