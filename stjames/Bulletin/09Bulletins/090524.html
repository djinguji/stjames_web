CATHOLIC 101: THE TEN COMMANDMENTS (As you have never thought of them before!) Join us on Tuesday, May 26 (VII); 1:00-3:00 or 7:00-9:00, Cathedral Place—second floor for an ongoing look at the newly published US Catechism for Adults. Helen Oesterle reviews the foundations of our Catholic faith from our American Catholic experience and facilitates lively conversations among the participants. (The series will continue in the fall with the eighth, ninth and tenth commandments.) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

FR. BENIGNO BELTRAN: STORIES OF SMOKEY MOUNTAIN AND POVERTY IN THE PHILIPPINES. Fr. Ben Beltran has worked for 30 years with the poor in Manila, who seek to eke out a living by scavenging on the large garbage dump called Smokey Mountain. Of his work, Fr. Beltran says: “I have learned that God is speaking through the poor. You should not come to the poor to save them, but to save yourself.” Fr. Beltran will share stories of his ministry Saturday, June 6, at 1:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Registration required! Information, or to register, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

SEATTLE SOUNDERS vs. HOUSTON DYNAMO. Tickets are going fast for the Sounders game at Qwest Field on Saturday, July 11 at 1:00pm. $20/person. Reserve your tickets now! (The game is sold out so this is the only way to see our home team!) Information, Theresa Van de Ven, 206-219-5822.

THE GLOBAL WATER CRISIS: A CHRISTIAN RESPONSE. Seattle University Professor of Christian Ethics Gary Chamberlain, author of Troubled Waters: Religion, Ethics and the Global Water Crisis, will speak on the intersection between our faith and the increasing problem of lack of access to water in many areas of the world. Monday, June 8, 2009, 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Registration required. Information, or to register, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

Volunteer Opportunities

SANDWICH VOLUNTEERS NEEDED. St. Martin de Porres Shelter has seen an increased need for meals and St. James is responding by adding a second night of service to our current Sandwich Ministry. We need a whole new crew of volunteers who can help us make 300+ sandwiches for the 4th Monday of each month. Information, Tara at sandwich.volunteers@gmail.com.

HELP A SENIOR IN NEED. Many in our community are in need of help with transportation, shopping, laundry, housework, and more. Age, illness, economics, and disabilities can all be barriers to accomplishing these routine, daily tasks. By volunteering just a few hours a month, you can make a huge difference in someone’s life, provide them peace of mind, and help them to stay in their own home rather than going to an institutionalized setting. If you are interested in joining this rewarding ministry, please come to the orientation on the 2nd floor of the Pastoral Outreach Center on Wednesday, June 10 at 6:30pm. Information Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

AMERICORPS OPENINGS WITH THE ST. JAMES ESL PROGRAM! As an AmeriCorps member you will develop job skills in volunteer management and providing services to diverse populations. Specifically, you will provide English instruction to two groups of adult, low-income refugees and/or immigrants in community locations in King County. You will work with program staff to organize groups, plan instruction, conduct intake and on-going support of students, provide English instruction, and assess student learning. You will also assist in the recruitment, intake and support of volunteer tutors. Information, Christopher Koehler, 206-382-4511. To apply, you must first fill out and submit an AmeriCorps application. The application and further information about AmeriCorps is available at www.kcls.org/literacyamericorps.
