FREDERIC OZANAM HOUSE is now open! Located directly across the street from St. James Cathedral, Frederic Ozanam House is a new 56-unit facility, managed by the Archdiocesan Housing Authority, providing permanent supported housing for formerly homeless men. TODAY, Sunday, July 12, following the 10:00 Mass, you are invited to an open house and tour of this new home. Please join us in welcoming our new neighbors! In the coming weeks, we will also be collecting donations for the residents of Ozanam House; watch the bulletin for additional details. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session began Wednesday, July 8. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

10th ANNUAL SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL. Join us on Tuesday, July 14 at 7:00pm in Cathedral Place, second floor, for the second film of the summer, The Secret Life of Bees. Haunted by the death of her mother, 14 year-old Lily escapes the dreary life she lives on her father’s farm and heads to Tiburon, SC, with her friend and caregiver Rosaleen, setting off on a life-changing journey of self-discovery. Set amidst the hotbed of racial tensions in the South in the 1960s, this dramatic tale is based on the bestselling novel by Sue Monk Kidd. Rated PG-13. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP. Wednesday, July 15, will take us to the spectacular Mt. St. Helens. We will see the devastated Toutle River valley, Spirit Lake, and the crater from the blast. From Johnston Ridge—named after David Johnston who died observing the mountain—we’ll have a full view of America’s only active volcano—now re-building itself after an 18-year period of quiet. Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

SOLANUS CASEY CENTER, our parish’s drop-in center for homeless and low-income people, is looking for volunteers to help in two areas:

  • Several volunteers are needed to help in the areas of hospitality, resources referral, and ministry of listening and presence. Must be available weekday afternoons.
  • We also seek one volunteer with an accounting background to assist with accounts payable, budget, and statistical reports.

The Solanus Casey Center serves approximately 300 people per month. Your help is essential to continuing and expanding the work of this important outreach ministry. Information, Joan Clough, 206-795-2951.

TOILETRIES DONATIONS NEEDED FOR WOMEN’S SUMMER SHELTER. St. James is starting a Summer Shelter for women this July and August. As part of their stay at our shelter, the women are given toiletries to freshen up before they head out (many go to work in the mornings). We are able to provide these toiletries thanks to your generous donations. Please consider dropping off any of the following unused items in the blue bin at coffee hour: disposable razors, deodorant, feminine hygiene products, cosmetics and cosmetic supplies, toothbrushes, and travel-sized toothpaste, shampoo, conditioner, soap, and mouthwash. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.
