From the Pastor’s Desk

August 2, 2009

Dear Friends,

It’s hard to imagine how last weekend’s celebration of the feast of St. James could have been any better. A few degrees drop in temperature might have been nice but it doesn’t seem right for Seattleites to complain about sunshine!

The picnic was fabulous and I want to thank the hard-working team who made it happen. Special thanks go to Trent Mummery, Stephen Pace, and Larry Brouse, along with a wonderful group of volunteers too numerous to mention who did the set-ups, the cooking, the serving, the staffing, and the clean-up. There were many highlights but for me but watching the faces of dozens of eager and excited kids assaulting the piňata scores high on my list. It was a great event in every way. The best picnic ever!

We apologize for the fact that the Seattle policemen who normally do the traffic control and directing outside the Cabrini Garage are not able to be with us this weekend. The annual Seafair celebrations demand their presence elsewhere. They will be back with us next weekend.

Summer is supposed to be a ‘down’ time but not around here. For us it’s a good time to catch up on some major maintenance items. If you saw heavy equipment in the Cathedral last week, it’s because we re-lamped every one of the light fixtures in the Cathedral. As you might expect, this is an expensive item (about $10,000!). Happily it only has to be done every-other-year. We are also spending $20,000 for a new furnace in Cathedral Place and several thousand dollars to replace many of the office and classroom windows in that building with thermo-pane glass. In the long run, this work will involve considerable cost-savings because of reduced energy consumption. Needless to say, I am more than grateful for your generosity that makes it possible for us to maintain our wonderful buildings.

I’m away in Spain this weekend for the wedding of a parishioner. Tough duty, but someone has to do it!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
