Bulletin - January 4, 2009

From the Desk of the Pastor

Dear Friends,

We will remember December of 2008! It was the Advent of the Deep Freeze, the Christmas of the Great Snow. Never in my memory have we had weather so icy and cold that we had a nearly empty Cathedral on the last two Sundays of Advent. Christmas was better, but attendance was only about half of what it has been in past years. For those of you who were unable to be here, I can well imagine how disappointed you were. Many of you, I’m sure, watched the Midnight Mass from St. James on television but that’s a poor substitute for being here in person. Another reminder of what an important part the community plays in our life and worship….

Any way you cut it, Seattle is a unique place. Only in Seattle would city officials take to the airwaves to assure people that the major arterials would be kept open for the benefit of those going to the football game on December 21 (making it clear that that was the only reason people should consider going out!). Only in Seattle! Despite that, a significant number of you did feel safe (and adventurous!) enough to make the trek to the Cathedral on that Sunday from all over the region. You came from Capitol Hill, Beacon Hill, and Rainier Valley; from Montlake and Mountlake Terrace, Magnolia and Madison Park; from North Bend, Newcastle, Preston, Issaquah, Bellevue, Squak Mountain, Federal Way, Shoreline, Sammamish, and Kenmore, and a whole lot of other places. I was impressed!

And I felt very badly for the hundreds and hundreds of you who couldn’t possibly get here. Some of you let us know through calls and e-mails how much you missed being with the community during days when being together, and praying together, and celebrating together in the Cathedral are the high point of your year.

I would have to confess to you that, for me, those days were an important learning time. If you know me at all, you know that I kind of like being on top of things and that I expect things to go as planned! Well, God used those days to teach me a little about my limits: about resignation, acceptance, and patience, gently reminding me that I wasn’t in charge (!) and that I ought, therefore, to relax a bit and let go. In a wonderful way, God turned those days from being the winter of my discontent into the winter of my discovery. It may not have been a lesson I was thrilled about getting, but I have no doubt it was one I needed.

I know you won’t take offense if I gently remind those of you who were unable to be here on those Advent Sundays or on Christmas to send in your offering, or put it in the collection basket next Sunday. As you might expect, dramatically reduced attendance resulted in drastically reduced collections. The actual shortfall for those two Advent Sundays and Christmas (compared with last year) was $50,000! As you know, we depend on every dollar to meet our payroll and program expenses, as well as to maintain the Cathedral and other parish buildings.

Here’s a closing thought for those of you who weren’t here for Christmas. Go to our wonderful website and take a look at the beautiful albums of photographs of the Christmas celebrations. Why, you can even read the Christmas homily if you have the inclination. And the patience!

Blessings for the New Year for you and your families.

Father Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s highly acclaimed journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct deposit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the kiosk at Coffee Hour. If you're not sure whether you're registered at St. James, call Maria Laughlin, 206-382- 4284.
