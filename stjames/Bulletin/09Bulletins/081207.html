WELCOME: If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

VOLUNTEER CHORE SERVICE ORIENTATION. VCS is a program of Catholic Community Services and helps to connect volunteers with people who need chore services and companionship in the Seattle area. Volunteers help to ensure that elders and individuals with disabilities and/or long-term illnesses can remain in their homes among familiar surroundings rather than going to an institutionalized setting. In October, our VCS volunteers contributed over 125 hours of service. If you are interested in joining them in this rewarding ministry, please come to the Orientation to be held in the Pastoral Outreach Center on Tuesday, December 9 at 6:30 pm. (If you are in need of assistance through VCS, their intake number is: 1-888-649-6850). Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

J-WALKING FOR HIGH SCHOOL TEENS. Are you a high school teen who likes to help others and wants to engage your faith in the world? Then J-Walking might be the perfect program for you! Join other teens from local parishes in this 4-month small group process that will start in January. The group will do service, learn about Catholic Social Teaching, discuss their experiences of living out their faith, and pray together. Applications are due December 12. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

THE CATHEDRAL KITCHEN is in need of clean 16-to 32-ounce plastic containers, with lids. Drop them off at the Parish Office or the Parish Hall. Thank you.

Next month, we have two unique ways to celebrate the Year of St. Paul. On Wednesday, January 21, 2009 at 7:00 pm, St. James will host a countywide prayer for Christian Unity. What better way to celebrate the Year of St. Paul than by praying for the unity of Christians? Our guest preacher will be Dr. Michael Kinnamon, President of the National Council of Churches of Christ in the USA and a widely-respected leader in the ecumenical movement. Bring a friend from another Christian denomination, and bring Paul’s vision of the Church to life! The service is cosponsored by the Church Council of Greater Seattle, the School of Theology and Ministry of Seattle University, and St. James Cathedral. Please visit www.thechurchcouncil.org, or our parish website for more information. The following Sunday, January 25, we have the rare opportunity to celebrate the Feast of the Conversion of St. Paul on a Sunday. Noted scripture scholar Father John Endres, SJ, will be guest homilist at all Masses for the feast.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Our Lady of Guadalupe, Seattle; St. Mary of Guadalupe, Ridgefield; and Immaculate Conception, Arlington and Mount Vernon.
