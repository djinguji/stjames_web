WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church's liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

BUILD HOPE COLLECTION. The Archbishop has asked us to raise $12,000 for this important collection which will fund a major local media campaign in Lent, 2010. Thank you to all who have given so generously to this important cause. A gift of $35 from each of our regular contributors would allow us to meet our goal.

CHILDREN’S FAITH FORMATION sessions begin this Sunday, September 27, 11:00 to 11:50 and will continue every Sunday thereafter. If you were not able to register your child, come to the second floor of Cathedral Place; you’ll still have the opportunity to do so. Information, Lita McBride, 206-654-4658; Theresa VanDeVen, 206-219-5822.

ROSARY FOR LIFE. Each Thursday in October, beginning this coming Thursday, October 1, the rosary will be prayed in the Cathedral at 6:00pm (shortly following the 5:30pm Mass). What better way to begin the month of October, Respect Life Month, than by praying the rosary together for a greater respect for all life, from conception to natural death? The rosary will be prayed each Thursday evening in October as we observe Respect Life Month.

FIRST FRIDAY. On Friday, October 2, Exposition of the Blessed Sacrament will begin following the 8:15am Mass and continue until 1:00pm, with a Holy Hour for Life beginning at 12:00 Noon, with Liturgy of the Hours, Rosary, and Benediction of the Blessed Sacrament. Deacon David Olsen will preside. All are welcome.

JOURNEY THROUGH GRIEF. Are you seeking comfort after the death of a loved one? Join others who have also experienced loss in a prayerful supportive environment. In a six-week guided group process we will journey through our grief and gently embrace our mourning so that ultimately we may find healing. Information, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

CATHOLIC 101: THE TEN COMMANDMENTS: LIVING A MORAL CHRISTIAN LIFE. Join us on Tuesday, September 29, 1:00-3:00 or 7:00-9:00, Cathedral Place, second floor for an ongoing look at the newly published US Catechism for Adults. Helen Oesterle reviews the foundations of our Catholic faith from our American Catholic experience and facilitates lively conversations among the participants. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514 (Next Class: The Tenth Commandment: Embrace Poverty of Spirit)

ADVOCACY TRAINING. During the year over 24,000 people experience homelessness in King County, many of whom are families with children. On Saturday, October 10 from 10:00am-Noon in Cathedral Hall, the St. James Housing Advocacy Committee will co-host a training program to make advocacy for the homeless politically effective, simple, and yes, even fun! This free program will be conducted by Nancy Amidei, a national expert in advocacy training who is renowned for her knowledge and the joy she brings to this important learning event. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

MARRIED COUPLES ENRICHMENT GROUP. Join us on Sunday, October 11 following the 12:00 Noon Mass for the first gathering of St. James’ Married Couples Enrichment Group. It will be an opportunity for couples to share ideas for what a marriage fellowship group might look like at St. James Cathedral. The gathering will take place on the second floor of Cathedral Place from 1:30-3:00 pm. Refreshments and babysitting provided. Information Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

SACRED MUSIC OF THE EARLY BAROQUE in the Cathedral Chapel, Sunday, October 11, 2:30pm. The singers and instrumentalists of Cathedral Resident Ensemble, Seattle Academy of Opera, present an hour-long program of masterpieces from the 17th century with Cathedral Resident Artists, Stephen Stubbs, lute; Maxine Eilander, baroque harp; Margriet Tindemans, viola da gamba; and Cathedral Organist, Joseph Adam. The Chapel concert includes the sublime soprano duet “Pulchra es” from Monteverdi’s 1610 Vespers and the modern debut of the expressive trio, Anima Peccatrice by Marco Marazzoli, composer to the Barberini Popes. Suggested donation: $10, students and seniors pay as able. Information www.seattleacademyofopera.org.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Matthew in Seattle, St. Gabriel in Port Orchard, St. Jerome in Ocean Shores, St. Therese in Seattle, and St. Theresa in Federal Way.
