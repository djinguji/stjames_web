Conversion of St. Paul

Bulletin - January 25, 2009

FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

January 25, 2009

Dear Friends,

On this Feast of the Conversion of St. Paul, we are fortunate to have as our guest homilist Father John Endres, S.J. Father Endres is an Oregon Province Jesuit and a native of Tacoma. I’ve had the privilege of knowing him and his family since I was a very young priest at St. Patrick’s parish in Tacoma where they were parishioners. John is currently a professor of Sacred Scripture at the Jesuit School of Theology at Berkeley. He has the added distinction of having been ordained to the priesthood here in St. James Cathedral by Archbishop Raymond Hunthausen. It is a joy to be able to welcome John to the pulpit of St. James!

Some more good news. As of this writing, 313 “snow angels” have helped rescue the Cathedral from the December snowfall/shortfall. This is pretty remarkable. No, let me rephrase that: you are pretty remarkable!

I’m told that there are people who don’t believe in angels. I don’t have to believe in them. I’ve met them!

You may recall that our shortfall for those two Advent Sundays and Christmas was $50,000. Thanks to so many of you who sent your envelopes in later, and thanks to each one of you who signed on as a snow angel, we are quite close to making up all our losses.

What incredible people you are! I am grateful beyond words.

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

P.S. There’s still time to become a “snow angel.” Just write snow angel on the memo line of your check (or on the visitor envelope) and drop it in the basket along with your regular offering. Thank you!

GLORIOUS SAINT PAUL, Apostle to the Gentiles,
     when the Lord called, you answered,
     and you spread the good news of the Gospel
     to the very ends of the earth.

Pray for us, that we may serve Christ as you did:
     proclaiming the name of Jesus
     in good times and bad,
     when convenient and when inconvenient.

Pray for us, that we may welcome
     all the people we meet
     with compassionate hearts and open minds.

Pray for us, that Christ may live in us
     as he lived in you.

We pray in the name of the One
     who is your Lord and ours,
     Christ Jesus our Lord. Amen.
