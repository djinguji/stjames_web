FIXING A BROKEN IMMIGRATION SYSTEM. The USCCB Justice for Immigrants Campaign calls for comprehensive immigration reform. But what does this mean and how can the immigration system be improved? HEALTH CARE REFORM: WHAT DOES THE CHURCH SAY? As part of the current debate over health care reform, the U.S. Bishops are calling for a truly universal health policy with respect for human life and dignity, access for all with a special concern for the poor and inclusion of legal immigrants, pursuing the common good and preserving pluralism including freedom of conscience and variety of options, and restraining costs and applying them equitably across the spectrum of payers. “In our Catholic tradition, health care is a basic human right. Access to health care should not depend on where a person works, how much a family earns, or where a person lives. Instead, every person, created in the image and likeness of God, has a right to life and to those things necessary to sustain life, including affordable, quality health care. This teaching is rooted in the biblical call to heal the sick and to serve ‘the least of these,’ our concern for human life and dignity, and the principle of the common good.” Learn more at USCCB website.
