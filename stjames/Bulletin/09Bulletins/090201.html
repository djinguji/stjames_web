Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • Today’s reading from First Corinthians comes in the midst of a long chapter on the blessings and challenges that come with relationships, especially marriage. In this passage, he speaks to the unique charisma, or spiritual gift, of celibacy. To what state of life have you been called by God? Do you see God’s hand in your relationships with others?
  • Paul tells the Corinthians that he encourages celibacy “not to impose a restraint upon you, but for the sake of… adherence to the Lord without distraction.” What distracts you from the Lord? What helps you to focus?
