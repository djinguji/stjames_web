Corpus Christi

From the Pastor’s Desk

June 14, 2009

Dear Friends,

It’s always good to have good news to share with you and today I have some very good news. Our parishioner, Todd Strange, was ordained to the priesthood here in the Cathedral yesterday.

Many of you know Todd. He came into the church back in 2001 through our RCIA program. After that he served as one of our Eucharistic Ministers and quietly reached out in loving service to people with special needs, including people in nursing homes and hospitals.

Todd is a gentle servant of the gospel and will be a wonderful priest.

Vocations to the priesthood are formed and fostered in many ways, but I firmly believe that a parish community has a critically important role to play in this regard. And Todd, I am sure, would be quick to affirm that about our parish, about you. You have had a part in leading him to the priesthood and his ordination is your celebration as well as his.

Todd will preside at a Mass of Thanksgiving today at the noon Mass here in the Cathedral. The Mass will be followed by a reception in the Cathedral Hall to which you are all invited. I hope you will be able to join Todd and his family as they and we give thanks to God for his vocation to service and leadership in the Church.


Father Michael G. Ryan

POSSIBLE PICKETERS. It has come to our attention that St. James Cathedral may be the site of some demonstrations by outside groups following the 10:00am Mass this Sunday. There will likely be picketers from a fringe evangelical group from Kansas which espouses a message of hate toward gay and lesbian people—a message that is completely at odds with Catholic teaching. The Church teaches clearly that homosexual persons “must be accepted with respect, compassion and sensitivity.” Rather than engage the picketers in any kind of conversation, we urge you to ignore them, to pray for them, and to focus your attention on the Corpus Christi procession which is our way of witnessing to the city our faith in Christ’s abiding presence in the sacrament of the Eucharist.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s highly acclaimed journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct deposit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. Please drop the completed card in the collection basket or put it in the mail.
If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.

A reminder about parking on Sundays
for the Cathedral

We know that parking in this area is at a premium and we are doing our best to make it available to you. In consideration of our neighbors and to ease parking problems on Sundays, please note these important Do’s and Don’ts.

You MAY park:

  • In the O’Dea High School lot (the Cathedral staff spots are also available during the weekend Masses).
  • In the Cabrini Tower Garage on Marion Street across from O’Dea High School for the Saturday 5:30 pm Mass and all Sunday Masses. The Cathedral pays for parking during these times, and you are encouraged to take advantage of it. (How often are you offered free parking in Seattle?)
  • In the P-1 parking lot under the Chancery building at 9th and Marion.

Please DO NOT park:

  • In the lot adjacent to the Envoy Apartments on 9th Avenue, directly across from the Cathedral.
  • In the Blood Bank parking lot at the corner of Terry and Columbia.
  • In the Frye Museum parking lots on Terry Avenue.
  • In the parking places at the Pastoral Outreach Center.
  • In the spaces on Terry Avenue reserved for loading and unloading of the parish vans.

 

ST. JAMES CATHEDRAL PARISH

We are the cathedral for the Catholic Archdiocese of Seattle and its Archbishop, The Most Reverend Alexander J. Brunett. We are also a parish church for a vibrant faith community with a long history that reaches back to Seattle’s early days.

We are an inner-city parish with an outreach to many who live on the edge of poverty and loneliness. We are a diverse community that welcomes, accepts, and celebrates the differences we all bring. We exist in the heart of the city, yet sometimes our parishioners come from considerable distances to worship here.

St. James Cathedral is a crossroads where ideas and challenges both old and new are explored in the light of the Gospel of Jesus Christ.

Our cathedral is also the center for many cultural and ecumenical events which both reflect and help to shape the fabric of a vital metropolitan community.

Above all, St. James Cathedral is a community of prayer.

 

Be sure to visit the place of prayer for the year of St. Paul in the south aisle of the Cathedral and online.
