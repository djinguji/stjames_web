November 15, 2009

Dear Friends,

If you see a little less of me this weekend than usual, or if you observe that my gait is a little gimpy (or I’m using crutches or a walker!), don’t feel too sorry for me. I had some surgery on my legs this past week that will necessitate my wearing “moon boots” for a couple of weeks and, inevitably, will probably cut down on my activity. You can imagine how happy that makes me! The surgery is one I’ve been putting off for years but I finally decided that delaying wasn’t going to improve things. So, I’ll appreciate a prayer or two for a quick recovery, and I’ll also appreciate your patience if it takes me a little longer to get from the chair to the pulpit to the altar!

I want to say a big thank you to Anna Horton and David Unger for their really wonderful presentation on Sacrificial Giving at the Masses last weekend, as well as to everyone who has turned in a Sacrificial Giving commitment form. And I would like to say a word of encouragement to those of you who have yet to do so. This parish depends on the generosity of each of our parishioners and families. I believe everyone can give something, even if it’s only the prayers you offer for your parish or the time and talent you share with our many ministries. And when it comes to your monetary contribution and how much you should give, all I would say is that it should reflect what God has given you. When you are at peace with yourself about the amount of your giving and don’t find yourself making an excuse for it, then you are probably giving at the right level. For some of you, that would probably be quite a lot because God has blessed you with considerable means and your gift can help make up for those who have much less. This is a wonderfully generous parish but, to be honest, too few are supporting too many! Someone asked me what our “goal” was for this campaign. Our only goal is that everyone in the parish will participate—everyone! Maybe it’s naïve to think we’ll ever get 100% participation. But I think you’ll agree St. James Cathedral can do better than the 40% participation we had last year! Please fill out our commitment card today, even if you are unable to make a pledge, and save the parish from spending a lot of money on costly follow-up mailings. Thank you!

During these November days, please continue to remember our deceased parishioners and friends, especially those whose names are printed on the cover of the bulletin. Their generous remembrance of the Cathedral in their estate planning has accounted for a significant part of the parish’s annual income over the years. Without their generous gifts we wouldn’t be who we are. Do keep them in your prayers.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH—MORALITY. Using the Bible, The Catechism of the Catholic Church, and Church documents, this adult enrichment program will explore the Ten Commandments and the Beatitudes as the framework of a moral life, identifying the call to dignity for each human person and the common good as two foundations of a moral life. Participants will look at objective moral truth and the sources of moral norms in the Church, and explore the Christian virtues and how they are relevant to moral decision-making. In addition, they will discuss what is meant by a “well-formed conscience” and a process for Catholic moral decision-making as well as the primacy of the Church’s moral teaching in the life of the believer and the inviolability of human conscience. Please bring your Bible and copy of the Catechism to the class. The class will take place on Sunday, November 22 at St. Peter’s in Beacon Hill from 1:30-5:30pm. Cost: $5 per class. (Cosponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.
