Bulletin Archive

 

WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last Sunday, we had visitors from Boerne, TX, Winkelman, AZ, and Mt. Pleasant, PA, to name a few. The winner of our drawing was from Gray, Louisiana.

FLOWERS IN THE CATHEDRAL are in memory of Frank and Estelle Nowakowski from their son Joseph.

GUEST CHOIR, SATURDAY 5:30. We welcome Nebraska Children’s Chorus Bel Canto, directed by Sean M. Burton.

PETER’S PENCE. This weekend we take up the annual Peter’s Pence Collection to support the works of the Holy Father. Your generosity will give emergency relief to those most in need around the globe, who suffer because of war, famine, disease, oppression, or natural disaster.

NEW ON THE WEBSITE. Visit the cathedral website to read Father Ryan’s homily for Corpus Christi online. You can also view photo albums of recent events, including the Corpus Christi Procession, Ordinations to the Priesthood, and Rev. Todd Strange’s Mass of Thanksgiving.

YOUNG ADULTS CELEBRATE SUMMER AT OUR INFORMAL WINE AND CHEESE GATHERING that will take place this Sunday, June 21 immediately following 5:30 Mass. Young adults age 21 and older, single or married, gather for fun, refreshments, and fellowship in the Fireside Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Newcomers and those not new to the parish are all welcome! Information, TerryAnn Bowen or Young Adults, 206-382-2018.

PRIEST DAYS. During the coming week, June 22-25, the priests of the Archdiocese will gather at Ocean Shores for their annual meeting with Archbishop Brunett. During this time, there will be just one Mass each day in the Cathedral, at 5:30pm. Morning Prayer will be prayed each day at 8:15am, and Midday Prayer at 12:10pm. The regular liturgy schedule will resume on Friday, June 26. During this novena of prayer to mark the end of the Year of St. Paul, each of these liturgies will conclude with a procession to the Place of Prayer in honor of St. Paul.

THE CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE. Stop by the bookstore today for a great selection of Cathedral souvenirs… postcards, note cards, books, mugs, magnets, and more!

SUMMER TOURS. During the summer months, June, July, and August, we offer a tour of the Cathedral every Wednesday at 1:00pm. This is a great way to show out-of-town guests your Cathedral! No reservations required.

MASS FOR DECEASED BISHOPS. On Friday, June 26 at 5:30pm, a special Mass will be offered in memory of all the deceased bishops of Seattle. In a special way, we will remember Archbishop Murphy, who died on June 26, 1997, and who is interred in the Cathedral crypt. Archbishop Brunett will preside. All are welcome.

CONCLUSION OF THE YEAR OF SAINT PAUL. In this week’s bulletin you will find a guide to joining in a novena of prayer to mark the conclusion of the Year of St. Paul. Each Mass this weekend and during the next nine days will conclude with a procession to the Place of Prayer. The Year of Saint Paul will conclude on Monday, June 29.

WALKING WITH OUR LADY. The Health and Healing Committee invites you to walk for your health to shrines of Our Lady around the world. Our goal is to collect 18,000 miles and to “travel” from St. James to Fatima and Lourdes! If you signed up at the Health and Healing Fair on May 5, you can bring your record sheet for June to the Hall next Sunday, June 28, at coffee hour. If you’d like to join the fun and get healthier at the same time, stop by the table at coffee hour for a free pedometer to get you going. Information, John Simpson, 206-274-3173.

TAIZÉ PRAYER AROUND THE CROSS. Each Friday at 6:30 pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

CLOTHING DONATIONS NEEDED FOR YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT. Next month, a group of teens from St. James and St. Mary’s will travel to Lynden, WA to volunteer in the fields, migrant camps, and Head Start daycare center. As part of their trip, the teens collect donations to distribute to migrant families in Lynden. Donations can be dropped off at the Parish Office through June 26. For a list of needed donations, visit the outreach website. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

WORLD PREMIERE. Sunday, June 28, 8:00pm Organist Douglas Cleveland, “a brilliant performer with prodigious technique,” joins Cathedral Resident Ensemble Seattle Pro Musica in the first performance of an exciting new work by Bruce Neswick, commissioned by the American Guild of Organists for the AGO Regional Convention. Free and open to all.

10th ANNUAL SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL. Join us on Tuesday, July 14 at 7:00pm in Cathedral Place, second floor, for the second film of the summer, The Secret Life of Bees. Haunted by the death of her mother, 14 year-old Lily escapes the dreary life she lives on her father’s farm and heads to Tiburon, SC, with her friend and caregiver Rosaleen, setting off on a life-changing journey of self-discovery. Set amidst the hotbed of racial tensions in the South in the 1960s, this dramatic tale is based on the bestselling novel by Sue Monk Kidd. Rated PG-13. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, July 8. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

WELCOME, PARENTS WITH YOUNG CHILDREN. We support all of our parishioners who are attending Mass with young children and we welcome those children into our midst. If you find that your child needs a quiet place away from the stimulation of the Mass, please feel free to step into the west vestibule or the cathedral chapel, where we have provided a sound system so that you can continue to participate in the Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. Please note that babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

REST IN PEACE. Please pray for the repose of the soul of Linda Condes.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Immaculate Heart of Mary in Sedro Woolley; Sacred Heart in Seattle, Bellevue, and Morton; and St. John the Baptist in Covington.
