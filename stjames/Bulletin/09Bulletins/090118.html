VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES

HELP A SENIOR IN NEED. Many people in our St. James community and beyond are in need of help with transportation, shopping, laundry, housework, and more. Age, illness, economics, and disabilities can all be barriers to accomplishing these routine daily tasks. By volunteering just a few hours a month, you can make a huge difference in someone’s life by providing them peace of mind and the option to stay in their own home rather than going to an institutionalized setting. If you are interested in joining this rewarding ministry, please come to the Orientation to be held in the Pastoral Outreach Center on Tuesday, January 27 at 6:30 pm. Information Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

CELEBRATING CATHOLIC SCHOOLS—THE RAINBOW SCHOOLS COALITION. On the weekend of January 31- February 1 we will celebrate Catholic School education, and in a particular way the Cathedral children who attend one of the five Rainbow Schools. Our parish is a contributing member of the Rainbow Schools Program whereby children from our parish can attend at “parishioner” tuition rates. Approximately 60 cathedral children are enrolled in one of these fine schools. Representatives of Sts. Edward, George, Paul, Joseph and Terese Catholic schools will be available after the 10:00 and 12:00 Masses during hospitality. Children are encouraged to come to Mass in their uniforms. In addition, our parish has established the Cathedral Scholarship Fund to assist families in meeting the financial obligation involved in sending their elementary school aged children to any on of the Rainbow Schools. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

  • Today we begin a sequence of readings from First Corinthians. In some of his letters, Paul emphasizes the tension between soul and body; but here, he reminds the Corinthians of the integrity of the whole human person. "Your bodies are members of Christ," he says. What are the implications of this statement for our Christian theology of the body?
  • Paul urges the Corinthians to "glorify God in your body." The great runner Eric Liddell, whose story is told in the film "Chariots of Fire," echoes these words of St. Paul when he says "God made me fast, and when I run, I feel His pleasure in my speed." How do you "glorify God in your body"?
