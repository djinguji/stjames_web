WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church's liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are from Catherine Bailey in memory of Sam Bailey, on the fifth anniversary of his death. Flowers at the Mary shrine are from the Saturday wedding of Lenore Mangalindan and Mark Corcoro.

ROSARY FOR LIFE. Each Thursday in October, the rosary will be prayed in the Cathedral at 6:00pm (shortly following the 5:30pm Mass). What better way to observe Respect Life Month, than by praying the rosary together for a greater respect for all life, from conception to natural death?

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. Visit the Respect Life table in the Cathedral Bookstore for a wide selection of reading on the whole spectrum of life issues. Also in stock: beautiful crosses made from stone quarried in the Holy Land.

SOLEMN MASS OF ALL SOULS. November 2, 7:30 pm. If you would like to be remember someone in the printed program or be a patron, please put your donation in an envelope marked “All Souls Patron” and drop it in the offering basket or send to the parish office. Be sure to include the name of the person you want to remember, as well as your name and address. Information, Music Office, 206-382-4874.

“BREAD FOR THE WORLD SUNDAY” is coming! Bread for the World is an ecumenical Christian voice urging our nation’s decision makers to end hunger at home and abroad. On the weekend of October 31 and November 1, St. James Cathedral will participate in the Bread for the World campaign. Volunteers will be available to assist you in writing letters to congressional representatives, urging them to support anti-hunger legislation. To learn more about hunger, and what you can do to make a difference, see Bread for the World’s website. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

“PEELING THE ONION: THE MORAL DIMENSIONS OF HEALTH CARE REFORM” (Co-sponsored by St. James Cathedral and SU School of Theology and Ministry) The event has been postponed due to ongoing legislative efforts in Washington D.C. More information to come soon!

Volunteer Opportunities

WINTER SHELTER TOILETRIES DONATIONS NEEDED. As part of their overnight stay, the men receive toiletries they can use to freshen up before heading out for the day (many go to work). We are able to provide these toiletries thanks to generous donations of St. James parishioners. Please drop off any of the following unused items in the blue bin at coffee hour: disposable razors, cough drops, individually wrapped toothbrushes and travel-sized toothpaste. Information, Denise Carlascio, 425-687-7495.

LET YOUR PRESENCE BE A PRESENT TO SOMEONE IN NEED. During times of personal crisis, in addition to practical services, people often need the caring presence of a compassionate person willing to listen. The Solanus Casey Center, a program of St. James Cathedral and Catholic Community Services, seeks to provide that listening ear, as well as referrals and limited assistance, to those in need in our community. The Center is currently in need of a few kind volunteers who can help one day a week from 12:45pm-4:15pm. Training is available. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

VOLUNTEERS NEEDED FOR GREAT MUSIC FOR GREAT CATHEDRALS 2010. Great Music will be February 11, 12, and 13, 2010 and we will need your support once again for this exciting St. James original production. Volunteers are needed for setup/strike, production, stage crew, vestments, hospitality, or office help. The time commitments for our projects range from a mere two hours to a very full two weeks and everything in between. Information Ian Newhall 206-382-4874.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, November 4 at 7:00 pm. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. You can also check out our website.

SEATTLE HOUSING LEVY RENEWAL. Our Catholic faith teaches that everyone deserves a safe, decent, affordable place to live. Please prayerfully reflect on these teachings of our faith as you vote on the Housing Levy, listed on November’s ballot as Prop. 1. You can see one success of the past levy across from the Cathedral at Ozanam House. Information, www.yesforhomes.org.

CATHOLIC 101: GOD CALLS US TO PRAYER. Join us on Tuesdays, November 3, 10, & 17, 1:00-3:00 or 7:00-9:00, Cathedral Place-second floor. We begin our year of reflection and study on prayer by reviewing the last section of the US Catechism for Adults on Christian prayer. Helen Oesterle reviews the foundations of our Catholic faith from our American Catholic experience and facilitates lively conversations among the participants. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

MASS FOR DECEASED HOMELESS. On Monday, November 9, 2009, at 5:30pm, you are invited to a special Mass in memory of the men and women who died on the streets of King County during the past year. November 9 is the Feast of the Dedication of the Lateran Basilica in Rome. As we recall the dedication of a great house of God, we also remember that Christ himself is still homeless, in his suffering brothers and sisters throughout the world and on our streets.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Charles Borromeo in Tacoma and Burlington, and Our Lady of the Olympics Mission in Quinault.
