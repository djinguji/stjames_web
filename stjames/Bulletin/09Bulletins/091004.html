From the Pastor’s Desk

October 4, 2009

Dear Friends,

With October comes Respect Life month. So precious is the gift of human life that once a year our bishops set aside a full month for us to reflect on it and to pray about it. In last Sunday’s bulletin (it’s also online at our website) you saw an insert that set the stage for this annual observance. It set forth very clearly and strongly what is commonly called the Church’s “consistent ethic of life,” or to use the phrase often used by the late Cardinal Joseph Bernardin, the “seamless garment” that is human life. Included in this seamless garment are issues such as abortion, stem cell research, euthanasia and assisted suicide, capital punishment, torture, war and terrorism, health care, homelessness, and a whole range of poverty issues—in a word, every issue that in any way impacts or adversely affects the precious gift of human life. The Church may not assign equal importance to each issue but its teaching about life makes sense, not when we pick and choose among them, but only when every one of them is taken into account and taken seriously. It is one of the glories of the Catholic Church that we are identified as being outspoken advocates for human life—all of human life.

On each of these October Sundays a different bulletin insert will offer some reflections and talking points on several of the great human life issues. I hope you will take time to read them and ponder them. I also hope you will make these issues a part of your personal prayer and your family prayer during these days.

On another note, I want to take this opportunity to thank you for your wonderfully generous response to the Build Hope collection this year. It is always very moving to me—and very humbling—to witness your generosity in response to an appeal. Thanks to you, we will be able to meet the goal set for us by the Archdiocese to support the Catholics Come Home media project that will take place during Lent of the coming year. Again, my heartfelt thanks.

The Archbishop Hunthausen Charity Golf Tournament last Monday was a great success and that was no easy achievement during down economic times. Thanks to a whole lot of good people, we raised significant funds that will be used to support our many Cathedral social outreach programs. Archbishop Hunthausen was unable to be with us this year but we felt his spirit there as we raised money for causes dear to his heart.

Fall is definitely upon us. For me, that means a welcome hiatus in the weekend wedding schedule and an opportunity to get away for some vacation. I’m ready. I’ll miss you, but something tells me I’ll get over it…! See you in a couple of weeks!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
