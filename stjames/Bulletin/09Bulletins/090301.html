JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Beginning this week, our newly Elect are wearing brown robes while attending Mass and other liturgies during Lent. This helps them to know, in a concrete way, that they have entered a new and significant stage in their final preparations for Easter. It also anticipates another change that will take place when they emerge from the baptismal waters at the Easter Vigil and change to white robes. Seek them out in our midst and, if possible, introduce yourself to them after Mass. Let them know you are praying for them and looking forward to Easter when they will join us at the Eucharistic table.

One of the many ways you can do this is to become a prayer partner for one of our Elect. You are asked to pray everyday for him/her during Lent, asking for God’s blessings and guidance. ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct deposit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the kiosk in Cathedral Hall. If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.

During this Lenten Season Catholic Relief Services invites us to journey in solidarity with our global family through Operation Rice Bowl. Donations collected in your Rice Bowl go to poor countries around the world to fund development projects—agriculture, water, education, microfinance—that help communities break free of hunger. Operation Rice Bowl collects money for Catholic Relief Services, the official aid agency of the US Catholic Church, who then distributes the money to local Catholic charities and other small community agencies who make a big difference in their communities. What comes out of the Rice Bowl is as important as what goes into it. In each folded-up cardboard bowl is a calendar with a prayer, reflection, fact, or action for every day of Lent, all based on Catholic Social Teaching. Each week the calendar invites you to fast in solidarity with the poor around the world, using a simple meal from the "developing" world. What goes into the bowl is your help to people around the world who face hunger and rising food prices. What comes out of the bowl is a way to walk through Lent in deeper solidarity with the whole human family... It is a journey of prayer, fasting, learning and giving, all with an international flavor.
