WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church's liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are in memory of Patrick Lanphier, from his family, on the third anniversary of his death.

ROSARY FOR LIFE. Each Thursday in October, the rosary will be prayed in the Cathedral at 6:00pm (shortly following the 5:30pm Mass). What better way to observe Respect Life Month, than by praying the rosary together for a greater respect for all life, from conception to natural death?

FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH SERIES: ISRAEL’S SACRED HISTORY AND INSTITUTIONS: Today, October 18 at St. Peter’s Parish Hall in Beacon Hill from 1:30-5:30 pm. In this Scripture class, participants will explore the historical books and the Major Prophets in the Old Testament – becoming familiar with the characters. They will learn about the importance of the roles of priest, prophet, and king in Israel’s history and understand how, through our baptism, we are called to be priest, prophet, and king. Cost: $5 per class. Please bring your Bible and copy of the Catechism to the class. (Cosponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

YOUNG ADULTS (21 - 30-SOMETHING) 3RD SUNDAY INFORMAL WINE AND CHEESE. Today, October 18 at 4:00 pm. Enjoy fellowship from 4:00 to 4:30 followed by guided study of the Scripture readings for the day. The study will finish in time to attend 5:30 Mass. Young adults over the age of 21, single or married, gather in the Fireside Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Newcomers and those not new to the parish are all welcome! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

“BREAD FOR THE WORLD SUNDAY” is coming! Bread for the World is an ecumenical Christian voice urging our nation’s decision makers to end hunger at home and abroad. On the weekend of October 31 and November 1, St. James Cathedral will participate in the Bread for the World campaign by having available volunteers to assist you in writing your letters to congressional representatives, urging them to support anti-hunger legislation. To learn more about hunger, and what you can do to make a difference, see Bread for the World’s website. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, November 4 at 7:00 pm. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. You can also check out our website.

SEATTLE HOUSING LEVY RENEWAL. Proposition 1 on November’s ballot will renew the Seattle Housing Levy. You can see one success of the past levy across 9th Avenue from the Cathedral at Ozanam House. Our Catholic faith teaches that a basic moral test of society is how it treats the poor, and that every person made in God’s image has a fundamental right to life and to those things required for human decency, including shelter. Please prayerfully reflect on these teachings of our faith as you vote on the Housing Levy. More info at www.yesforhomes.org.
