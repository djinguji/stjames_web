WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church's liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are in memory of Robert Verfurth, from his family, on the fifth anniversary of his death.

ROSARY FOR LIFE. Each Thursday in October, the rosary will be prayed in the Cathedral at 6:00pm (shortly following the 5:30pm Mass). What better way to observe Respect Life Month, than by praying the rosary together for a greater respect for all life, from conception to natural death?

MARRIED COUPLES ENRICHMENT GROUP. Join us today, Sunday, October 11 following the 12:00 Noon Mass for the first gathering of St. James’ Married Couples Enrichment Group. It will be an opportunity for couples to share ideas for what a marriage fellowship group might look like at St. James Cathedral. The gathering will take place on the second floor of Cathedral Place from 1:30-3:00 pm. Refreshments and babysitting provided.

SACRED MUSIC OF THE EARLY BAROQUE IN THE CATHEDRAL CHAPEL. Today, October 11, 2:30 pm, the singers and continuo players of Resident Ensemble, with Seattle Academy of Opera. Suggested donation $10, students and seniors pay as able.

THIS YEAR’S LAST SENIOR TRIP will be Wednesday October 14. Join us as we visit the Future of Flight exhibit, and take the tour of the Boeing plant in Everett. We will look at cutting-edge airline production and the future of aviation. The cost is $15. Also, the tour may require a one-third-mile walk and 21 steep stairs. Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place leave a message with John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH SERIES: ISRAEL’S SACRED HISTORY AND INSTITUTIONS: Sunday, October 18 at St. Peter’s Parish Hall in Beacon Hill from 1:30-5:30 pm. In this Scripture class, participants will explore the historical books and the Major Prophets in the Old Testament – becoming familiar with the characters. They will learn about the importance of the roles of priest, prophet and king in Israel’s history and understand how, through our baptism, we are called to be priest, prophet and king. Cost: $5 per class. Please bring your Bible and copy of the Catechism to the class. (Cosponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

YOUNG ADULTS (21 - 30-SOMETHING) 3RD SUNDAY informal wine and cheese gathering will be Sunday, October 18 starting at 4:00 pm. Enjoy fellowship from 4:00 to 4:30 followed by guided study of the Scripture readings for the day. The study will finish in time to attend 5:30 Mass. Young adults over the age of 21, single or married, gather in the Fireside Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Newcomers and those not new to the parish are all welcome! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

SOLEMN MASS OF ALL SOULS. November 2, 7:30 pm. If you would like to be remember someone in the printed program or be a patron, please put your donation in an envelope marked “All Souls Patron” and drop it in the offering basket or send to the parish office. Be sure to include the name of the person you want to remember, as well as your name and address. Information, Music Office, 206-382-4874.

“BREAD FOR THE WORLD SUNDAY” is coming! Bread for the World is an ecumenical Christian voice urging our nation’s decision makers to end hunger at home and abroad. On the weekend of October 31 and November 1, St. James Cathedral will participate in the Bread for the World campaign by having available volunteers to assist you in writing your letters to congressional representatives, urging them to support anti-hunger legislation. To learn more about hunger, and what you can do to make a difference, see Bread for the World’s website. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, November 4 at 7:00 pm. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. You can also check out our website.

WINTER SHELTER TOILETRIES DONATIONS NEEDED. St. James starts its 18th year of Winter Shelter this weekend. An important piece of the ministry is toiletries distribution. As part of their overnight stay, the men receive toiletries they can use to freshen up before heading out for the day (many go to work). We are able to provide these toiletries thanks to generous donations given by St. James parishioners. Please drop off any of the following unused items in the blue bin at coffee hour: disposable razors, cough drops, individually wrapped toothbrushes and travel-sized toothpaste. Information, Denise Carlascio, 425-687-7495.

“PEELING THE ONION: THE MORAL DIMENSIONS OF HEALTH CARE REFORM ” (Co-sponsored by St. James Cathedral and SU School of Theology and Ministry) Reserve Friday, October 23, 7:00 pm in Cathedral Hall for a panel discussion with distinguished guests and SU professors who will peel back the complex components of the health care reform effort—its political, economic, patient care, and legal dimensions—to unearth the moral issues underpinning the entire conversation that are not explored sufficiently in this heated national debate. More information to come next week!

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Luke, Shoreline; Our Lady of Fatima, Seattle; and St. Edward, Seattle and Shelton.
