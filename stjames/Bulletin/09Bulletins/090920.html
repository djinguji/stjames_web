From the Pastor’s Desk

September 13, 2009

Dear Friends,

This is “Back to School” time for many of our children. It may also be “Back to the Cathedral” time for families that have been traveling this summer or perhaps attending church closer to home. In any case, it’s wonderful to be together again! While summer brought us the largest number of visitors in my memory, no visitor could ever take your place!

It’s good, too, to have the Cathedral Choir back with us at the 10:00 Mass. Next week, we will welcome back the Women’s Schola to the Sunday evening Mass.

The special “Build Hope” collection today has an importance beyond what it has had in past years. A major purpose of this collection has long been to fund, at the national level, the use of the media for preaching the gospel message. This year, however, the emphasis will be on buying time on local media (especially television). During Lent 2010, the Archdiocese will be undertaking a massive local media campaign called “Catholics Come Home.” It will involve a series of very thought-provoking television spots addressed to Catholics who no longer practice their faith as well as others who might be interested in exploring the Catholic faith. Needless to say, the television time is extremely expensive but I think you will agree that the cause is more than worthwhile.

Our parish has been asked to contribute $12,000 to the “Catholics Come Home” media campaign. In order to meet this, I need to ask that you consider giving a larger gift to the Build Hope collection than you may have done in the past. I know each of you will do what you can because you always do. A suggestion: a gift of $35 from each of our regular contributors would allow us to meet our goal.

Let me thank you in advance. As always, I am deeply grateful for your generosity.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

HELP A SENIOR IN NEED. Many in our community are in need of help with transportation, shopping, laundry, housework, and more. If you are interested in joining this rewarding ministry, please come to the orientation on the 1st floor of the Pastoral Outreach Center on Tuesday, September 22 at 6:30pm.
