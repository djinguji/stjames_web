JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

This week we celebrate the third and final Scrutiny with the Gospel story of the raising of Lazarus. Death is our greatest fear and this story reminds us that God’s power and mercy are greater than ANY fear, sin or pain that we have in our lives. The Elect are asked to think about what needs to die in their lives in order for them to receive the new life being offered to them in baptism. For some, the question of whether they even want to come out of the tomb is important, whether they are willing to risk coming out into the light of Christ to see what awaits them. This week they will also be presented with The Lord’s Prayer, which teaches us that God loves as a loving father and will “give us …our daily bread” and “lead us from temptation.” As they approach these last days of preparation before baptism, keep them in your prayers. IT IS NOT TOO LATE TO ADOPT ONE OF OUR ELECT! Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

OPERATION RICE BOWL calls us to walk in solidarity with our brothers and sisters in need. During this fifth week of Lent, we focus on Ghana, where Operation Rice Bowl funds support the Quality Education Improvement Program. The program provides teachers with formal training and helps increase community involvement in schools. In Ghana, most girls still do not attend school, and most boys have to limit their school days in order to go to work to help their families. The educational programs supported by Operation Rice Bowl are working to improve education for all children in Ghana. Let us accept the challenge of Operation Rice Bowl: to pray that we overcome indifference to the needs of others; to fast in solidarity with those who are hungry by eating simply; to learn about the needs of the poor, and to give money saved by fasting and eating simply to Operation Rice Bowl.

During the Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • The passage from the Letter to the Romans is full of contrasts: death and life; flesh and spirit; sin and righteousness. This passage suggests that we are all Lazarus, bound by the death that is sin. What tombs hold you bound in your life? Sinful patterns of behavior; addictions; destructive relationships? Lent is a time to become aware of what holds us bound, and to let Jesus call us forth into new life.
  • St. Paul speaks in the future tense: “If the Spirit of the one who raised Jesus from the dead dwells in you, the one who raised Christ from the dead will give life to your mortal bodies also, through his Spirit dwelling in you.” The Spirit, as St. Paul says elsewhere, is given to us as a first installment—we aren’t there yet! What do you think the fulfillment will be like?
