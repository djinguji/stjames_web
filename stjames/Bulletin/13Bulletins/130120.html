WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

CHRISTIAN UNITY WEEK. The Week of Prayer for Christian Unity begins on January 18 and concludes on January 25. Visit the World Council of Churches website to find prayers and reflections on this year’s theme: “What does the Lord require? Only to do justice, to love mercy, and to walk humbly with your God” (Micah 6:6-8). The Countywide Prayer for Christian Unity will be held at St. Ignatius Chapel on the Seattle University campus on Tuesday, January 22, 2013, at 7:00 pm. And all are invited to conclude the week by participating in our ecumenical prayer for Christian Unity on Friday evening, January 25 at 6:30pm, where Father Ryan will be joined by leaders of different downtown congregations.

VATICAN II AT 50: TOWARD A DYNAMIC UNDERSTANDING OF CONCILIAR RECEPTION. On Thursday, January 24, at 7:00pm at Pigott Auditorium, Seattle University, you’re invited to continue exploring the Second Vatican Council. What does it means to say that an ecumenical council’s teaching has been “received”? This lecture will explore how a dynamic understanding of conciliar reception must involve dialogue, participation, and even “re-invention.” A member of the IHM Sisters, Mary Ann Hinsdale is an associate professor of systematic theology at Boston College. This lecture is free and open to the public.

WOULD YOU CONSIDER ASSISTING A PERSON who wants to become Catholic? We have a number of new inquirers starting our Christian Initiation process (RCIA) to learn more about the Catholic Church. We need St. James parishioners to be companions with some of our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. The time commitment is reasonable–two Wednesday evening sessions a month. Training and support are provided. The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your own faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. If you are not sure whether or not you’re registered, call the Parish Office, 206-622-3559.
