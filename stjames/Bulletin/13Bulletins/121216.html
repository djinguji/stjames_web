WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE. Cathedral Christmas cards are now in stock, including some beautiful new designs! Also, Sunday Missals for 2013 are in, as well as some wonderful new prayer books for young children. And Cathedral Music CDs make great Christmas gifts! Share your Cathedral with family and friends.

DEDICATION OF THE CATHEDRAL. On Friday, December 21 at the 5:30pm Mass, we will celebrate our annual solemnity marking the anniversary of dedication of this Cathedral church. St. James was dedicated on December 22, 1907 by Bishop Edward O’Dea, and rededicated on December 22, 1994 by Archbishop Thomas J. Murphy. The Mass will begin with a procession through the Cathedral for the lighting of the dedication candles. All are welcome to participate as we celebrate this beautiful house of prayer.

NO TAIZE PRAYER FRIDAY 12/21. On Friday, December 21, in order to prepare the Cathedral for Christmas services, we are not able to have our usual Taizé Prayer service. Taizé Prayer will resume on Friday, December 28 at 6:30pm.

COMMUNAL RECONCILIATION SERVICES will be held on Monday, December 17 at 7:30pm (not 4:00pm as printed in the monthly calendar) and Saturday, December 22 at 4:00pm. During these beautiful services of readings, reflection and song, we will open ourselves to experience the gift of God’s gracious and loving forgiveness in the Sacrament of Penance. It has long been a tradition in the church to confess one’s sins before celebrating the great feast of Christmas. These will be ideal times to do so.

CHRISTMAS MASSES. Christmas at the Cathedral begins at 5:30pm on Monday, December 24 with the Vigil Mass of Christmas, and continues with the Midnight Mass, and the Masses of Christmas Day—8:00am, 10:00am, and 12:00 Noon. Solemn Vespers of Christmas will be at 4:00pm on Christmas Day. (There is no 5:30pm Mass on Christmas Day.) View the complete schedule of Christmas Masses at St. James Cathedral.

SACRIFICIAL SUNDAY GIVING. We are four weeks into our annual Sacrificial Giving campaign. We have heard from more than 700 parishioners—which is great news! But that leaves 1,900 who have not yet completed a pledge card for 2013. Please complete and turn in a card even if you are unable to commit to a specific amount in 2013. This will save you from receiving follow up letters and e-mails. If you are able to give, please turn in your pledge today or go online to make a pledge. Thank you!

DO YOU SHOP AT BARTELL’S? If you shop at Bartell’s, every purchase you make can benefit St. James Cathedral if you register for their “B” Caring program. Ask at Bartell’s for details. If you’re already signed up, be sure to use your “B” Caring card when you check out.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. If you are not sure whether or not you’re registered, call the Parish Office, 206-622-3559.

Getting to know Good Pope John

Be sure to stop by our new shrine in honor of Blessed John XXIII to offer a prayer to this great saint of modern times.

As he was dying, John said to his secretary, Msgr. Capovilla: “We’ve worked together and served the Church without stopping to pick up and throw back the stones that have sometimes blocked our path. You’ve put up with my defects, and I’ve put up with yours. We’ll always be friends… when all this is over, get some rest and go see your mother.”

From Hebbelthwaite’s biography of Blessed Pope John XXIII

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Our Lady of Guadalupe in Seattle and St. Mary of Guadalupe in Ridgefield.
