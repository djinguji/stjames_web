WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE. Get your last-minute Christmas shopping done! We have a wide variety of excellent Catholic reading, including some wonderful new prayer books for young children. And Cathedral Music CDs make great Christmas gifts! Share your Cathedral with family and friends.

NO SANDWICHES THIS MONDAY. This Monday, December 24 is the fourth Monday of the month and typically is a day the cathedral makes sandwiches for St. Martin de Porres Shelter. This Monday they DO NOT need sandwiches from the Cathedral. We will be providing sandwiches again on Monday, December 31.

CHRISTMAS MASSES. View the complete schedule of Christmas Masses at St. James Cathedral, as well as details about music at each liturgy.

HAVE YOU BEEN CONFIRMED? Adults and youth aged 16 and older are invited to complete their Christian initiation beginning in early 2013. ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, January 9, 2013 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

OPPORTUNITIES TO SERVE

WOULD YOU CONSIDER ASSISTING A PERSON WHO WANTS TO BECOME CATHOLIC? We have a number of new inquirers starting our Christian Initiation process (RCIA) in January 2013 to learn more about the Catholic Church. We need St. James parishioners to be companions with some of our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. The time commitment is reasonable – two Wednesday evening sessions a month. Training and support are provided. The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your own faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

CATHEDRAL FLORAL WORKSHOP, Saturday, January 19, 9:00 am-12:00 Noon. Cathedral Florist, Debe Meder, will share her techniques and creative ideas with parishioners interested in assisting in the preparation of floral offerings. Information, or to enroll, call the Music Office, 206-382-4874

MEET OTHER PARISHIONERS. Join with hundreds of parishioners in our parish’s upcoming production of Great Music for Great Cathedrals. We need folks who can volunteer a couple of hours or a couple of weeks and everything in between. We need costume sewers, stage crew, lighting assistants, office support, etc. This is a great way to get to know other parishioners and make new friendships. Information, Music Office, 206-382-4874.

TOILETRY DONATIONS. We are collecting donations of toiletries for our Winter Shelter and for the Solanus Casey Center. Please bring in donations of shaving cream, razors, soaps, lotions, etc. Drop them off at Coffee Hour after masses or at the parish office Monday-Friday 9am-5pm.

THE MENTAL HEALTH MINISTRY RESOURCE CENTER is open the last Sunday of every month 9am – 2pm. Our next open house is Sunday, December 30! Please drop in! The resource center provides information related to mental health/illnesses, emotional distress, living well; referrals to professional and medical assistance, as well as someone to listen, talk to, guide you through. We will have brochures, reference books and other reading materials available – all in a relaxed, comfortable and welcoming environment. Come and visit. Information, Rick Martin, 206-382-4269.

DO YOU SHOP AT BARTELL’S? If you shop at Bartell’s, every purchase you make can benefit St. James Cathedral if you register for their “B” Caring program. Ask at Bartell’s for details. If you’re already signed up, be sure to use your “B” Caring card when you check out.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. John in Vancouver and Seattle; St. John Mission in Montesano and Mukilteo; St. Stephen the Martyr in Renton, and St. John of the Woods in Tacoma.
