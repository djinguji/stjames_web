Advent I

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

ADVENT BAZAAR. Today, after the morning Masses, at coffee hour in Cathedral Hall, you are invited to start your Christmas shopping at our annual Advent bazaar, specializing in fair trade items and great gifts supporting excellent causes. If you ordered a Christmas wreath or poinsettia, you can pick it up today. You can also stop by to donate socks or toiletries, or to take a tag from our Giving Tree.

SAINT KATERI TEKAKWITHA. The Archdiocese of Seattle has commissioned a large icon of one of the newest saints of the Church, Kateri Tekakwitha, the first Native American to be canonized. The icon is in the Cathedral Chapel for a short time—stop by to view it and to read a little more about this extraordinary icon of an extraordinary woman.

CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE. Cathedral Christmas cards are back in stock, including some beautiful new designs! Also, Sunday Missals for 2013 are now in, as well as some wonderful new prayer books for young children.

FIRST FRIDAY EXPOSITION AND HOLY HOUR. This coming Friday, December 7, Exposition of the Blessed Sacrament will begin following the 8:15am Mass and continue until 1:00pm. Holy Hour will begin at 12:00 Noon, with readings, hymns, and rosary as we prepare to honor Mary’s Immaculate Conception on December 8. You are invited to join in all or part of this contemplative prayer in the presence of the Blessed Sacrament.

HOLY DAY SCHEDULE. During the coming week, we will celebrate the Solemnity of the Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin Mary. There will be two holy day Masses in the Cathedral: Friday, December 7 at 5:30pm, and Saturday, December 8 at 8:15am. Please note that the evening Mass on December 8 will be an Advent Mass, not a Mass of the Immaculate Conception.

THANKSGIVING AT THE CATHEDRAL KITCHEN. Last Wednesday morning, five volunteers prepared and baked 25 pumpkin pies, even making homemade crust! With 30 volunteers on Thanksgiving Day, we prepared a beautiful traditional turkey dinner for 191 guests. We received donations of sixteen 15lb. birds, as well as plenty of potatoes, green beans, onions, bread, cranberries, and milk to prepare a very tasty meal. We know, particularly this year with our volunteer guest chef, that there was not a more satisfying meal served anywhere in the city of Seattle. Thank you to all of you that participated in this beautiful “table” of plenty, for our less fortunate brothers and sisters who come to our door. Our guests thank you!

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Francis Xavier in Toledo, St. Nicholas in Gig Harbor, and St. Barbara in Black Diamond.
