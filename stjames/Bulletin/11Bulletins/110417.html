PUBLIC FAST FOR A JUST AND COMPASSIONATE BUDGET. This year, Holy Week coincides with the last week of the scheduled legislative session in Olympia. One of the core principles of Catholic Social Teaching is that economic decisions must be judged in light of their effect on the poor. The budget debates at the state and federal level threaten to cut services to our most vulnerable brothers and sisters. During Holy Week, we have an opportunity to combine our Lenten practices of prayer and fasting with a particular focus on seeking a budget that reflects justice. The Church Council of Greater Seattle is organizing public fasts at the state capital in Olympia; information can be found at www.thechurchcouncil.org/fast.htm. Even if you cannot participate in the public fast, please consider uniting your prayer and fasting during Holy Week with the cause of a just and compassionate budget. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, May 11 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Our Elect are now entering their final week of preparation for the Easter Vigil, where they will be baptized, confirmed, and receive Eucharist for the first time as one with us in the faith. They will participate with us in our Holy Week liturgies, being dismissed for the last time on Holy Thursday evening. They will also participate in a daylong retreat on Holy Saturday in their final preparation rites prior to the celebration of the Vigil of all vigils! You are encouraged to join us at the Easter Vigil to celebrate this most important day of their lives where we, the baptized, will also renew our baptismal promises as we celebrate the resurrection of our Lord.

Thank you for keeping our Elect in your prayers during this season of Lent. Now is the time to turn in your personalized cards or notes for our Elect that will be distributed to them on Holy Saturday. You can drop off your cards in the baskets located at the place of prayer in the Cathedral, in the Hall during hospitality on Sunday mornings, or in the Bookstore during its regular hours. They need to be turned in no later than Holy Thursday evening. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.
