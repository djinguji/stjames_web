Easter III

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, May 11 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

TAKE ME OUT TO THE BALLGAME! Join us for Cathedral Night at Safeco Field as the Seattle Mariners take on the New York Yankees on Friday, May 27 at 7:10pm. $17/person (some accessible seating is available-first come, first serve). Reserve your tickets now! You can download the ticket order form at our parish website. Information, Theresa Van de Ven, 206-219-5822.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP will be Wednesday May 11. We will visit the Bremerton Naval Museum and explore the naval history of the region. In addition to the fascinating ship-building exhibits experience life as a sailor onboard one of the U.S. Navy's nuclear aircraft carriers. Explore where sailors sleep, what they eat, and how they spend their time. Please join us! Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners. Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET. Next Sunday, May 15 from 11:30am-1:00 pm in Gallagher Center (lower level of rectory—Columbia St. entrance) will be the continuation of our ongoing series to support persons in the job market. This series incorporates efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions are facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

DO YOU HAVE A CHILD WHO IS: At least 7 years old seeking Baptism in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 7 years old baptized in another Christian tradition seeking full communion in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 9 years old, baptized Catholic and needs to prepare for First Holy Communion? Please call for information about RCIA Adapted for Older Children, designed to teach children who fall within these guidelines about our Catholic faith and to prepare them for the Sacraments of Initiation. Information and registration, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? It is not too late to join our Welcome Back program – a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The first session was last Monday, but you can still join us Monday, May 9 from 7:00-9:00 pm and then each Monday through August 1 in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

CAREGIVER BURNOUT. Our Health and Healing Ministry is brining Steve Emmer, MSW, Director of Care Consultation, ElderHealth Northwest, back for a second workshop on “Caregiver Burnout.” You will learn the signs of burnout, both subtle and not-sosubtle, as well as tips on how to handle and prevent it. There will be ample time for questions as well. Saturday, May 14, 2011, Pastoral Outreach Center, Holy Names Room, 10:00am-12 Noon. Information, John Simpson, 206-274-3173.
