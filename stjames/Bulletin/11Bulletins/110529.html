Easter VI

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are from the Impett family and Jack and Shirley Meredith, in loving memory of Jim Impett.

MEMORIAL DAY HOLIDAY. On Memorial Day, Monday, May 30, the Parish Office will be closed. There will be one Mass in the Cathedral at 9:00am. The Cathedral will remain open for prayer until 5:00pm.

FIRST FRIDAY. On Friday, June 3, Exposition of the Blessed Sacrament will follow the 8:15am Mass, and will conclude with a Holy Hour and Rosary from Noon-1:00pm.

PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

OUR NEW MENTAL HEALTH MINISTRY is sponsoring an information day, TODAY, Sunday May 29. Be sure to stop by our information table at all coffee hours. There will be information regarding the ministry, mental illness, as well as the patron saint of those suffering from mental illnesses, St. Dymphna. There will also be information regarding your call to become a volunteer. This is a good opportunity to meet our Parish Mental Health Nurse, Beth Rose, who will be available during these times to answer any questions. Information, Beth Rose, 206-382-4269.

SENIOR TEA RESCHEDULED. Our annual Senior Tea, originally scheduled for Thursday, June 2, has been rescheduled for Thursday, June 9. Invitations are being sent to all our Cathedral Parishioners 75 and older this week. Information, John Simpson, 206-274-3173.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. In honor of Mary and all the St. James mothers, the proceeds from the espresso cart will go to the Babysitting Program for the month of May.

YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT MISSION TRIP 2011. It is not too late for high school students to sign up for a week of service and fun as we work with migrant workers and their families. Opportunities include working with children in the daycare, and assisting at the food and clothing banks. Don’t miss out!! Application and information, Christine Henderson, 253-973-1441.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP will be Wednesday, June 8. Enjoy the wildlife and lush landscape of Northwest Trek as well as the company of God’s people! We’ll stop for lunch at a buffet too. Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

DO YOU HAVE A CHILD WHO IS: At least 7 years old seeking Baptism in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 7 years old baptized in another Christian tradition seeking full communion in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 9 years old, baptized Catholic and needs to prepare for First Holy Communion? Please call for information about RCIA Adapted for Older Children, designed to teach children who fall within these guidelines about our Catholic faith and to prepare them for the Sacraments of Initiation. Information and registration, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

CHOIR CAMP. St. James Cathedral's Youth Music Program is now registering students in grades 4-8 (treble voices) for our annual Choir Camp, August 15-21. This exciting week-long camp provides children training in music fundamentals, Orff instruments, and proper singing techniques; and fosters their active participation in the liturgy through music. For audition and general information, Music Office, 206-382-4874.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL. Join us Tuesday, June 14 for the return of this popular summer series in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm. Our first film, cosponsored by our ESL program, is entitled Innocent Voices—it is an inspirational coming-of-age story of screenwriter Oscar Torres, who, as an 11 year-old boy, was caught up in the El Salvador’s civil war. His saga is harrowing and intensely moving. The movie is principally about the effects of war on children (in Spanish with subtitles—rated R). Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

NEED PASTORAL CARE? The Cathedral provides a variety of such ministries. Whether you are sick or homebound and in need of communion, whether you would like a CD of Sunday’s Mass, or if you need the sacrament of the Anointing of the Sick, we can help! Information John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.
