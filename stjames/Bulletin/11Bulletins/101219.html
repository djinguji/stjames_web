Advent IV

December 19, 2010

Dear Friends,

When I came to you back in November to speak about Sacrificial Giving, I told you that I came with confidence, a confidence borne of my long experience of your generosity. My confidence was well-placed. Your response to my request to increase your level of giving in order to offset a serious budget deficit was nothing short of phenomenal. As of this writing, 466 of you have made a commitment to increase your giving this year, while 129 of you have made a pledge for the first time — with the result that our projected $375,000 deficit has been all but erased!

I want to take this opportunity to thank you for your incredibly generous response. I include in my thanks each one of you who has filled out a commitment card — not just those of you who were able to increase your giving. And I thank those, too, who indicated that they will support the work of the parish by their prayers.

As we approach the great feast of Christmas and are reminded of the incomparably great gift God has given to us in Jesus Christ, I want to tell you how deeply grateful I am for the gifts you share so generously with this wonderful parish.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET: Principles and Tools to Jump Start your Job Search. Beginning in January a two part series of classes will be offered during the coming year that will incorporate efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope. Part one, which will take place on the first Tuesday evening of each month, will focus on the principles and tools needed for effective job searching, including resume writing, interviewing techniques and networking. Part two, which will take place on the third Sunday morning of each month (following the 10:00 am Mass), will focus on the application of concepts learned in part one, including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions will be facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.
