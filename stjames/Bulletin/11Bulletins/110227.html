WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. During the month of February all proceeds will benefit the Homeless Ministry and Operation Nightwatch.

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. A DVD of the KING-TV broadcast of Midnight Mass, 2010, is now available in the Cathedral Bookstore—for a limited time! We also have the book Father Ryan mentioned in his homily last Sunday, I Will Not Hate by the “Gaza Doctor,” Izzeldin Abuelaish.

FIND OUT MORE ABOUT VOLUNTEER CHORE. Many people in our community require help with transportation, shopping, laundry, housework, and more. By volunteering just a few hours a month with Volunteer Chore Services, you can help someone stay in his or her own home, avoiding the need to move to a nursing home or other institutional setting. You’re invited to a Come and See Information Session TODAY, Sunday, February 27, 11:15-11:45am in the Mother Mary Rose Room, Pastoral Outreach Center.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET: Principles and Tools to Jump Start Your Job Search. Tuesday, March 1, 7-9 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Join us for a continuation of our ongoing series to support persons in the job market. This series incorporates efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions are facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. New people are always welcome. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. (This month’s topic: Interviewing Techniques, Part I.)

DO YOU NEED HELP WITH YOUR APPLICATION FOR U.S. CITIZENSHIP? On Saturday, March 5 on the first floor of the Pastoral Outreach Center, the ESL Program will be assisting people with their naturalization applications. If you, someone in your family, or a friend need help applying for U.S. citizenship, call to sign up for an appointment. When you call, you will get more information about the services we provide and find out what you will need to bring with you. If you are low-income and live in Seattle, the city will cover the application assistance fee. For all others, the application assistance costs $100. Information, Jenna Serghini, 206-382-4511.

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. For those who wish, babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

NEW TRANSLATION OF THE MASS

If you’d like to find out more about the new Roman Missal, mentioned in Father Ryan’s homily today, visit the the “Roman Missal” page on the Cathedral website. We will have many opportunities to learn about the new translations in the months to come.
