WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last week, we had visitors from Highlands Ranch, CO; Coquitlam, B.C.; Algueirao, Portugal, and many other places! The winner of our drawing was from Champlin, MN.

SUMMER TOURS. During the summer months, June, July, and August, we offer a tour of the Cathedral each Wednesday at 1:00pm. This is a great opportunity to share your Cathedral with out-of-town guests. No reservations are necessary; gather in the west vestibule.

DISASTER PREPAREDNESS information and handouts from the American Red Cross will be available at the coffee hours after today’s Masses. Only 7% of Americans have taken the recommended actions to prepare for disasters. If you’re in the remaining 93%, the American Red Cross urges you to make a plan and create a disaster supplies kit. Your family will cope best by preparing for earthquakes, floods or winter storms before they strike. Sponsored by the Health and Healing Ministry.

World-famous Lutheran musicians, conductor Anton Armstrong, organist John Ferguson, our own Cathedral Brass, and a nearly 200-voice Festival Chorus with congregational singing like you have seldom experienced. FIRST HILL NIGHT OUT BLOCK PARTY. Come meet our cathedral neighbors and have some fun! Dine on hot dogs and ice cream with the residents of Ozanam House, Cabrini Apartments, Chancery Place, and Skyline at First Hill this coming Tuesday, August 2 from 6:15 to 7:30pm. The 900 block of Columbia between 9th & Terry Avenues will be closed to traffic during this time. SUMMER CHOIR sings at the 12:00 Noon Sunday Mass during the month of August. It is open to anyone who wishes to “make a joyful noise” and who is able to rehearse just before the Mass. This spirit-filled, music-filled, prayer-filled opportunity is open to any and all singers: young and old; trained and untrained; small voice and big voice; in-tune and in-some-other-tune singer; travelers and locals; first time and long time—all are welcome! Rehearsals are Sundays of August, 11:15 am, at the Choir Room in Cathedral Place; enter up stairway on Terry Avenue.

The Western Baroque Music Festival faculty will present a concert featuring works by Telemann, Dornel, Chauvon, Hotteterre and Forqueray in conjuction with their 2011 workshop now held in Seattle. Information Cathedral music website. Advance passes are available from the Early Music Guild, 206-325-7066.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL—“GET LOW” Join us on Tuesday, August 9 for the third film of our summer series. This film tells the story of how Felix, a 40 year-old recluse, stages his own funeral during the Great Depression with the local funeral proprietor while he is still alive so he can hear what people say about him. It is a film about life, death, reconciliation and relationships with others. Starring Robert Duvall, Sissy Spacek and Bill Murray (Rated PG-13). Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

JUSTFAITH. Are you interested in exploring the social justice tradition of your faith? Do you want to know more about the spiritual journey of Francis of Assisi, Mother Theresa, Oscar Romero and the prophets? Did you participate in the Living the Gospel series and want to take those issues a step further? Consider taking part in JustFaith, an intensive program that explores Catholic Social Teaching, the church’s “best kept secret.” The program includes readings, videos, conversations about challenging topics, face-to-face encounters with the poor, and an investigation into the intersection of spirituality and Christian activism. JustFaith changes people; those people change the world! Information night Wednesday August 10, 6:30pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP. Friday, August 12, will take us on a beautiful adventure to Diablo Lake in the North Cascades. The all-day trip will be highlighted by a two and a half hour cruise aboard the Alice Ross III. We will explore the pristine waters of the reservoir ringed by snow-capped peaks, surrounded by rugged wilderness. Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

EMMAUS COMPANIONS are parishioners who are available during the weekend Masses and coffee hours to assist people who have come to the Cathedral in crisis or with special needs. We are looking for a few new people to join us in this important ministry of welcoming and presence. All that is needed is a listening ear and an open heart. Training and mentoring are provided. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Operation Nightwatch is an ecumenical Christian ministry providing hot meals and shelter referrals to 150-200 homeless people every night. St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner at the Nightwatch Center three times a month. Food donors are needed for the 1st and 3rd Tuesdays. If you would like to become a monthly donor of sandwich meats, cheeses, desserts, plastic bags, paper plates, and drink mixes, or if your business would like to donate regularly, please contact Mary Nichols, 206-420-8788.

CATHEDRAL CHOIR AUDITIONS. The Cathedral Choir is auditioning tenors with excellent sight-reading skills. Information, Stacey Sunde 206-941-9450.

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. For those who wish, babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

NEED PASTORAL CARE? Whether you are sick or homebound and in need of communion, or would like a CD of Sunday’s Mass, or need the sacrament of the Anointing of the Sick, we can help! Information John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Olaf in Poulsbo, St. Alphonsus in Seattle, and St. John Vianney in Kirkland, Vashon, and Darrington.
