Easter II

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass.

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. The Women of St. James Schola have released their first CD, Century of Chant. Stop in to get a copy (or two!) today. Also: Monks of Tibhirine, the story of the Algerian monks Father Ryan referenced in his homily on Easter Sunday, as well as titles by our Tre Ore speaker for this year, Father Paul Murray.

CHORAL MUSIC THIS SUNDAY 12:00 NOON. We welcome the Polish community's celebrated Vivat Musika! Conducted by Monika Stewert, the Polish members of the Cathedral Chamber Orchestra and the Radka family youth Psalmists. Sunday Evening we welcome the Forest Ridge School choir, Alison Seaton, director.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, May 11 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET. Join us on Tuesday, May 3 at 7:00 PM in Cathedral Place, second floor (803 Terry Ave.) This series incorporates efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions are facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. (This month’s topic: Cover Letters)

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? Our Welcome Back program provides a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are offered on Mondays, May 2 through August 1, 7-9pm on 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP will be Wednesday May 11. We will visit the Bremerton Naval Museum and explore the naval history of the region. In addition to the fascinating ship-building exhibits experience life as a sailor onboard one of the U.S. Navy's nuclear aircraft carriers. Explore where sailors sleep, what they eat, and how they spend their time. Please join us! Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners. Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

DO YOU HAVE A CHILD WHO IS: At least 7 years old seeking Baptism in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 7 years old baptized in another Christian tradition seeking full communion in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 9 years old, baptized Catholic and needs to prepare for First Holy Communion? Please call for information about RCIA Adapted for Older Children, designed to teach children who fall within these guidelines about our Catholic faith and to prepare them for the Sacraments of Initiation. Information and registration, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

CYO SUMMER CAMPS. The Catholic Youth Organization offers Residential Camps, Day Camps (with a new, fun theme each week!), and Adventure Trip for youth grades 1st through high school. At camp you can make new friends, try something new, sing by the campfire, and grow in your Catholic faith through prayer and the sacraments. CYO offers weekend camps for the whole family as well. Transportation and financial aid are available. This is your summer! Information and registration, www.camping.seattleoyyam.org.

CABRINI MINISTRY TRAINING offers quality training for lay ministers, including five months of weekly sessions with emphasis on pastoral skills, along with ethics, self-awareness, and more. CMT graduates minister in parishes, hospitals, nursing homes, and prisons; giving solace to the elderly, persons with special needs, the homebound, the homeless, the dying, and many more. Plan to attend an Information Meeting on Monday, May 9 at Cathedral Place (7-8:30pm). Information, Tom Curran at 206-760-0583 Ext 4.

NEW ON THE WEBSITE. Visit our Cathedral website to read Father Ryan’s homilies for Holy Thursday and Easter Sunday online. You can also view photo albums of the great days of Holy Week and Easter, find out about coming events, and much more!

HELP A NEIGHBOR IN NEED. Many people in our community need help with transportation, shopping, laundry, and housework. By volunteering just a few hours a month, you can help someone remain in his or her own home and avoid moving to an institutional setting. Training will be held Tuesday, May 3 at 7:00pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Brendan in Bothell, St. Philip in Woodland, and St. Joseph the Worker Mission in Yacolt.
