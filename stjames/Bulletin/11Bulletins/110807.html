SUMMER CHOIR. We are pleased to welcome members of the Chancel Choir of St. Charles Borromeo, Tacoma, Elly Racek, director, who are joining our Summer Choir today. Summer Choir sings at the 12:00 Noon Sunday Mass during the month of August and is open to anyone who wishes to “make a joyful noise” and who is able to rehearse just before the Mass. This spirit-filled, music-filled, prayer-filled opportunity is open to any and all singers: young and old; trained and untrained; small voice and big voice; in-tune and in-some-other-tune singer; visitors and parishioners; travelers and locals; first time and long time—all are welcome! Rehearsals are Sundays of August, 11:15 am, at the Choir Room in Cathedral Place; enter up stairway on Terry Avenue.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL—“GET LOW” Join us on Tuesday, August 9 for the third film of our summer series. This film tells the Depression-era story of Felix, a recluse who stages his own funeral while he is still alive so he can hear what people say about him. It is a film about life, death, reconciliation and relationships with others. Starring Robert Duvall, Sissy Spacek and Bill Murray (Rated PG-13). Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

JUSTFAITH. Are you interested in exploring the social justice tradition of your faith? Do you want to know more about the spiritual journey of Francis of Assisi, Mother Theresa, Oscar Romero and the prophets? Did you participate in the Living the Gospel series and want to take those issues a step further? Consider taking part in JustFaith, an intensive program that explores Catholic Social Teaching, the church’s “best kept secret.” The program includes readings, videos, conversations about challenging topics, face-to-face encounters with the poor, and an investigation into the intersection of spirituality and Christian activism. JustFaith changes people; those people change the world! Information night Wednesday August 10, 6:30pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Operation Nightwatch is an ecumenical Christian ministry providing hot meals and shelter referrals to 150-200 homeless people every night. St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner at the Nightwatch Center three times a month. Food donors are needed for the 1st and 3rd Tuesdays. If you would like to become a monthly donor of sandwich meats, cheeses, desserts, plastic bags, paper plates, and drink mixes, or if your business would like to donate regularly, please contact Mary Nichols, 206-420-8788.

ESL ADVISORY BOARD. If you want to make a difference in the lives of refugees and immigrants, and can commit to a few hours per month, we want you! The ESL advisory board advocates for immigration reform, raises funds, evaluates the program, helps plan, and provides general program oversight. We meet on the third Tuesday of each month from 6:00 to 7:30 PM. We ask for a commitment of two years. Information: Chris Koehler, (206) 382-4511.
