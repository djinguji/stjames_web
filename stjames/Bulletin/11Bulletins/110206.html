WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. During the month of February all proceeds will benefit the Homeless Ministry and Operation Nightwatch.

ST. JAMES ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE GROUP. Open Meeting Tuesday, February 8, 6:00 pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room, Pastoral Outreach Center. Our faith calls us to care for all of God’s creation. Do you feel that call? Do you want to learn more and get involved? Join the St. James Environmental Justice Group to learn about environmental activities at the parish, and to share your own ideas. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

All are welcome to attend the Mass. If you would like to receive the sacrament of Anointing of the Sick, or have questions, please contact John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister. Information 206-274-3173.

DISCOVERING THE AMAZING GIFT OF YOUR MARRIAGE. (A Two-Part Workshop) Saturdays, February 12 and April 2; 9:00-4:00 pm; Pastoral Outreach Center. Couples will learn effective skills in speaking and listening to one another, how to protect the relationship from negative words and behavior, learn a simple way to make decisions and solve problems, and fall in love all over again! Presenter: Dr. Robert Fontana, director of Catholic Life Ministries. $40/couple for both days, $25 for one day (scholarships available). Babysitting services available. To register please complete the form on parish website or in bulletin. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

CATHEDRAL KITCHEN JOB OPENING. The parish has an opening for a Kitchen Assistant in the Cathedral Kitchen. This is a part time position with a full benefits package. Information, Peggy Behnken at 206-382-4650.

CONFIRMATION CLASSES FOR ADULTS AND HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS 16 YEARS AND OLDER started on Thursday, February 3 and will continue on Thursdays 6:30-8:30pm in Cathedral Place Room 411 through April 28. It is not too late to join in the preparation classes for this important Sacrament. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

HELP A NEIGHBOR IN NEED. Many people in our community need help with transportation, shopping, laundry, and housework. Advanced age, chronic illness and financial difficulties can prevent our neighbors from accomplishing these routine daily tasks. By volunteering just a few hours a month with Volunteer Chore Services, you can help someone stay in his or her own home, avoiding the need to move to an institutional setting. To learn more about VCS, plan to attend a brief information session on Sunday, February 20 from 11:15 to 11:45am in the Mother Mary Rose Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center (907 Columbia). Information Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

“THE SCREWTAPE LETTERS” – A THEATRICAL PRODUCTION. Join other Cathedral parishioners on Saturday, February 26 at 8:00 pm at the Moore Theatre. One day only! JOURNEY THROUGH GRIEF. Are you seeking comfort after the death of a loved one? Join others who have also experienced loss in a prayerful supportive environment. In a six-week guided group process we will journey through our grief and gently embrace our mourning so that ultimately we may find healing. Information, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET: PRINCIPLES AND TOOLS TO JUMP START YOUR JOB SEARCH. During this year a two-part series of classes will be offered that will incorporate efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope. Part one, which takes place on the first Tuesday evening of each month from 7-9 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor (please note change in location) will focus on the principles and tools needed for effective job searching. Part two, which takes place on the third Sunday morning of each month from 11:30-1:00 pm in Gallagher Center, basement of the Rectory, will focus on the application of concepts learned in part one, including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions are facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. This month’s topic (February 1 and 20): Resumes and References.

CATHOLIC ADVOCACY DAY. Washington State faces massive budget cuts that imperil those in our community who are most in need. As people of faith, we must do all we can to protect the basic needs of families and individuals who are experiencing poverty and homelessness. All St. James parish members are invited to join Catholic social service and justice organizations Thursday, March 24, 9am–3pm at the State Capitol in Olympia to promote a state budget that safeguards the least among us. Following a policy briefing, participants will visit their local legislators for scheduled appointments. A free chartered bus for registered participants will depart from Seattle and Tacoma. To register, contact the Intercommunity Peace & Justice Center or 206-223-1138. Information Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Our Lady of Lourdes in Seattle and Vancouver, and the mission church of Our Lady of Lourdes in Wilkeson.
