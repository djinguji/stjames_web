Advent II

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

CHORAL MUSIC AT 10:00. This year during the Sundays of Advent, the Cathedral Choir and Soloists will be singing the Advent portion of Handel’s Messiah.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. During the month of December, all proceeds will benefit the 7th annual Advent sock drive for homeless men. If you are interested in becoming a Cathedral barista, please call Sean and Katherine Berry at 206-571-6868. Training is provided!

ADVENT FAIR TRADE SALE is here! for our annual fair trade/alternative gift sale to be held after the morning masses. Vendors will include Catholic Relief Services Work of Human Hands, L’Arche, and several of our own cathedral parishioners. This will be a great opportunity to purchase hand-made gifts for Christmas giving. (The Cathedral Bookstore is also open if you are looking for Cathedral Christmas cards!)

NEW ON THE WEBSITE. Visit the Cathedral website to see albums of some red-letter days in the history of the Archdiocese of Seattle—the installation of our new Archbishop, J. Peter Sartain. Find out about events for Advent, and much more!

CROSSING BORDERS. This winter, we will again be offering an eight-week course on Catholic social teaching on immigration. Through readings, discussion and prayer we will open ourselves to being informed, formed, and transformed on this topic. Weekly sessions will take place Monday, January 17 through Monday, March 7 from 6:30-9:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Please plan to attend all eight sessions. While there is no class fee, the cost for books and materials is $50. Scholarships are available. Registration with payment is required by Monday, December 13. Information and registration, Christopher Koehler at 206-382-45151.

Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

YOUNG ADULTS “WARM HEARTS AND SOLES” 7th annual Advent sock drive for homeless men continues today, Sunday, December 5. Your generosity is warming our hearts, but we still have a way to go to reach our goal of 2010 pairs for delivery in January to warm the soles of those in need. Let’s keep it up by dropping off new socks at the Young Adult table in Cathedral Hall after 8:00am, 10:00am, and 12:00 Sunday Masses, or during the week at 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Such a worthwhile project – let’s keep those hearts and soles warm this winter! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

HELP A NEIGHBOR IN NEED. Many people in our community need help with transportation, shopping, laundry, and housework. Advanced age, chronic illness and financial difficulties can prevent our neighbors from accomplishing these routine daily tasks. By volunteering just a few hours a month with Volunteer Chore Services (VCS), you can help someone stay in his or her own home, avoiding the need to move to a nursing home or other institutional setting. The next training for VCS volunteers is scheduled for Wednesday, January 19 at 6:15pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center (907 Columbia). Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.
