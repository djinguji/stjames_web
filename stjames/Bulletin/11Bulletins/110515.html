WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET. TODAY, May 15 from 11:30am-1:00 pm in Gallagher Center (lower level of rectory—Columbia St. entrance) will be the continuation of our ongoing series to support persons in the job market. This series incorporates efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions are facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

SHARE SAFE HAVEN SHELTER. We are pleased to welcome back our friends from the SHARE Safe Haven Shelter, who will once again be staying in Cathedral Hall each night this summer. Safe Haven is a self-governing group of approximately 25 homeless men and women whom we have had the privilege of hosting during previous summers when our Winter Shelter program is not in operation, and we are happy to have them with us again this year. Homelessness does not take a “summer vacation” and neither should our care and concern for our homeless brothers and sisters. Please hold the members of the Safe Haven Shelter in your prayers this summer.

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. For those who wish, babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session just began on Wednesday evenings at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

THE ST VINCENT DE PAUL CONFERENCES of St. James thank cathedral parishioners for their generous donations. During April we made a total of 66 home visits to our low income neighbors. Your contributions allowed us forestall the evictions of 45 families; help 4 households with their utilities; supply food to 36 people; help 2 people with emergency airfare; and provide 42 St. Vincent de Paul Thrift Store vouchers for clothes, beds, furniture, and other household items. We actively seek new members. Information, Rob Millar 206-954-8902 and Tammy Bendix 206-595-9158.
