SPECIAL COLLECTION FOR SOMALIA DROUGHT RELIEF. The United Nations has declared a famine in parts of Somalia, and an estimated 11 million people are facing severe food shortages in East Africa. A special collection will be taken up at the conclusion of all Masses this weekend to help fund the work of CRS in East Africa.

JUSTFAITH. Are you interested in exploring the social justice tradition of your faith? Do you want to know more about the spiritual journey of Francis of Assisi, Mother Theresa, Oscar Romero and the prophets? Did you participate in the Living the Gospel series and want to take those issues a step further? Consider taking part in JustFaith, an intensive program that explores Catholic Social Teaching, the church’s “best kept secret.” The program includes readings, videos, conversations about challenging topics, face-to-face encounters with the poor, and an investigation into the intersection of spirituality and Christian activism. There are still a few spots available in this year’s group. Information night Tuesday, September 6 at 6:30pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, September 14 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP will be Wednesday, September 14. We’ll tour cabins and replicas of a community from the 1880’s at The Pioneer Farm Museum. See examples of everyday chores and activities from over a century ago! Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

FIND OUT MORE ABOUT CABRINI MINISTRY TRAINING. Come to Cabrini Information Night, Monday, September 12, 7:00pm-8:30pm St. James Cathedral Place, Room 1E&W. The 20 weekly classes begin mid-October and run through mid-March. Information, Cabrini Ministry Training, 206-760-0583, ext 4, or visit the Cabrini Ministry website.

DO YOU HAVE A CHILD WHO IS: at least 7 years old seeking Baptism in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 7 years old baptized in another Christian tradition seeking full communion in the Catholic Church? Or, at least 9 years old, baptized Catholic and needs to prepare for First Holy Communion? Please call for information about RCIA Adapted for Older Children, designed to teach children who fall within these guidelines about our Catholic faith and to prepare them for the Sacraments of Initiation. Information and registration, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? Our Welcome Back program provides a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are offered on Mondays, September 12 through December 5, 7:00-9:00pm on 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Operation Nightwatch is an ecumenical Christian ministry providing hot meals and shelter referrals to 150-200 homeless people every night. St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner at the Nightwatch Center three times a month. We need food donors for the 1st and 3rd Tuesdays. Items we could use include the following: frozen meat for the main meal, sandwich meats, sliced cheese, bread, condiment packets. Leave your donations marked Operation Nightwatch in the Cathedral Hall kitchen after Masses on Sunday, or deliver them to the Parish Office before 4:00 pm on the 1st and 3rd Tuesdays. Information, Mary Nichols 206-420-8788.

REMEMBERING 9/11

MUSIC FOR 9/11. Next Sunday at the 10:00am Mass the Cathedral Choir and congregation will sing Herbolsheimer’s moving St. James Mass for Peace. This musical prayer, commissioned in 1982 for Archbishop Hunthausen, features hauntingly beautiful meditations for oboe solo as well as very familiar hymns and responses for the congregation. Information, Cathedral music website.

MUSLIMS FOR LIFE CAMPAIGN. The Ahmadiyya Muslim Community is working to collect 10,000 units of blood nationwide in the month of September in honor of those who lost their lives on 9/11. The campaign is based on the fundamental tenant of Islam upholding the sanctity of life. Donate blood at one of their many drives sponsored throughout the region. Visit Muslims for Life website for more information and Ahmadiyya website for locations.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Blessed Teresa of Calcutta in Woodinville, St. Rose of Viterbo in Longview, and St. John Chrysostom (Byzantine) in Seattle.
