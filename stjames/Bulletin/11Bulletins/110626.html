Corpus Christi

WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last week, we had visitors from Parker, AZ; Sutherland Springs, TX; Dayton, OH, and many other places! The winner of our drawing was from Flourtown, PA.

FLOWERS IN THE CATHEDRAL. Flowers at the altar are in loving memory of Joy Allan on the first anniversary of her death, from Peggy, Elizabeth, Anna, and Matthew Behnken. Flowers at the Shrine of the Blessed Virgin Mary in the north transept are from Jess and Elizabeth Hernandez in memory of Linda Condes on the second anniversary of her death. Flowers in the Cathedral are also from the Saturday weddings of Sonali Roy and Aaron Son, and Heidi Rise and Gerald Macheso.

PETER’S PENCE COLLECTION. This weekend we take up our annual collection to support the works and ministries of the Holy Father, Benedict XVI.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. During the month of June, all proceeds will go towards scholarships for the Schola Cantorum Choir Camp. Try our special drink of the month: the Jubi-latte! Please note that the espresso cart will be closed during the months of July and August.

SUMMER TOURS. During the summer months, June, July, and August, we offer a tour of the Cathedral each Wednesday at 1:00pm. This is a great opportunity to share your Cathedral with out-of-town guests. No reservations are necessary; gather in the west vestibule.

PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT MISSION TRIP 2011. It is not too late for high school students to sign up for a week of service and fun as we work with migrant workers and their families. Opportunities include working with children in the daycare, and assisting at the food and clothing banks. Don’t miss out!! Application and information, Christine Henderson, 253-973-1441.

Volunteer Opportunities at St. James

COFFEE HOUR HOSPITALITY NEEDS YOU! As we move into the warmer summer weather, we will again be offering hospitality “al fresco” in the Archbishop Murphy courtyard following the Sunday morning masses. But we need your help to make this happen! We are looking for several outgoing people to help with set-up, serving and clean-up for this very important ministry of hospitality. We especially need help following the 12:00 Mass. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

IS GOD CALLING YOU TO A MINISTRY AMONG CHILDREN, helping them to grow in their relationship with Jesus? The wonderful children in our parish need volunteers to help in Sunday school as catechists (teachers), assistants, a third supportive adult presence in class or as the welcoming presence at the doors. Information, Lita McBride, 206-654-4658, Marianne Coté, 206-382-4512, or Theresa Van de Ven, 206-219-5822.

EMMAUS COMPANIONS are parishioners who are available during the weekend Masses and coffee hours to assist people who have come to the Cathedral in crisis or with special needs. We are looking for a few new people to join us in this important ministry of welcoming and presence. All that is needed is a listening ear and an open heart. Training and mentoring are provided. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

VOLUNTEERS NEEDED FOR HOSPITALITY. Looking for a “few good people” to assist in setting up, greeting participants, and putting things away for our weekly RCIA gathering. The time commitment is once a month from 6:00-9:30pm. It is a great way to meet new people and support an important parish ministry. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL—“OF GODS AND MEN” Join us on Tuesday, July 12 in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm to watch this critically acclaimed film. It centers on the monastery of Tibhirine, where nine Trappist monks lived in harmony with the largely Muslim population of Algeria, until seven of them were kidnapped and assassinated in 1996 during the Algerian Civil War. (Rated R) This is the movie Father Ryan talked about in his homily on Easter Sunday. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

CONGRATULATIONS AND GOD’S BLESSINGS to Donna Fung and Remigio Isla, married in the Cathedral on Saturday, June 18. Please keep them in your prayers as they begin their new life together.

CHOIR CAMP. St. James Cathedral's Youth Music Program is now registering students in grades 4-8 (treble voices) for our annual Choir Camp, August 15-21. This exciting week-long camp provides children training in music fundamentals, Orff instruments, and proper singing techniques; and fosters their active participation in the liturgy through music. For audition and general information, Music Office, 206-382-4874.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, July 13 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.
