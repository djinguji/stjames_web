WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

PARISH OFFICE CLOSED. On Monday, January 3, 2011, the Parish Office will be closed in observance of New Year’s Day. Masses in the Cathedral will follow the usual weekday schedule.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? Our Welcome Back program provides a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are offered on Mondays, January 3 through April 4, 7:00-9:00pm on 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. During the month of January, all proceeds will benefit the Cathedral’s Youth Music Program. If you are interested in becoming a Cathedral barista, please call Sean and Katherine Berry at 206-571-6868. Training is provided!

CATHEDRAL CHOIR OPENING. We have an opening for a tenor and a low bass in the Cathedral Choir. If you have a love for the liturgy, strong sight-reading skills and good intonation, and time to participate with our famous choir, please contact Stacey Sunde at 206-382-4874.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin on Wednesday, January 12. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. You can also check out our website.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET: PRINCIPLES AND TOOLS TO JUMP START YOUR JOB SEARCH. Beginning in January, a two-part series of classes will be offered during the coming year that will incorporate efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope. Part one, which will take place on the first Tuesday evening of each month, will focus on the principles and tools needed for effective job searching, including resume writing, interviewing techniques and networking. Part two, which will take place on the third Sunday morning of each month (following the 10:00 am Mass), will focus on the application of concepts learned in part one, including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions will be facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

ANNUAL MASS AND MARCH FOR LIFE. Please join Archbishop J. Peter Sartain in celebration of the Annual Mass for Life, Tuesday, January 18 at 10:00am at St. Michael Parish in Olympia. Following Mass, participants will process to the Capitol steps where they will call on their legislators and elected officials to work for public policies that protect all human life. Two cathedral vans will be available for parishioners who would like a ride to this event. Information: Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

TO HUNGER FOR GOD. Join us on Thursday, January 27 from 7:00-8:30 pm in Cathedral Hall as Father Mike Raschko (Associate Professor at the School of Theology and Ministry at SU, Theologian for the Archdiocese, and parish priest) returns to talk about his most recently published book, A Christian Understanding of Human Nature: To Hunger For God, describing the relationship between God and the human person. He will also be available for book signings. As many of you know, he is a dynamic speaker who can translate spiritual and theological ideas into meaningful and practical concepts for our own faith lives. Suggested donation: $10. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

FROM SOUND TO SILENCE: THE SPIRITUALITY AND PRACTICE OF CENTERING PRAYER. Are you seeking calm in the midst of the busyness of your life? Would you like to give your prayer life a “jump-start” before the Lenten season? If so, join Sr. Joyce Cox, BVM, (Formation and Spiritual Director of the Palisades Retreat Center for the Archdiocese of Seattle; Cathedral parishioner) as she shares her wisdom and expertise in the ancient practice of centering prayer. Saturdays, February 5, 12, 19 & 26, from 10am-Noon in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Whether you are a beginner or regular practitioner, there will be something new to learn and experience. Suggested donation $20/series. Information: Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

ARE YOU LOOKING FOR A WAY TO HELP OTHERS AS A NEW YEAR BEGINS WHILE ALSO LEARNING MORE ABOUT YOUR CATHOLIC FAITH? Consider being a sponsor for one of our many new inquirers coming to participate in our Christian Initiation process to learn more about the Catholic Church (RCIA). We need St. James parishioners to be companions with our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. All training and support will be provided and the time commitment is reasonable—a minimum of 2 sessions a month (Wednesday evenings). To learn more, come to our Sponsor Information meeting on Wednesday, January 12 from 7:00-9:00 pm at Cathedral Place, second floor. The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, Helen Oesterle at 206-382-4514.

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. Please note that babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarship money). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct debit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. Please drop the completed card in the collection basket or put it in the mail. If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.

PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30 pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

ARCHBISHOP HUNTHAUSEN LIBRARY. Visit your friendly and accessible Archbishop Hunthausen Library in Cathedral Hall during the Sunday coffee hours. We have a good selection of old favorites and recent releases.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Elizabeth Ann Seton in Bothell, and St. Mary in Castle Rock and Centralia.
