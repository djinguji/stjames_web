JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Beginning this week, those preparing to be baptized at the Easter Vigil (our newly Elect) are wearing brown or red robes while attending Mass and other liturgies in the cathedral. This helps them to know, in a concrete way, that they have entered a new and significant stage in their final preparations for Easter. It also anticipates another significant moment that will take place when they emerge from the baptismal waters at the Easter Vigil and change into white robes. Seek them out in our midst and, if possible, introduce yourself to them after Mass. Let them know you are praying for them and looking forward to Easter when they will join us at the Eucharistic table.

LENTEN PRAYER PARTNERS FOR THE ELECT. The Church tells us that it is the entire parish’s responsibility to support and welcome those preparing for baptism this Easter. One of the many ways you can do this is to become a prayer partner for one of our Elect. You are asked to pray everyday for him/her during Lent, asking for God’s blessings and guidance. Also, notes and cards will be collected during Holy Week to be given to the Elect before Easter. Prayer cards are available at the place of prayer located in the north aisle of the west nave of the Cathedral (where you can also learn more about each of them in our Book of the Elect), in the bookstore, and in Cathedral Hall during Sunday morning hospitality. You can also meet our Elect on our parish website. Your prayers make a difference!!! Information Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

As we enter the desert of Lent together, Jesus shows us that to live fully the life God has given us, we must obey the Commandments and follow the Holy Spirit. Just as we are called to care for the gift of our eternal life, we are also called to care for the gift of God’s creation. Catholic Relief Services’ Operation Rice Bowl supports agriculture programs in Haiti and around the world that teach farmers effective and ecologically sensitive growing methods which allow them to get the most out of their land while preserving it for future generations. For more information on Haiti, refer to your Rice Bowl calendar or visit CRS online.
