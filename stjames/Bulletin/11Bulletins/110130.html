WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS IN THE CATHEDRAL are from Aurora Turla in memory of Jose Turla, on the fourteenth anniversary of his death.

ORGAN RECITAL CHANGED. Due to health issues Ruth Draper has postponed her recital to later in the spring; however, she will be here on Monday, January 31 at 7:30pm for a short demonstration of the famous organs of St. James and then she plans to play some amazing works for feet alone! Visitors will be invited to sit in the East Apse to be able to see and hear easily. There is absolutely no charge for this musical opportunity. Information, 206-382-4874.

FEAST OF THE PRESENTATION AND BLESSING OF CANDLES. On February 2, the feast of the Presentation, the Church blesses candles for use in the liturgy during the coming year. You are invited to bring your own candles from home for the blessing, which takes place during the Masses at 8:15am and 5:30pm. The solemn celebration with music will be at 5:30pm on Wednesday, February 2.

BLESSING OF THROATS ON THE FEAST OF ST. BLASE. St. Blase is credited with saving the life of a boy who was choking to death on a fishbone. Each year on his feast day, February 3, the Church observes the traditional blessing of throats. The blessing takes place in the context of the regularly scheduled Masses, 8:15am and 5:30pm. The blessing will also be offered following the Office of Midday Prayer at 12:10pm in the Chapel.

FIRST FRIDAY EXPOSITION AND HOLY HOUR. On Friday, February 4, Exposition of the Blessed Sacrament will begin following the 8:15am Mass, and continue until 1:00pm. Holy Hour, with songs, readings, prayers, and rosary, will begin at 12 Noon. All are welcome to join in all or part of this beautiful prayer in the presence of the Blessed Sacrament.

CONFIRMATION CLASSES FOR ADULTS AND HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS 16 YEARS AND OLDER begin Thursday, February 3, 6:30-8:30 pm in Cathedral Place Room 411. Full sacramental initiation (Baptism, Confirmation, and Eucharist) is important for all practicing Catholics, and is particularly important for those who are considering getting married. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET: PRINCIPLES AND TOOLS TO JUMP START YOUR JOB SEARCH. During this year a two-part series of classes will be offered that will incorporate efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope. Part one, which takes place on the first Tuesday evening of each month from 7-9 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor (please note change in location) will focus on the principles and tools needed for effective job searching. Part two, which takes place on the third Sunday morning of each month from 11:30-1:00 pm in Gallagher Center, basement of the Rectory, will focus on the application of concepts learned in part one, including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions are facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. This month’s topic (February 1 and 20): Resumes and References.

YOUTH MUSIC FESTIVAL. On Saturday February 5, young musicians will gather at St. James for a day-long festival of music making. Participants include St. James Cathedral, Plymouth Congregational Church, St. Stephen’s Episcopal Church, St. Mark’s Cathedral, Moorlands Elementary, Cross of Christ Lutheran Church, with Cathedral Youth Artist Award winner Lana Dziekonski, violist, and Cathedral Organ Scholar Thomas Varas. There will be a free concert at 1:00 pm in the Cathedral. All are invited to attend. Information, 206-382-4874.

FROM SOUND TO SILENCE: THE SPIRITUALITY AND PRACTICE OF CENTERING PRAYER. Would you like to give your prayer life a “jump-start” before the Lenten season? If so, join Sister Joyce Cox, BVM, (Formation and Spiritual Director of the Palisades Retreat Center for the Archdiocese of Seattle and Cathedral parishioner) as she shares her wisdom and expertise in the ancient practice of centering prayer. Saturdays, February 5, 12, 19 and 26, from 10:00am-Noon in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Suggested donation $20/series. Information: Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

CELEBRATING CATHOLIC SCHOOLS—THE RAINBOW SCHOOLS COALITION. On Sunday, February 6 we will celebrate Catholic School education, and in a particular way the Cathedral children who attend one of the five Rainbow Schools. Our parish is a contributing member of the Rainbow Schools Program whereby children from our parish can attend at “parishioner” tuition rates. Approximately 60 cathedral children are enrolled in one of these fine schools. Representatives of Sts. Edward, George, Paul, Joseph and Therese Catholic Schools will be available after the 10:00 and 12:00 Masses during hospitality. Children attending the Rainbow Schools and other Catholic Schools are encouraged to wear their uniforms on February 6! Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Thomas Aquinas in Camas and St. John Bosco in Tacoma.
