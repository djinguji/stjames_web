September 18, 2011

Dear Friends,

I have some important news relating to our cathedral staff. When I first began thinking about how I was going to tell you this, I had a pretty heavy heart, but that is no longer true, thank God!

Helen Oesterle, our Director of RCIA and Adult Faith Formation, has been offered the position of Pastoral Associate at Our Lady of Guadalupe Parish in West Seattle and she has accepted. It’s a wonderful opportunity for her because it is close to home for her and almost across the street from Mount St. Vincent’s where her Dad is living. It’s hard not to see the hand of God at work in this.

It’s equally hard not to see the hand of God at work in the second part of what I have to share with you. I have asked TerryAnn Bowen who for the past five years has ably led our Welcome Back program, children’s RCIA, and confirmation program, to step into Helen’s shoes and she has happily and very enthusiastically agreed.

Needless to say, it will be very difficult to let Helen go after all her years with us! She has done an amazing job and touched more lives than any of us can count. She has not only directed our RCIA and adult faith formation programs, she has prepared hundreds of families for the celebration of the sacrament of baptism and the sacrament of matrimony. She has led Bible Study, Catholic 101, our Summer Film Festival—and so much more. And her energy and enthusiasm for her ministry have been contagious. In fact, I think it’s safe to say that each one of us has ‘caught’ Helen’s ‘fever’ and is the better for it! It won’t be at all easy to see her go. At the same time, I am very happy that this opportunity has come her way because it seems the right fit at the right time.

For now, please join me in thanking God for opening up a wonderful new possibility for Helen and for sending TerryAnn to us. Later this fall, we’ll have a chance to celebrate Helen’s ministry and to thank her for her service.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session began Wednesday, September 14 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates. In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct debit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. If you are not sure whether or not you’re registered, call the Parish Office, 206-622-3559.

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We welcome all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. For those who wish, babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.
