HOW SWEET IT IS: NEWS FROM THE CATHEDRAL KITCHEN. What do you do with 1,000 pounds of candy? On Tuesday evenings, Matt Secrest, a sixth grade teacher at Holy Rosary School in West Seattle, comes to Cathedral Kitchen to volunteer his dishwashing expertise. A couple of weeks ago he asked if we would be interested in receiving Halloween candy that the students would donate after Trick or Treating. We said “Great”—thinking that he would be bringing a couple of bags and that we could use the candy in our lunch bags as an extra sweet. Much to our surprise, we received bags and boxes of candy and more candy. Matt said that it all weighed in at over 1,000 pounds—a blessing, to say the least! We have been able to include the candy not only in our lunch bags and on our dessert trays, but also have shared these treats with the Winter Shelter, the Cathedral Youth Music Program, Children’s Faith Formation, the Frederic Ozanam House, Chancery Place, and St. Mary’s Food Bank. Thank you to all the students at Holy Rosary School for their partnership in sharing.
