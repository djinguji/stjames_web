Easter V

May 22, 2011

Dear Friends,

Back in 1981, my predecessor as pastor, Father William Gallagher, had the wisdom and enlightened good sense to hire Dr. James Savage as the Cathedral’s music director. His decision turned out to be a profoundly important one. We are still experiencing its effects today.

When Jim arrived here, the Cathedral was, in many respects, a far different place than it is today. It looked very different (on the inside!), and the parish that gathered here was very different: only about a third of the size it now is and made up mostly of people living in the neighborhood. Children were hard to find. One of the only constants throughout those 30 years has been Jim Savage himself!

Thirty years is a long time. It’s nearly a third of the Cathedral’s history. It’s also time enough to create a legacy, and Jim has surely done that. Thanks to his leadership, the music program of St. James Cathedral has come to be recognized throughout this country as both superior and superb. And when I say that I include the entire music program, all of it world class: the organists, the Cathedral Choir of St. James, the Women of St. James Schola, the Cathedral Cantorei, the Cathedral Chamber Orchestra, and the Cathedral’s three children’s choirs (Schola Cantorum, St. Gregory Choir, St. Cecilia Singers). When Jim arrived on the scene, there was an organist and a rather small choir — a music program at a fairly low ebb even though there had been some high points in its history.

But it’s not just the music program that has changed over these years. The Cathedral building itself has changed and Jim played an important role in all those changes, serving as my trusted advisor on nearly every artistic and liturgical judgment that was made during the great renovation and restoration of 1994. In the Catholic Church, liturgy and music and the arts are not separate entities: they are intricately and inseparably woven together in the great fabric that is Catholic worship. With his considerable gifts, Jim was very instrumental in making sure that each of those essential elements played its proper part in that great undertaking.

Thirty years is a long time, but the mere passage of time is no guarantee of progress, growth, or success. In Jim’s case, however, 30 years has meant the growth of something truly wonderful and the blossoming of something beyond beautiful. Sunday after Sunday when we come together, each of us is a part of that beautiful something as we sing together the praises of our God in our magnificent Cathedral. And as we do – and especially so on Jim’s thirtieth anniversary weekend – I hope we will stop to give thanks to God for sending Jim Savage to St. James, thanks, too, to Jim for using his amazing gifts to help St. James become the wonderful praying, singing, and serving community it is!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. For those who wish, babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

SHARE SAFE HAVEN SHELTER. We are pleased to welcome back our friends from the SHARE Safe Haven Shelter, who will once again be staying in Cathedral Hall each night this summer. Safe Haven is a self-governing group of approximately 25 homeless men and women whom we have had the privilege of hosting during previous summers when our Winter Shelter program is not in operation, and we are happy to have them with us again this year. Homelessness does not take a “summer vacation” and neither should our care and concern for our homeless brothers and sisters. Please hold the members of the Safe Haven Shelter in your prayers this summer.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates. In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct debit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. If you are not sure whether or not you’re registered, call the Parish Office, 206-622-3559.
