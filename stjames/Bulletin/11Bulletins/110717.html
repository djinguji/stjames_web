Bulletin Archive

 

WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last week, we had visitors from Indian Trail, NC; Chandler, AZ; Abuja, Nigeria; and many other places! The winner of our drawing was from Dubuque, IA.

SUMMER TOURS. During the summer months, June, July, and August, we offer a tour of the Cathedral each Wednesday at 1:00pm. This is a great opportunity to share your Cathedral with out-of-town guests. No reservations are necessary; gather in the west vestibule.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session began Wednesday, July 13 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN THE CAMINO DE SANTIAGO? The Puget Sound Chapter of American Pilgrims on the Camino will hold its 3rd annual Saint James Day Walk, Mass and Potluck, Saturday, July 23. Experienced and aspiring pilgrims are invited to meet at St. Mark’s Episcopal Cathedral at 2:30 for a scenic walk to St James Cathedral for 5:30 Mass, then on to a potluck dinner. Information, Martha Crites.

TALLIS SCHOLARS SUMMER SESSION EVENTS. Members of the world-famous Tallis Scholars return to St. James for three exciting events: Sunday, July 24, 8:00 pm, Tudor Choir directed by Peter Phillips and David Willcocks; Wednesday, July 27, 5:30 pm Mass, Renaissance Polyphony, Peter Phillips, conductor, Father Ryan, Presider; Friday, July 29, 8:00 pm, concert featuring Thomas Tallis’ monumental 40-voice Spem in alium for eight 5-voice choirs. The Wednesday, July 27, 3:15 pm Organ Recital has been cancelled. Information Cathedral music website.

SUMMER CHOIR sings at the 12:00 Noon Sunday Mass during the month of August. It is open to anyone who wishes to “make a joyful noise”and who is able to rehearse just before the Mass. This spirit-filled, music-filled, prayer-filled opportunity is open to any and all singers: young and old; trained and untrained; small voice and big voice; in-tune and in-some-other-tune singer; travelers and locals; first time and long time—all are welcome! Rehearsals are Sundays of August, 11:15 am, at the Choir Room in Cathedral Place; enter up stairway on Terry Avenue.

Information Cathedral music website.

I AM IN YOUR MIDST
AS ONE WHO SERVES
Volunteer Opportunities at St. James

IS GOD CALLING YOU TO A MINISTRY AMONG CHILDREN, helping them to grow in their relationship with Jesus? The wonderful children in our parish need volunteers to help in Sunday school as catechists (teachers), assistants, a third supportive adult presence in class or as the welcoming presence at the doors. Information, Lita McBride, 206-654-4658, Marianne Coté, 206-382-4512, or Theresa Van de Ven, 206-219-5822.

EMMAUS COMPANIONS are parishioners who are available during the weekend Masses and coffee hours to assist people who have come to the Cathedral in crisis or with special needs. We are looking for a few new people to join us in this important ministry of welcoming and presence. All that is needed is a listening ear and an open heart. Training and mentoring are provided. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Operation Nightwatch is an ecumenical Christian ministry serving people on the streets. St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner to over 150 homeless people at the Nightwatch Dispatch Center near 14th and Jackson on the first and third Tuesdays of every month. Nightwatch recently asked the cathedral for help on the second Sunday of the month as well. Please consider making a monthly commitment from 8:30 to 10:30 pm on one of these three nights. Food donations are also needed. Information, Mary Nichols, 206-420-8788.

CATHEDRAL CHOIR AUDITIONS. The Cathedral Choir is auditioning tenors with excellent sight-reading skills. Information, Stacey Sunde 206-941-9450.

WESTERN BAROQUE FESTIVAL CONCERT. Sunday, August 7, 8:00 pm. The Western Baroque Music Festival faculty will present a concert featuring works by Telemann, Dornel, Chauvon, Hotteterre and Forqueray in conjuction with their 2011 workshop now held in Seattle. Information Cathedral music website. Advance passes are available from the Early Music Guild, 206-325-7066.

ARE YOU A GRADUATE OF CATHEDRAL SCHOOL? A reunion picnic for the Cathedral School Class of 1961 will be held Saturday afternoon, August 20, 2011. Information, Jocelyn Phillips, 206-524-2950.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Ann in Seattle, Tacoma, and Forks; St. Mary Magdalene in Everett, and the Carmelite Monastery of St. Joseph in Shoreline.
