WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

YOUNG ADULTS (21 - 30-SOMETHING) 3RD SUNDAY. Scripture sharing and informal social time is today, Sunday, January 17 starting at 4:00pm. Do you ever wonder how the Sunday readings relate to our everyday lives? Enjoy fellowship, wine, and cheese from 4:00 to 4:30 followed by sharing how the Scripture readings for the day speak to you in your everyday life. The study will finish in time to attend 5:30 Mass. Young adults over the age of 21, single or married, gather in the Fireside Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. Newcomers and those not new to the parish are all welcome! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

FINDING HOPE IN A TOPSY-TURVY JOB MARKET: Principles and Tools to Jump Start Your Job Search. This Sunday, January 16, from 11:30-1:00 in Gallagher Center (basement of parish rectory—access on Columbia Street) is part two of our monthly job support series which incorporates efforts to search for a job within a context of faith and hope. Today’s workshop will focus on the application of concepts learned in part one, including sharing strategies and struggles with real life experiences of looking for a job. Sessions are facilitated by parishioners who are professionals in this field. New people are welcome. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

PRAYER FOR LIFE

ANNUAL MASS AND MARCH FOR LIFE. Tuesday, January 18 at 10:00am at St. Michael's, Olympia. Please join Archbishop J. Peter Sartain in celebration of the Annual Mass for Life, Tuesday, January 18 at 10:00am at St. Michael Parish in Olympia. Following Mass, participants will process to the Capitol steps where they will call on their legislators and elected officials to work for public policies that protect all human life. Two cathedral vans will be available for parishioners who would like a ride to this event. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

MASS FOR LIFE. Friday, January 21 at 5:30pm. We gather on the eve of the anniversary of the Supreme Court decision Roe v. Wade to pray for an end to abortion and a greater respect for all human life, from conception to natural death. Father Ryan will preside.

DAY OF PENANCE. Saturday, January 22. The United States Bishops have declared January 22, the anniversary of the Supreme Court decision Roe v. Wade, to be a Day of Penance for violations to the dignity of the human person committed through acts of abortion, and prayer for the full restoration of the legal guarantee of the right to life. Mass for the Day of Penance will be at 8:15am, followed by a Rosary for Life. All are welcome.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session just began last Wednesday evening and you are most welcome to join us. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. You can also check out our website.

2,239!!! IS THE FINAL COUNT OF PAIRS OF SOCKS donated to the Young Adults 2010 Warm Hearts and Soles Advent sock drive for homeless men. Many, many thanks to our generous parishioners who helped us to surpass our goal of 2,010 pairs. In addition to the men’s socks there were also donations of men’s underwear, gloves, stocking hats, scarves, thermal shirts, jackets, and women’s socks. All have gone to organizations we work with to be distributed to those in need. And many thanks, too, to our young adults who organize and sponsor the drive, and donate their time to collect the socks following the Advent Masses.

FROM SOUND TO SILENCE: THE SPIRITUALITY AND PRACTICE OF CENTERING PRAYER. Are you seeking calm in the midst of the busyness of your life? Would you like to give your prayer life a “jump-start” before the Lenten season? If so, join Sr. Joyce Cox, BVM, (Formation and Spiritual Director of the Palisades Retreat Center for the Archdiocese of Seattle; Cathedral parishioner) as she shares her wisdom and expertise in the ancient practice of centering prayer. Saturdays, February 5, 12, 19 & 26, from 10am-Noon in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Whether you are a beginner or regular practitioner, there will be something new to learn and experience. Suggested donation $20/series. Information: Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

FINDING GOD IN ALL THINGS. (A retreat offering from The Palisades Retreat Center) February 4-6, 2011. During this retreat, we will consider some of the ordinary ways we can find God: in nature, in music, in poetry, in our relationships and in the struggles of our daily lives. This faith sharing retreat for women will be led by Fr. John Fuchs, S.J. and Pam McCauley. Information, www.seattlearchdiocese.org/palisades or 1-800-330-7622.

FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH SERIES—THE TRINITY. Sunday, January 23, 1:30-5:30 pm at St. Peter’s Parish Hall in Beacon Hill. This class will discuss Jesus as the central reality of the Church’s faith and how the doctrine of the Trinity flows from that faith. This is a wonderful opportunity for an in-depth look at Catholic teaching and its implications for our everyday lives. Cost: $5 per class and new people always welcome. (Co-sponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Mary in Aberdeen, Seattle, and Seaview; St. Mary Mission in Coupeville; and Sacred Heart in Battle Ground and Enumclaw.
