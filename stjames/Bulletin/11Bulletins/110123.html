WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FROM SOUND TO SILENCE: THE SPIRITUALITY AND PRACTICE OF CENTERING PRAYER. Are you seeking calm in the midst of the busyness of your life? Would you like to give your prayer life a “jump-start” before the Lenten season? If so, join Sr. Joyce Cox, BVM, (Formation and Spiritual Director of the Palisades Retreat Center for the Archdiocese of Seattle; Cathedral parishioner) as she shares her wisdom and expertise in the ancient practice of centering prayer. Saturdays, February 5, 12, 19, and 26, from 10am-Noon in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Whether you are a beginner or regular practitioner, there will be something new to learn and experience. Suggested donation $20/series. Information: Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

FOUNDATIONS IN FAITH SERIES—THE TRINITY. TODAY, Sunday, January 23, 1:30-5:30 pm at St. Peter’s Parish Hall in Beacon Hill. This class will discuss Jesus as the central reality of the Church’s faith and how the doctrine of the Trinity flows from that faith. We will explore our belief in the Trinity logically, historically and inspirationally while defining terms such as “theology,” “Incarnation,” and “economy of salvation.” A discussion of the relationship of our Triune God will lead to a deeper understanding of God’s relationship to us and ours to each Person of the Trinity. This is a wonderful opportunity for an in-depth look at Catholic teaching and its implications for our everyday lives. Cost: $5 per class and new people always welcome. (Co-sponsored by St. James Cathedral with the South Seattle Deanery) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514. (Next class: The History of the Church in US on March 6)

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. Please note that babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Paul in LaConner and Seattle, and St. Paul Mission in Westport.
