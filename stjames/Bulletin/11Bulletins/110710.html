WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last week, we had visitors from McKinney, TX; Duluth, GA; Cardiff, United Kingdom, and many other places! The winner of our drawing was from Flemington, NJ.

SUMMER TOURS. During the summer months, June, July, and August, we offer a tour of the Cathedral each Wednesday at 1:00pm. This is a great opportunity to share your Cathedral with out-of-town guests. No reservations are necessary; gather in the west vestibule.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL—“OF GODS AND MEN” Join us on Tuesday, July 12 in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm to watch this critically acclaimed film. It centers on the monastery of Tibhirine, where nine Trappist monks lived in harmony with the largely Muslim population of Algeria, until seven of them were kidnapped and assassinated in 1996 during the Algerian Civil War. (Rated R) This is the movie Father Ryan talked about in his homily on Easter Sunday. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, July 13 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place leave a message with John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

THE ST VINCENT DE PAUL CONFERENCES of St. James thank cathedral parishioners for their generous donations last month. During June, we made 50 home visits to our low income neighbors and continued to focus on preventing homelessness. Your contributions made it possible for us to help 33 families and individuals stay in their homes, and 15 households avoid utility shot-offs, In addition, we provided food and furniture to 20 households, and supplied 10 St. Vincent de Paul Thrift Store vouchers for housewares and clothing. Members of the St. Vincent de Paul Society follow Jesus through service to those in need. Bearing witness to Christ’s compassionate and liberating love, we show our commitment through person-to-person contact. Home visits are always made in pairs. As calls for help increase, we actively seek new members. Please consider joining this beautiful ministry to the poor among us. Information, Rob Millar 206-954-8902 and Tammy Bendix 206-595-9158.

SUMMER CHOIR sings at the 12:00 Noon Sunday Mass during the month of August. It is open to anyone who wishes to “make a joyful noise”and who is able to rehearse just before the Mass. This spirit-filled, music-filled, prayer-filled opportunity is open to any and all singers: young and old; trained and untrained; small voice and big voice; in-tune and in-some-other-tune singer; visitors and parishioners; travelers and locals; first time and long time—all are welcome! Rehearsals are Sundays of August, 11:15 am, at the Choir Room in Cathedral Place; enter up stairway on Terry Avenue.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Operation Nightwatch is an ecumenical Christian ministry serving people on the streets. St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner to over 150 homeless people at the Nightwatch Dispatch Center near 14th and Jackson on the first and third Tuesdays of every month. Nightwatch recently asked the cathedral for help on the second Sunday of the month as well. Please consider making a monthly commitment from 8:30 to 10:30 pm on one of these three nights. Food donations are also needed. Information, Mary Nichols, 206-420-8788.
