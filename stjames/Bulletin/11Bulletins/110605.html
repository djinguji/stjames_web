WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last week, we had visitors from Rensselaer, NY; Montrose, CO; Guadalajara, Mexico, and many other places! The winner of our drawing was from Honolulu, Hawaii.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are from Emma Deuna Lopez in loving memory of Armando E. Deuna and Peregrina L. Gambalan on the anniversary of their deaths. Floral offerings are a wonderful way to remember a deceased loved one, celebrate an anniversary, or give thanks for a prayer that has been answered. If you have been inspired to give thanks with flowers, please contact Peggy Behnken at 206-654-4650.

GUEST CHOIR SATURDAY 5:30. We welcome the choir of Holy Trinity Lutheran Church, Mercer Island, directed by Chrade Younkin, with organist David Locke.

PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

SENIOR TEA. Our annual Senior Tea will take place on Thursday, June 9. Invitations were sent to all our Cathedral Parishioners 75 and older this week. If you did not receive your invitation, but would like to attend, contact John Simpson, 206-274-3173.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL. Join us Tuesday, June 14 for the return of this popular summer series in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm. Our first film, co-sponsored by our ESL program, is entitled Innocent Voices—it is an inspirational coming-of-age story of screenwriter Oscar Torres, who, as an 11-year-old boy, was caught up in the El Salvador’s civil war. His saga is harrowing and intensely moving. The movie is principally about the effects of war on children (in Spanish with subtitles—rated R). Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT MISSION TRIP 2011. It is not too late for high school students to sign up for a week of service and fun as we work with migrant workers and their families. Opportunities include working with children in the daycare, and assisting at the food and clothing banks. Don’t miss out!! Application and information, Christine Henderson, 253-973-1441.

Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, at 206-274-3173.

VAN DRIVER NEEDED. Want to make someone’s day? Help our senior parishioners attend Mass by driving one of our vans one Sunday per month. Information John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

REACH OUT TO SOMEONE SUFFERING FROM HIV/AIDS, MS OR ADDICTION. People struggling to recover from illness need more than a good doctor. They need the care and concern of a supportive community. St. James parishioners work in partnership with the Rosehedge Multifaith Works to form CareTeams that offer practical and emotional support to people isolated by their illness and cut off from the community. CareTeam training will be held Saturday, June 18, 8:45 am to 4:45 pm at the Rosehedge Multifaith Works Office, 115 – 16th Avenue, Seattle. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

NEED PASTORAL CARE? Whether you are sick or homebound and in need of communion, or would like a CD of Sunday’s Mass, or need the sacrament of the Anointing of the Sick, we can help! Information John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

SUMMER TOURS. During the summer months, June, July, and August, we offer a tour of the Cathedral each Wednesday at 1:00pm. This is a great opportunity to share your Cathedral with out-of-town guests. No reservations are necessary; gather in the west vestibule.

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. For those who wish, babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

LISTENING ASSISTANCE DEVICES. If you have trouble hearing the spoken word in the Cathedral, ask an usher about using one of our listening assistance devices, which provide clear and clean sound. Not just for the hard of hearing!
