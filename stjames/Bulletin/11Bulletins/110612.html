Pentecost

WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. Last week, we had visitors from Perth, Australia; Anchorage, AK; Springfield, LA, and many other places! The winner of our drawing was from Keizer, OR.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are from Seattle University in honor of the Class of 2011, and from the Archdiocese of Seattle in thanksgiving for our five newly ordained priests. Floral offerings are a wonderful way to remember a deceased loved one, celebrate an anniversary, or give thanks for a prayer that has been answered. If you have been inspired to give thanks with flowers, please contact Peggy Behnken at 206-654-4650.

THE CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO CART is up and running! Your friendly Cathedral baristas will be serving espresso drinks after the 10:00 Mass. During the month of June, all proceeds will go towards scholarships for the Schola Cantorum Choir Camp. Try our special drink of the month: the Jubi-latte!

SUMMER TOURS. During the summer months, June, July, and August, we offer a tour of the Cathedral each Wednesday at 1:00pm. This is a great opportunity to share your Cathedral with out-of-town guests. No reservations are necessary; gather in the west vestibule.

PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL. Join us Tuesday, June 14 for the return of this popular summer series in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor at 7:00pm. Our first film, co-sponsored by our ESL program, is entitled Innocent Voices—it is an inspirational coming-of-age story of screenwriter Oscar Torres, who, as an 11-year-old boy, was caught up in the El Salvador’s civil war. The movie is principally about the effects of war on children (in Spanish with subtitles—rated R). Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT MISSION TRIP 2011. It is not too late for high school students to sign up for a week of service and fun as we work with migrant workers and their families. Opportunities include working with children in the daycare, and assisting at the food and clothing banks. Don’t miss out!! Application and information, Christine Henderson, 253-973-1441.

COFFEE HOUR HOSPITALITY NEEDS YOU! As we move into the warmer summer weather, we will again be offering hospitality “al fresco” in the Archbishop Murphy courtyard following the Sunday morning masses. But we need your help to make this happen! We are looking for several outgoing people to help with set-up, serving and clean-up for this very important ministry of hospitality. We especially need help following the 12:00 mass. It’s a lot of fun, and a minimal time commitment. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

THE ST. VINCENT DE PAUL conferences of St. James thank Cathedral parishioners for their generous donations last month. During May, we made a total of 79 home visits to our low income neighbors. We continued to focus the majority of our efforts on preventing homelessness: your contributions made it possible for us to keep 37 individuals and families in their homes. We also provided food for 18 households; helped pay heat and utilities for 7 families; and gave out 20 vouchers to the St. Vincent de Paul Thrift Store for beds and furniture, housewares, and other items. As requests for help steadily increase, we are seeking new members to help meet the growing need. Please consider joining this deeply rewarding ministry to the poor among us. Information, Rob Millar 206-954-8902 and Tammy Bendix 206-595-9158.

CHOIR CAMP. St. James Cathedral's Youth Music Program is now registering students in grades 4-8 (treble voices) for our annual Choir Camp, August 15-21. This exciting week-long camp provides children training in music fundamentals, Orff instruments, and proper singing techniques; and fosters their active participation in the liturgy through music. For audition and general information, Music Office, 206-382-4874.

REACH OUT TO SOMEONE SUFFERING FROM HIV/AIDS, MS OR ADDICTION. People struggling to recover from illness need more than a good doctor. They need the care and concern of a supportive community. St. James parishioners work in partnership with the Rosehedge Multifaith Works to form CareTeams that offer practical and emotional support to people isolated by their illness and cut off from the community. CareTeam training will be held Saturday, June 18, 8:45 am to 4:45 pm at the Rosehedge Multifaith Works Office, 115 – 16th Avenue, Seattle. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.
