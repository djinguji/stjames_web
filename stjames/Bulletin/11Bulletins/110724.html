St. James

July 24, 2011

Dear Friends,

Happy Feast Day! As I write this, early in the week and only too aware of how iffy our weather has been, I find myself wondering what kind of a day we’re going to get for our patronal feast day and parish picnic. Hope springs eternal, and we do have history on our side. Not once in all my years here have we been rained out on St. James Day. May our record continue!

I have phenomenally good news to share with you. We have gone over our goal for this year’s Annual Catholic Appeal. When I say “goal,” I’m referring to our combined Archdiocesan and parish goals. The Archdiocese asked us to raise $310,000 and we set an additional goal of $175,000 in order to support the great work of the Cathedral Kitchen. As of this week, we have more than met that combined goal. The last figure we received from the archdiocese is $519,000! Those are the numbers. The real story is the sacrifice so many of you made in order to make this possible; the real story is the generous response of just over 900 of our parish households. Admittedly, this represents only about 40 percent of our registered parishioners but, even so, it is a remarkable achievement and one that far exceeds that of any other parish in the archdiocese. I wish I could thank each of you personally for your amazing response but there’s no way I can. But I do trust that you know how truly grateful I am – and how proud I am to be your pastor.

Enjoy St. James day, rain or shine, and together, let us commit ourselves to following together in the steps of our great patron, St. James the Apostle, who is our constant companion on the great pilgrimage of life and faith.


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT—PRAYERS AND BLESSINGS ON OUR YOUTH AND CHAPERONES. Please keep in your prayers this week the Cathedral youth and adult chaperones who will be working with the migrant families of the Skagit Valley. Along with participants from St. Mary’s Parish, their activities will include helping with the daycare program for migrant children, working in the fields that grow produce for a local food bank, and hosting a fiesta for the migrant families. In addition, in the evenings they will reflect upon their experiences in light of Catholic Social Teaching and its implications for their everyday lives as Catholics. Vaya con Dios!

JUSTFAITH. Are you interested in exploring the social justice tradition of your faith? Do you want to know more about the spiritual journey of Francis of Assisi, Mother Theresa, Oscar Romero and the prophets? Did you participate in the Living the Gospel series and want to take those issues a step further? Consider taking part in JustFaith, an intensive program that explores Catholic Social Teaching, the church’s “best kept secret.” The program includes readings, videos, conversations about challenging topics, face-to-face encounters with the poor, and an investigation into the intersection of spirituality and Christian activism. JustFaith changes people; those people change the world! Information night Wednesday August 10, 6:30pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.
