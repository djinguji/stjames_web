Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • All of Paul’s letters are about unity in one way or another. In today’s reading from the letter to the Philippians, he pleads with them to “complete my joy” by being united in heart, love, mind, and thought. Why is unity so important in the Christian community? What does St. Paul see as the threats to unity?
  • Paul says, “humbly regard others as more important than yourselves.” Scripture scholar Vincent Smiles has said of this passage, “being humble has nothing to do with being a doormat. The humility Paul describes is self-sacrifice for others born out of unity in Christ.” Have you known people who are truly humble—in Paul’s sense of the word?

From the Desk of the Pastor
September 28, 2008

Dear Friends,

It is always a privilege to be able to welcome new members into the Full Communion of the Catholic Church. This weekend’s group is a small one but no less significant for that. At the Sunday evening Mass, Jenohn Thogerson and Adrienne Krieger will make their Profession of Faith with all of you and then receive the sacraments of Confirmation and Holy Eucharist. Please join me in praying for them and in welcoming them to the Church and to our parish.

We are well aware that our St. James Cathedral Lecture with Sister Helen Prejean, scheduled for this coming Thursday evening at 7:30pm, is on the same night as the Vice-Presidential Debate. As I mentioned a couple of weeks ago, we chose the date long before the political parties settled on the same one. But we’ve come up with a good plan to resolve the conflict. The debate begins at 6:00pm and we will be able to watch it together in the Pastoral Outreach Center, Community Room. Feel free to bring a brown bag lunch if you like. In some ways, that should be more interesting than watching it home alone! Following the debate, Sister Helen will present her lecture in Cathedral Hall.

After nearly 30 weddings since the first of June, and many more funerals than we usually have during the summer months, I’m ready for some vacation! Among other things, I’m looking forward to spending some days with good friends in Italy and to baptizing their new daughter. I’ll be remembering you in prayer from afar and will look forward to seeing you in a couple of weeks.

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
