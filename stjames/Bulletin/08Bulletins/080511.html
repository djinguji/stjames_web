WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FAITHFUL CITIZENSHIP. The U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops have stated: "In the Catholic Tradition, responsible citizenship is a virtue, and participation in political life is a moral obligation." In this important election year, we are challenged as Catholics to reflect on the issues in light of our faith and our church’s social teachings. Please join us for the second in a three-part series on Faithful Citizenship: Formation and Exercise of Conscience presented by Helen Oesterle, Monday, May 12, 7:00 pm, Cathedral Place, fourth floor. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

WOULD YOU CONSIDER BEING A RCIA SPONSOR? We continue to have many new inquirers coming to participate in our Christian Initiation process to learn more about the Catholic Church (RCIA). We need St. James parishioners to be companions with our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. All training and support will be provided and the time commitment is reasonable, a minimum of 2 sessions a month (Wednesday evenings). Sponsor Information Meeting: May 14. The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, Helen Oesterle at 206-382-4514.

ADVOCACY TRAINING TO END HOMELESSNESS. The Archdiocesan Housing Authority’s FAITH program is hosting an Advocacy Training and Issue Briefing on Saturday, May 17 at 10:30 am at Keystone United Church of Christ (5019 Keystone Place N, Seattle 98103). Participants will be briefed on federal legislation relating to housing and homelessness. They will also hear a spirited and inspiring presentation from Nancy Amidei on how to get involved in the campaign to end homelessness. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. On Tuesday, May 27, at 7:00 pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center, you’re invited to gather for a discussion of Jean Vanier’s Befriending the Stranger. VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES

HELP CREATE A COMMUNITY OF COMPASSION. Are you interested in joining other St. James parishioners to provide companionship and assistance to persons living with AIDS or MS? Multifaith Works has an unprecedented number of people waiting for volunteers. Consider attending one of their Volunteer Orientations to be held at Multifaith Works (115 16th Ave, Seattle) at 6:30 pm on Monday, May 12 or Wednesday, May 14. The orientations are only one hour and are purely informational. They will be followed by a day-long CareTeam training at First Baptist Church on June 14. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 106-654-4640.

VOLUNTEER CHORE SERVICE ORIENTATION. VCS helps connect parishioners with people who need chore services and companionship. Volunteers help to ensure that elders and individuals with disabilities and/or long-term illnesses can remain in their homes among familiar surroundings rather than going to an institutionalized setting. In March, our VCS volunteers contributed nearly 200 hours of direct service. If you are interested in this rewarding ministry, please come to the Orientation to be held in the Mother Cabrini Room (basement level) of the Pastoral Outreach Center on Tuesday, May 13th at 6:30pm. Information Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

PREGNANCY SUPPORT MINISTRY. The U.S. Bishops have stated: "The Catholic Church proclaims that human life is sacred and that the dignity of the human person is the foundation of a moral vision for society." Pregnancy Support Ministry is dedicated to helping foster human life by providing practical assistance and emotional and spiritual support for pregnant and newly parenting women. The need is great, and we are looking for additional volunteers to join us. If you can cook a meal, or drive a client to an appointment, or help with light chores, we need you! Next Training May 19, 2008. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Holy Spirit, Kent; St. Brendan, Bothell; St. Mary in Aberdeen, Seattle and Seaview; and St. Mary Mission, Coupeville.
