FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

November 9, 2008

Dear Friends,

The month of November gives us a wonderful opportunity to come close in prayer and in gratitude to the faithful departed—all those who have gone before us in the peace of Christ. We have long had the custom of remembering on the front cover of all the November bulletins all those generous parishioners who have remembered the Cathedral by leaving a bequest. Those listed have all died during the twenty years I have been privileged to serve here. Reading over their names is a wistful thing for me—but a good thing—as I see their faces, recall their personalities, and remember their goodness. By including the Cathedral in their will, these people—some of them of very modest means, others quite wealthy—have continued to make a difference in the lives of many people long after they have gone home to God. Please join me in praying for them and in thanking God for them, as well as for all our parishioners who have gone home to God.

This is our annual Sacrificial Giving Sunday. It happens to fall this year on the day the Church celebrates the dedication of the great basilica of St. John Lateran, the Pope’s Cathedral, the Cathedral church of Rome. Ironically, the gospel is the story of Jesus’ cleansing of the temple by turning over the tables of the money changers. How’s that for bad timing! However, I know you realize that any similarity between our talking about money today and what Jesus did to the money changers is pretty superficial. They had compromised the holiness of the temple; we, on the other hand, actually contribute to the holiness of this place by the sacrifices we make. Let me take this opportunity to thank you in advance for your generous response to this annual invitation to support the parish. I realize that the current economic crisis is a very real worry for many. All I ask is that you respond in a way that is in keeping with God’s goodness to you. If you do, we will be able to meet our obligations and continue our outreach to the poor and needy.

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • On this Feast of the Dedication of St. John Lateran, we read a passage from St. Paul’s first letter to the Corinthians (I Cor. 3: 9c-11, 16-17). In his writings, St. Paul often uses vivid and memorable images to describe various aspects of the life of faith. In this passage, he says, “You are God’s building” (3:9). How is this metaphor different from some of the others Paul uses (a field, for example, or the human body)? What does it reveal about the Church? Do you have a sense of being the Church?
  • St. Paul applies the image of the building not only to the community of believers, but to each individual: “you are the temple of God.” How does the knowledge that the Holy Spirit dwells in you affect what you do, and how you treat others? Our Sacrificial Giving theme this year echoes St. Paul: “You are St. James Cathedral.” When have you had a sense of being part of this community of faith? Of being the face of Christ for others?
