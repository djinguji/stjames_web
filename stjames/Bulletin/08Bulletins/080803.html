Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • In today’s second reading (Romans 8: 35, 37- 39), Paul gives a litany of the many different forces that seek to separate us from God— earthly trials, like pain, persecution, hunger, and war; and other powers as well: death and life, time itself. And with absolute confidence he says that nothing can separate us from Christ. In your own life, what forces do you find trying to separate you from Christ? Do you let them?
  • St. Paul himself experienced all the sufferings he mentions in this reading. He endured incredible physical as well as spiritual trials in his life, and yet he says, “in all these things we conquer overwhelmingly through him who loved us.” Can you think of a time when suffering has brought you closer to God?

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates (and become eligible for Cathedral scholarships). In addition, if you are a registered parishioner and use envelopes, or are on the direct debit program, we will mail you a quarterly report of your contributions to the parish. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. Please drop the completed card in the collection basket or put it in the mail. If you’re not sure whether you’re registered at St. James, call the parish office at 206-622-3559.

A reminder about parking on Sundays
for the Cathedral

We know that parking in this area is at a premium and we are doing our best to make it available to you. In consideration of our neighbors and to ease parking problems on Sundays, please note these important Do’s and Don’ts.

You MAY park:

  • In the O’Dea High School lot (the Cathedral staff spots are also available during the weekend Masses).
  • In the Cabrini Tower Garage on Marion Street across from O’Dea High School for the Saturday 5:30 pm Mass and all Sunday Masses. The Cathedral pays for parking during these times, and you are encouraged to take advantage of it. (How often are you offered free parking in Seattle?)
  • In the P-1 parking lot under the Chancery building at 9th and Marion.

Please DO NOT park:

  • In the lot adjacent to the Envoy Apartments on 9th Avenue, directly across from the Cathedral.
  • In the Blood Bank parking lot at the corner of Terry and Columbia.
  • In the Frye Museum parking lots on Terry Avenue.
  • In the parking places at the Pastoral Outreach Center.
  • In the spaces on Terry Avenue reserved for loading and unloading of the parish vans.

FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

August 3, 2008

Dear Friends,

Our celebration of the feast of St. James last weekend was wonderful in every way (except, perhaps, for the weather!). A number of visitors told me that they had couldn’t remember a more beautiful celebration of Mass. And to top it all off, we had a great picnic! Many thanks to everyone who made the celebration possible: liturgical ministers as well as picnic planners and a host of volunteers. Special thanks to Anna Horton and Stephen Pace who, once again this year, organized the entire event and worked their special magic both behind the scenes and on the scene.

Let me take this opportunity to thank you for your amazing response to this year’s Annual Catholic Appeal. Thanks to your incredible generosity, we not only met the goal set for us by the Archdiocese ($255,000), but also raised an additional $180,000 for the parish. There are no adjectives that can adequately describe this achievement, and there are no words that can adequately describe the gratitude I feel. “Thank you” will have to do. Needless to say, there is no other parish in the Archdiocese that came even remotely close to what you did.

I’m doing double duty today (Sunday). After preaching at the 10:00 Mass I will be making a mad dash over to St. Mark’s Episcopal Cathedral where I’ve been invited to preach at the 11:00 Eucharist. It was an honor to be asked and I look forward to it. My stay over there will have to be brief, however, since we have a large ‘crop’ of babies – 13, I’m told, to be baptized at our noon Mass!

The summer was late in arriving and is quick in passing. It’s hard to believe that we’re on the downward slope of it already. I hope the remaining weeks give you a chance for some R & R before the busy fall months arrive.

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
