WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS IN THE CATHEDRAL are from the Saturday wedding of Arlene Nobleza and Derek Silverio. Flowers are also from the Quintana-Leon family in memory of Steven W. Cather. Floral offerings are a venerable custom in the Church, and a wonderful way to remember a deceased loved one, celebrate an anniversary, or give thanks for an answered prayer. There are still several weekends available during the coming months. Information Kay Kaiser, 206-654-4650.

YOUNG ADULTS INFORMAL "THIRD SUNDAY" gathering is Sunday, May 18 immediately following 5:30 Mass. Young adults ages 21 to 35, single or married, gather for fun, refreshments, and fellowship in the Fireside Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center. This is the final "Third Sunday" gathering until fall, so let’s get together to prime ourselves up for summer! Newcomers and those not new to the parish are all welcome! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

ST. JAMES WINTER SHELTER CELEBRATES. The 2007-2008 Winter Shelter successfully concluded at the end of April. Over 80 volunteers helped to provide 1494 bed nights, 1958 breakfasts, and 3136 hours of service. Countless others contributed toiletries each week. Thanks to all of the parishioners who have supported this ministry to our brothers and sisters in need.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. On Tuesday, May 27, at 7:00 pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center, you’re invited to gather for a discussion of Jean Vanier’s Befriending the Stranger. Vanier opens up God’s invitation to us to create new places of belonging and sharing, of peace and kindness, where each person is loved and accepted. Befriending the Stranger is available in the Cathedral Bookstore. No previous book group experience required! Information, Maria Laughlin, 206-382-4284.

We are called to be compassionate as our God is compassionate (see Luke 6:36). What does the compassion of Christ look like in the modern world? How do I become a more compassionate person? The JustFaith program is an intensive thirty-week program that helps to answer these questions through reflection, study, prayer, and community. To learn more, come to the Information Night on Wednesday, June 4 at 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

Old voices-young voices; trained-untrained; in-tune singers and in-their-own-tune singers—all are welcome. Sunday, June 1, 10:00 am. Rehearsal Thursday, May 29, 7:30 pm. THE WAY OF COMPANIONSHIP. How can we reach out with love to those who are hurting? How can I see the face of Christ in the face of a homeless person? There are many times when we are called up to listen to another person, to hear their story and to offer some kind of help or support. Craig Rennebohm from the Mental Health Chaplaincy will present a training in the practice of companionship, a way of being gently present to another and sharing a little of the journey together. Tuesday, June 3, 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information and to register, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL. Join us on the second Tuesdays of the summer months in Cathedral Place, second floor at 7:00 pm for the return of this popular film series. Our first film on June 10 will be The Heart Has Its Reasons, the story of Jean Vanier and of the joys and struggles of daily life in community with mentally and physically handicapped people. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

TAKE ME OUT TO THE BALLGAME! Join us on Sunday, July 20 at 1:05 pm when our Mariners take on the Cleveland Indians at 1:05 pm. Tickets are $17. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514, or complete the order form found on our website.

SUMMER COFFEE HOUR. Summer is coming, and we’ll soon be sipping coffee in the sunshine. Weather permitting, the Sunday morning coffee hours will take place in the Archbishop Murphy courtyard starting on June 1 and continuing through Labor Day weekend. But this can only happen with your help. Hosting coffee hour outdoors requires extra hands, and our regular coffee hour crew is already stretched. If you have enjoyed the summer coffee hour in the past, would you please consider helping us bring it back? We are looking for several outgoing people to help with set-up, serving and clean-up for this very important ministry of hospitality. It’s fun, it’s easy, and you might even get a suntan! Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Holy Trinity, Bremerton; St. Rita, Tacoma; and St. Yves Mission, Harmony.
