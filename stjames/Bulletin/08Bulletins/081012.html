FOUNDATIONS OF OUR CATHOLIC FAITH: INTRODUCTION TO THE BIBLE—OLD AND NEW TESTAMENTS. Sunday, October 26, 1:30-5:30 pm at St. Peter’s Parish Hall, Beacon Hill (Co-sponsored by St. James). Come explore the Catholic understanding of Scripture and Tradition. Participants will be able to identify the Books of the Bible and understand their variety of literary forms, genres and key themes. They will discuss the process of arriving at the Canon of Scripture and understand why we have more books than other Christian denominations. They will also learn about the role of the Magisterium, in Biblical interpretation. Registration is required and the cost is $5 per person/class for parishioners. Brochures are available at the Parish Office or contact Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • In today’s reading from the letter to the Philippians, St. Paul writes, “I have learned the secret of being well fed and of going hungry”; of living “in humble circumstances” and “with abundance.” What is that secret? Have you learned it?
  • “I can do all things in him who strengthens me.” St. Frances Xavier Cabrini chose this passage for her motto. When have you experienced this “strength” of Christ working through you? When have you witnessed its power in others?
