WELCOME: If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

SACRIFICIAL SUNDAY GIVING 2009

If you’ve never made a commitment to Sacrificial Giving, please consider making one this year. Our goal is not a dollar amount. Instead, we’re going for 100% parishioner participation! MASS OF THANKSGIVING DAY. The word “Eucharist” means “thanksgiving,” and so we come together as a parish family on Thanksgiving Day, Thursday, November 27, at 9:30 am, to give thanks by celebrating the Eucharist. During the Mass, you will be invited to bring your gifts of non-perishable food items for the poor and needy to the altar.

Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • In today’s passage from the letter to the Thessalonians, Paul reminds us that we cannot know when “the day of the Lord” will be. And yet he tells us that our awareness that that day will come is to shape every aspect of daily conduct. Does this awareness shape your worldview?
  • “All of you are children of the light and of the day.” What do you think Paul means by these words? How do you deal in your day to day life as a Christian with the challenge of staying “alert and sober”?

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Andrew, Sumner; St. Francis Xavier, Toledo; and St. Cecilia, Stanwood and Bainbridge Island.
