WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

VOTER REGISTRATION. Are you a Faithful Citizen? The church teaches that we have a moral obligation to participate in political life, which includes voting. St. James Cathedral will be offering voter registration one Sunday each month throughout the summer and early fall. Betty Sullivan, St. James Cathedral parishioner and member of the League of Women Voters, will be available during the morning coffee hours in the Hall TODAY, June 8, to register voters and to answer any questions you might have about voter registration.

NIGHT OF 1,000 CONVERSATIONS. On June 19, thousands of people from across the country will gather in homes, offices, coffee shops, and places of worship to discuss immigration, civil liberties and human rights. Come join a conversation hosted by the ESL advisory board and learn more about Justice for Immigrants, the Catholic campaign for immigration reform. Learn about Catholic teachings that underpin this campaign, as well as proposals from USCCB to achieve reforms in our nations laws and policies that better reflect our values as a nation of immigrants. We will meet on Thursday, June 19 at 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Please RSVP to the ESL Program at 206-382-4511.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. On Tuesday, June 24, at 7:00 pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center, you’re invited to gather for a discussion of Garry Wills’ What Paul Meant. This study of Paul will challenge us to a deeper understanding of the great apostle as we begin a year dedicated to St. Paul (June 2008-June 2009). What Paul Meant is available in the Cathedral Bookstore. Information, Maria Laughlin, 206-382-4284.

YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT CAR WASH. On July 6, teens from St. James, St. Mary’s, St. George’s, St. Edward’s, and St. Paul’s will head to Lynden, Washington to participate in the Archdiocese’s Youth Migrant Project mission trip. The teens will volunteer in migrant daycare centers, run a Vacation Bible School and Reading Camp, experience farm work, and have time to reflect on their experiences. In order to help fund their trip, they are hosting two car washes. The first will take place today, June 8 from 10:30 am- 2:00 pm at St. Mary’s Church (611 20 Ave S., Seattle). Please plan on coming to get your car washed and support a great cause! Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

WINTER SHELTER TOILETRIES DONATIONS NEEDED. As part of their overnight stay at our Winter Shelter for men, our guests are given toiletries that they can use to freshen up before they head out for the day (many head to work in the mornings). We are able to provide these toiletries thanks to the generous donations given by St. James parishioners. Although Winter Shelter has recently ended for the season, we continue to collect donations over the summer. Please consider dropping off any of the following unused items in the bin at coffee hour over the next few months: disposable razors, small sewing kits, cough drops, economy-sized bottles of Tums and Tylenol. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

AMERICORPS OPENING! This is an opportunity not to be missed! The ESL Program has one current opening for an AmeriCorps member starting in September. The AmeriCorps will develop job skills in volunteer management and providing services to diverse populations. Specifically, the AmeriCorps member will provide English instruction to 1-3 groups of adult low-income refugees and/or immigrants in Seattle and South King County. Open until filled. To apply, you must first fill out and submit an AmeriCorps application available at http://www.kcls.org/literacyamericorps/index.cfm. Visit the ESL program website. Information, Christopher J. Koehler, 206-382-4511.

Our first film on June 10 will be The Heart Has Its Reasons, the story of Jean Vanier and of the joys and struggles of daily life in community with mentally and physically handicapped people. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

TAKE ME OUT TO THE BALLGAME! Join us on Sunday, July 20 at 1:05 pm when our Mariners take on the Cleveland Indians at 1:05 pm. Tickets are $17. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514 or complete the order form found on our website.
