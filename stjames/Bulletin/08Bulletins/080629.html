June 29, 2008

Dear Friends,

It’s a nice coincidence that the feast of Saints Peter and Paul falls on a Sunday this year. I say that because the Church begins today a special year dedicated to St. Paul. Why this year? Well, 2008 marks the two-thousandth anniversary (give or take – we don’t have actual dates to go by) of his birth. Why a year dedicated to St. Paul? Because a case can be made for calling him the second most influential figure in the entire history of Christianity. Second only to Jesus. During the coming year, our parish will continue to offer educational opportunities to learn more about St. Paul and his writings. You can read more on a special page in today’s bulletin, or the more technologically savvy among you can check the special feature on the Cathedral’s website.

Last Sunday night we were privileged to host Jean Vanier, the founder of the L’Arche movement and, without doubt, one of the living saints of our time. His profound yet simple message awakened all of us to the mysterious way God is present in the little people of our world — the poor, the vulnerable, the ones we call disabled – and of how they nourish us spiritually and call us to what is truly important in life. The joy and peace and love that are so visible in Vanier’s face – even before he ever speaks a word – must be something like the joy and peace and love that people once saw in the face of Jesus. People often remark to me that they feel a Presence — hard to define but hard to miss — whenever they come into St. James Cathedral. If you feel that Presence today, perhaps it’s because of the quiet grace Jean Vanier and the wonderful family of L’Arche brought to us last Sunday evening. How blessed we were!

You will be happy to know that we will be hosting the SHARE Safe Haven overnight shelter for the months of July and August. Until recently this shelter has been housed in the former INS building. When the GSA terminated their arrangement SHARE asked if we would be willing to give them the use of the Cathedral Hall for the remainder of the summer. I’m sure you will agree that it’s a privilege to be able to share our facilities with 30 homeless people.

Summer seemed slow to arrive this year but better late than never. I hope these days are good for you and that they bring some opportunities for relaxation and leisure. May they also be days of deepening our relationship with God. Have a blessed summer!

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
