FOUNDATIONS OF OUR CATHOLIC FAITH: INTRODUCTION TO THE BIBLE—OLD AND NEW TESTAMENTS. This Sunday, October 26, 1:30-5:30 pm at St. Peter’s Parish Hall, Beacon Hill (Co-sponsored by St. James). Come explore the Catholic understanding of Scripture and Tradition. Participants will be able identify the Books of the Bible and understand their variety of literary forms, genres and key themes. They will discuss the process of arriving at the Canon of Scripture and understand why we have more books than other Christian denominations. They will also learn about the role of the Magisterium, in Biblical interpretation. The cost is $5 per person for parishioners.

KEEP ST. MARTIN DE PORRES SHELTER AND LAZARUS DAY CENTER OPEN. Two of the Archdiocesan Housing Authority’s programs, the Lazarus Day Center and St. Martin’s Shelter, are in danger of having to close their doors two days a week due to budget short falls. They are asking the City of Seattle for $85,000 to help keep their doors open. Without this funding, hundreds of elderly homeless people will be at risk. Please stop by coffee hour after Mass during the next two weekends to sign letters to the City Council. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • St. Paul gives high praise to the Thessalonians in today’s second reading: “in every place your faith in God has gone forth, so that we have no need to say anything.” Can that be said of our own Christian community? What can we do so that our faith in God can “go forth”?
  • St. Paul describes the conversion of the Thessalonians: “receiving the word in great affliction, with joy from the Holy Spirit.” Does this ring true of your own conversion experience (or your experience of being a follower of Christ)? When have you experienced this mixture of “affliction” and “joy”?
