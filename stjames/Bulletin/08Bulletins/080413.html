Bulletin - April 13, 2008

 Easter IV

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS in the Cathedral are from the Saturday wedding of Breanne Zahren and Roger Carr.

GUEST CHORAL ARTIST. The Choir of the Cathedral of the Madeleine, Salt Lake City, Utah, former St. James Parishioner Greg Glenn, Director, Sunday, April 13, 12:00 Noon Mass, 8:00 pm concert. Read more about the Madeleine Choir School at www.madeleinechoirschool.org.

PAPAL VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES. A place of prayer has been established in the west aisle for Pope Benedict XVI’s first apostolic journey to the United States, April 15-20. During the Holy Father’s visit, each of the Cathedral’s Masses on weekdays and Sundays will conclude with a procession to the place of prayer, where we offer our prayers for the unity and peace of our Church and our world during these historic days. A prayer guide is included in your bulletin today so you can join in this special prayer from home. You can also find the prayers and additional resources online.

CATHOLIC 101: SACRAMENTS OF HEALING AND VOCATION. Join us on Tuesdays, April 15, & 29, 1:00-3:00 or 7:00-9:00, Cathedral Place-second floor for an ongoing look at the newly published US Catechism for Adults. Helen Oesterle reviews the foundations of our Catholic faith from our American Catholic experience and facilitates lively conversations among the participants. (Focus on April 15: Sacrament of Holy Orders) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values. A new inquiry session just started and you are welcome to join us. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

IN THE BOOKSTORE. Deluxe Cathedral duffel bags. Available only while supplies last. We have a wide selection of First Communion books and gifts, also St. Francis garden art.

HONOR POPE BENEDICT XVI’S VISIT TO THE US by getting involved in service in your community. In his message for Lent, the Holy Father said, "Every time when, for love of God, we share our goods with our neighbor in need, we discover that the fullness of life comes from love and all is returned to us a blessing in the form of peace, inner satisfaction and joy." Explore the St. James website to learn how you can get involved in serving our neighbors in need. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. On Thursday, April 24, at 7:00 pm in the Rectory Parlors (note date and place change!), you’re invited to gather for a discussion of Henri Nouwen’s Adam: God’s Beloved. This is a memoir about Nouwen’s friendship with Adam, a severely handicapped man he knew at the L'Arche Daybreak Community in Canada. Although Adam could not speak and was wracked with violent seizures, Nouwen called Adam "my friend, my teacher, and my guide." Adam is available in the Cathedral Bookstore. Information, Maria Laughlin, 206-382-4284.

CATHEDRAL SCHOLARSHIP FUND FOR RAINBOW SCHOOLS’ STUDENTS. Our parish has established a scholarship fund to assist families in meeting the financial obligation involved in sending their elementary school age children to any one of the Rainbow Schools (Sts. George, Edward, Paul, Therese, and Joseph). Applications are now available for the 2008-2009 school year. Families are required to be registered in our parish, have a Sacrificial Giving card on file (whether they are able to make a monetary pledge or not), and are active in the parish in some capacity. Applications are due April 30. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

THE ST. VINCENT DE PAUL SOCIETY at St. James appreciates the $7,721.54 donated in March. Vouchers were provided to feed 37 people, help 29 with rent, purchase basic furniture for 13, provide clothing for 8, assist 4 with utility bills, and cover the cost of 1 person’s medical prescription. Your generosity enables us to assist those in need in First Hill, Downtown, Pioneer Square and Capitol Hill. Thank you! If you are interested in joining this ministry to the poor, please contact us. Information, Jijo Jose, 425-260-3476, or Melissa Sanders, 206-328-6595.

“LIVING STONES CONFERENCE” St. James Cathedral is a co-sponsor of the “Living Stones” conference on April 18 and 19 at University Presbyterian Church and University Christian Chuch. The conference brings together ecumenical leaders from Catholic, Protestant, and Evangelical churches in Western Washington. Among the speakers who will bring news of our Christian brothers and sisters in the Holy Land are Palestinian Christians Sami Awad, Dr. Bernard Sabella, and Jonathan Kuttab. Information, 800-836-3006 or www.livingstonesconference.org.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? It is not too late to join our Welcome Back program – a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are held on Mondays from 7:00- 9:00 pm, April 7 through June 30 in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

AIDS AND MS CARE TEAM. Are you interested in joining the St. James CareTeam to provide companionship and assistance to persons living with AIDS or MS? Consider attending the Volunteer Orientation to be held at Multifaith Works (115 16th Avenue, Seattle) on Friday, April 25 at 10:30 am. The orientation is only one hour and is purely informational. It will be followed by a day-long CareTeam training at First Baptist Church on June 14. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

THE ROAD TO EMMAUS. Last Sunday, we heard the gospel story of the disciples’ encounter with the risen Jesus on the road to Emmaus. As Father Ryan said in his homily, "Jesus is our unexpected and unrecognized companion on the journey." The Emmaus Companions ministry is dedicated to recognizing and serving Jesus in the form of the stranger we encounter on the journey. We provide compassionate listening and resource information for people in need during the weekend masses. We are looking for a few new people to join us in this important ministry of welcoming and presence. All that is needed is a listening ear and an open heart. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Joseph, Elma and Sequim; and St. Bernadette, Seattle.
