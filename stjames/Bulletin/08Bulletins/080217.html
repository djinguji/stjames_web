JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Lent is a particularly special time for the Elect who have been journeying towards baptism, Eucharist and confirmation at the Easter Vigil. As part of their preparation, the Church celebrates anointing with the oil of the Catechumen, blessings and exorcisms. Beginning next Sunday, we will join in prayer for our Elect as they participate in the ancient ceremonies called the Scrutinies that symbolize the spiritual purification taking place in their lives as they prepare for baptism. Through these Scrutinies, blessings and anointings, the Elect are encouraged to reflect more deeply on their own spiritual challenges and weaknesses. At the same time, these rites offer powerful prayers of faith, hope and strength on their behalf, calling upon God’s grace and mercy to penetrate their entire lives. Please continue to keep them in your prayers.

As we celebrate the second Sunday of Lent, Operation Rice Bowl begins to focus on the West African country of Mali. One of the programs that Catholic Relief Services runs in Mali is a microfinance project that lends money to people who want to start or expand their own small business. These projects work with a group dynamic bringing people together to ensure accountability and help one to another.

St. Paul tells us to “bear your hardship for the Gospel with the strength that comes from God.” These groups working together in Mali are bearing hardship together. By praying and giving with Operation Rice Bowl, we too are helping to shoulder their burden and to express a profound message of solidarity.

A reminder about parking on Sundays
for the Cathedral

We know that parking in this area is at a premium and we are doing our best to make it available to you. In consideration of our neighbors and to ease parking problems on Sundays, please note these important Do’s and Don’ts.

You MAY park:

  • In the unmarked spaces in the O’Dea High School lot. THE NUMBERED SPACES AGAINST THE WALL ARE NEVER AVAILABLE FOR GENERAL PARKING, AS THEY ARE ASSIGNED TO SPECIFIC CATHEDRAL EMPLOYEES.
  • In the Cabrini Tower Garage on Marion Street across from O’Dea High School for the Saturday 5:30 pm Mass and all Sunday Masses. The Cathedral pays for parking during these times, and you are encouraged to take advantage of it. (How often are you offered free parking in Seattle?)
  • In the parking lot at the corner of 9th Avenue and Columbia Street.
  • In the P-1 parking lot under the Chancery building at 9th and Marion.

Please DO NOT park:

  • In the lot adjacent to the Envoy Apartments on 9th Avenue, directly across from the Cathedral.
  • In the Blood Bank parking lot at the corner of Terry and Columbia.
  • In the Frye Museum parking lots on Terry Avenue.
  • In the parking places at the Pastoral Outreach Center.
  • In the spaces on Terry Avenue reserved for loading and unloading of the parish vans.
