FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

December 16, 2007

Dear Friends,

Next weekend, we will put the finishing touches on our Cathedral Centennial celebration. Archbishop Brunett will preside and preach at the evening Mass on Saturday, December 22, the actual day, one-hundred years ago, on which Bishop O’Dea dedicated the Cathedral. Following the Mass, he will lead a procession outside to the Archbishop Murphy Courtyard where he will bless the huge granite mensa or altar-top of the Cathedral’s original altar, consecrated by Bishop O’Dea on that December day so long ago. When we renovated the Cathedral in 1994, this huge piece of granite that was the high altar was removed and placed in storage. Now it will be mounted in the courtyard on the outer wall of the Cathedral with an inscription that will recall important milestones in the Cathedral’s long and blessed history.

A similar blessing will take place on Sunday morning, December 23, after the 10:00 Mass.

I know you will do your best to be here for one of these historic celebrations, and I also know I can count on you to pray for good weather!

May these remaining days of Advent deepen within you a longing for Christ’s coming among us at Christmas!

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

P.S. Since, after many months of searching, we have yet to locate the cornerstone Bishop O’Dea laid in 1905, maybe we can think of the granite altar he dedicated in 1907 as the cornerstone for the next hundred years!
