FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

November 23, 2008

Dear Friends,

I want to thank every one of you who has made a Sacrificial Giving commitment to the parish for the coming year. For some of you, given the precarious situation with the economy, making a commitment this year was also making an act of faith. Your sacrifice is personally very moving to me and I am deeply grateful. Many of you who always make a commitment have yet to do so and I encourage you to do so this week if at all possible. And, then, to those of you who have not been in the habit of participating in Sacrificial Giving, I make an earnest plea that you consider doing so this year and that you do it in a way that will involve some sacrifice. Too many of us are still content to make our parish giving an afterthought instead of a planned thought and this usually results in dropping a dollar or two in the collection basket when it comes around. Of course, if that represents a sacrifice, I understand, but I don’t think it always does – not when you consider that many of us think little or nothing of spending significant amounts to buy a latte, an article of clothing, a DVD, the latest electronic gadget, or even a new car. Considering the cost of such things, I hope you will agree with me that a weekly planned gift to your parish – made out of gratitude to God for all God’s gifts in your life – isn’t asking too much.

This Saturday morning, November 22, I am in Milwaukee attending the ordination to the Diaconate of one of our parishioners, Todd Strange. Todd is known to many of you since he served here as a Eucharistic minister for a number of years. He is a wonderful young man, a gifted minister, and a product of our RCIA! His ordination is taking place at Sacred Heart Seminary near Milwaukee. Todd will be ordained to the priesthood here in the Cathedral in June and will celebrate his Mass of Thanksgiving here on the Feast of Corpus Christi. I know he will be grateful for your prayers.

As always, it will be a joy to gather with you here in the Cathedral at 9:30 am on Thanksgiving Day to celebrate our annual Thanksgiving Mass. This Mass is always one of the high points of the year for me and I know it is for many of you. I know I don’t need to encourage the hundreds of you who come for this Mass every year because you would never miss it. But to those of you who have never been to this Mass, I would offer a word of invitation and encouragement. It will quickly become an indispensable part of your Thanksgiving celebration! To all who do come, a reminder that at the Offertory we always invite people to come to the altar with their gifts of non-perishable food items which will be shared with a local food bank. Happy Thanksgiving!

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • In the second reading, St. Paul makes it clear where Christ’s kingship comes from: from his death on the cross and his resurrection to new life. What does this say about the kingship of Christ? Is Christ king in your life?
  • The Fathers of the Second Vatican Council wrote: “Now Christ has communicated this royal power to His disciples that they might be constituted in royal freedom and that … they might conquer the reign of sin in themselves. He has shared this power so that serving Christ in others, they might by humility and patience lead their brothers and sisters to that King for whom to serve is to reign.” (Lumen Gentium 36). How do you serve others? How does your service help you to feel part of the building of God’s kingdom?
