WELCOME: If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

KEEP ST. MARTIN DE PORRES SHELTER AND LAZARUS DAY CENTER OPEN. Two of the Archdiocesan Housing Authority’s programs, the Lazarus Day Center and St. Martin’s Shelter, are in danger of having to close their doors two days a week due to budget short falls. They are asking the City of Seattle for $85,000 to help keep their doors open. Without this funding, hundreds of elderly homeless people will be at risk. Please stop by coffee hour after Mass during the next two weekends to sign letters to the City Council. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

FOUNDATIONS OF OUR CATHOLIC FAITH: INTRODUCTION TO THE BIBLE—OLD AND NEW TESTAMENTS. Sunday, October 26, 1:30-5:30 pm at St. Peter’s Parish Hall, Beacon Hill (Co-sponsored by St. James). Come explore the Catholic understanding of Scripture and Tradition. Participants will be able to identify the Books of the Bible and understand their variety of literary forms, genres and key themes. They will discuss the process of arriving at the Canon of Scripture and understand why we have more books than other Christian denominations. They will also learn about the role of the Magisterium, in Biblical interpretation. Registration is required and the cost is $5 per person/class for parishioners. Brochures are available at the Parish Office or contact Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • Today we begin a new letter, to the Thessalonians. Paul begins his letter by affirming the great grace and goodness present in the Church of Thessalonica, and giving thanks for it. So often we notice the ways in which Christians (and even our Church) fall short of their calling. Do we also remember to notice and to give thanks for what is good?
  • “Our gospel did not come to you in word alone.” In what ways have you experienced the power of the Gospel, apart from words?
