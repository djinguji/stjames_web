THE DEVIL’S HIGHWAY. St. James ESL invites you to gather for a discussion of The Devil’s Highway by award-winning author Luis Alberto Urrea. It is a powerful and moving account of 26 Mexicans who braved crossing the desert in southern Arizona, seeking a better life. Join us for a discussion on Tuesday, August 26 at 7:00 pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room, Pastoral Outreach Center. This tragic and graphic story, full of courage and betrayal, is not for the squeamish. Available now in the Cathedral Bookstore. Information, Christopher J. Koehler 206-382-4511.

It also includes the story behind the writing of the great hymn, Amazing Grace. Rated PG. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • Last week, we heard about the tension between “flesh” and “spirit.” This week (Romans 8: 18-23), Paul continues to explore that tension. Though we have received “the firstfruits of the Spirit,” we do not have the Spirit in its fullness. We continue to suffer and struggle, but with a new hope. If you had to explain your hope in Christ, what would you say?
  • St. Paul tells us that not only human beings, but all of creation shares in the “sufferings of the present time,” and awaits the glory that is to come. How might these words help us to reflect on the present environmental crisis?

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Star of the Sea, Bremerton and Port Townsend; Star of the Sea Mission, Stevenson; and the Carmelite Monastery of St. Joseph, Shoreline.
