FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

January 13, 2008

Dear Friends,

Do you have the feeling we have been in church a lot lately? It’s more than a feeling. It’s a fact! There were the Advent Masses, the Advent Reconciliation Service, several Advent musical events, Christmas, New Year’s, Epiphany, and today, the Feast of the Baptism. It’s been a journey toward the Light through the year’s darkest days, a blessed journey to be sure.

To all of you who worked so hard to make those days the beautiful ones they were, a heartfelt word of thanks. Your work did not go unnoticed. As one person put it to me after the 10:00 Mass on Christmas morning, “St. James really knows how to do Christmas!”

But we did more than pray during those days. True to form, hundreds of you accepted the challenge to reach out to the people in the Southwest part of our state who suffered so greatly during the early December floods. In response to the invitation one Sunday, and without any prior notice, you spontaneously dropped more than $8,000 into the collection baskets as you left the Cathedral. The following Sunday you gave another $8,000-plus to the St. Vincent de Paul Society which they generously turned over to the flood victims. In his letter of thanks to the parish, our parishioner, Andre de Klaver, Executive Director of the Society of St. Vincent de Paul, wrote, “St. James Cathedral is the place where people in Seattle go for help and comfort in times of tragedy. The flood victims around Western Washington will now also benefit from your compassionate outreach. May God bless you for your generosity.”

What can I add to that? May God indeed bless you for your generosity. And may this Feast of the Baptism of Christ we celebrate remind us of why we do all we do around here!

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

ST. JAMES WINTER SHELTER VOLUNTEERS NEEDED. Each Winter, from October through April, St. James operates an overflow shelter for 10-14 men from Catholic Community Services’ St. Martin de Porres Shelter. The men are 50 and over and cannot stay at St. Martin’s because of lack of space in the cold winter months. Pairs of volunteers serve as overnight and/or breakfast hosts once a month. We always welcome more breakfast cooks but are especially in need of volunteers who can serve as overnight hosts on a Friday, Saturday, Sunday, or Monday night. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

VOLUNTEER CHORE SERVICE helps connect parishioners with people who need chore services and companionship. Volunteers help to ensure that elders and individuals with disabilities can remain in their homes among familiar surroundings rather than going to an institutionalized setting. If you are interested in this rewarding ministry, please sign up for the Orientation to be held in the Pastoral Outreach Center at 6:30 pm on Tuesday, January 22.
