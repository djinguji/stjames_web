JOURNEY OF THE ELECT

Beginning this week, our newly Elect are wearing brown robes while attending Mass and other liturgies during Lent. This helps them to know, in a concrete way, that they have entered a new and significant stage in their final preparations for Easter. It also anticipates another change that will take place when they emerge from the baptismal waters at the Easter Vigil and change to white robes. Seek them out in our midst and, if possible, introduce yourself to them after Mass. Let them know you are praying for them and looking forward to Easter when they will join us at the Eucharistic table.

LENTEN PRAYER PARTNERS FOR THE ELECT. The Church tells us that it is the entire parish’s responsibility to support and welcome those preparing for baptism this Easter. One of the many ways you can do this is to become a prayer partner for one of our Elect. You are asked to pray every day for him/her during Lent, asking for God’s blessings and guidance. Also, notes and cards will be collected during Holy Week to be given to the Elect before Easter. Prayer cards are available at the place of prayer located in the north aisle of the west nave of the Cathedral (where you can also learn more about each of them in our Book of the Elect), in the bookstore, and in Cathedral Hall during Sunday morning hospitality. You can also meet our Elect by going to our parish website. Your prayers make a difference! Information Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

MIDDAY PRAYER. Each weekday at 12:10 pm in the Chapel, we gather to pray the office of Midday Prayer from the Liturgy of the Hours. Called “the prayer of the Church,” the Liturgy of the Hours consists of psalms, scripture, and intercessory prayer, and is prayed daily by bishops, priests, and religious throughout the world. All are welcome.

This past week on Ash Wednesday our Lenten journey began. Catholic Relief Services invites us to make this journey in solidarity with our global family, and Operation Rice Bowl makes this easy and enriching. It is a journey of prayer, fasting, learning and giving, all with an international flavor. To be Catholic means to be linked not just to our local communities, but also to our global community. Last year, the $8 million offered by participants in Operation Rice Bowl made a huge difference to people all around the world. Our participation in Operation Rice Bowl during this Lenten season will challenge us to expand our own horizons. We will be provoked to alleviate suffering and combat injustice.

FROM THE DESK OF THE PASTOR

February 10, 2008

Dear Friends,

It’s not unusual, after the Christmas season, to have someone remark to me, “Well, now that your busy season is over….” That’s the common perception for sure, but it doesn’t really reflect the situation at St. James. In fact, I have come to believe that there is no season around here that is not busy! That’s partly why St. James Cathedral is such an exciting and vibrant place.

Take this past week. On Tuesday, we were privileged to play host to Olivier Latry, the Titular Organist of the Cathedral of Notre-Dame in Paris. To have M. Latry play here at St. James was something of a coup, I would say, and the concert he presented demonstrated not only his remarkable skills but also the glories of the two Cathedral organs. Lucky for us, of course, we are regularly spoiled around here by the extraordinary gifts of our two highly gifted Cathedral organists….

The next day, Ash Wednesday, saw huge numbers of people crowding the Cathedral morning, noon, and night. Counting the mid-morning Mass for the O’Dea High School students, there were well over 3,000 people in the Cathedral on Ash Wednesday -- a hopeful and impressive beginning to the season of Lent and a wonderful testimonial to faith.

The next day we hosted another major gathering in the Cathedral – the first of three Rites of Election for catechumens from parishes around the Archdiocese. The Cathedral was full to overflowing Thursday evening as Archbishop Brunett welcomed hundreds of people, including a large group from our own parish, who will be baptized during the coming Easter Vigil. Two more of these Rites of Election will be held on Monday and Wednesday of this coming week. If you are looking for signs of hope for the Church, this surely has to be one of them!

As part of our parish’s Lenten journey, we launched this past week another round of “Putting God First.” I’m happy to share with you that 65 of your fellow parishioners accepted the invitation to participate in this five-week retreat program that gathers people in small groups with other parishioners to talk about faith and discipleship. “Putting God First” is a St. James Cathedral ‘original’ that began nearly two years ago. To date, more than 300 people have participated in it and they would be the first to tell you that it has been a wonderful spark to their growth in faith. More opportunities are planned for the future.

The first lap of my personal Lenten journey will be some days of retreat at a Benedictine Abbey in England. I’m looking forward to the opportunity to idle my engines for awhile and to plug into the prayerful rhythm of the monastic community of Downside Abbey. You can be sure that I will be remembering you in prayer during my days away.

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
