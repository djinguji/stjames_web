Bulletin - December 9, 2007

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are—whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

TEN MINUTE TOUR. Following the 10:00 am and Sunday evening Masses TODAY, you are invited to gather near the ambo for a short tour of the Cathedral. This week, we will look at the big picture: the shape of the Cathedral itself. Ever wondered what a nave is? Or a transept? This is your chance to find out! This is the last of our ten-minute tours, which have been offered on the second and fourth Sundays of the month throughout our Centennial Year 2007.

VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES

MAILING VOLUNTEERS NEEDED. The ESL Program needs volunteers to help with our monthly mailing projects, approximately 6 hours per month, flexible schedule. Coordinate with staff and other volunteers to fold, label and stuff, and then deliver them to the Business Mail office (near Safeco Field). A car is necessary. Information, Mali Main, 206-382-4511.

HELP REFUGEES WITH CROSS-CULTURAL ORIENTATION and English language instruction. Burmese, Afghani, and Meskhetian Turk refugees need tutors and group instructors in South Seattle, Kent, Tukwila, and Federal Way. Flexible schedule. We provide a pre-service training in English language instruction techniques. If you are fluent in English, you can tutor. Tutors do not need to speak a second language. Information, Mali Main, 206-382-4511.

VOLUNTEER CHORE SERVICE helps connect parishioners with people who need chore services and companionship. Volunteers help to ensure that elders and individuals with disabilities can remain in their homes among familiar surroundings rather than going to an institutionalized setting. If you are interested in this important ministry, sign up for the Orientation to be held in the Pastoral Outreach Center at 7:00 pm on Monday, December 10th. Information Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Our Lady of Guadalupe, Seattle; St. Mary of Guadalupe, Ridgefield; and Immaculate Conception, Arlington and Mount Vernon.
