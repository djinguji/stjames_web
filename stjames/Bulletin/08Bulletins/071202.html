Bulletin - December 2, 2007

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are—whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

LISTENING DEVICES. Did you know the Cathedral has an assistive listening system? If you have difficulty hearing the spoken word, please talk to an usher about borrowing a listening device to use during Mass.

CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE. Now available, the newest Cathedral CD, Receive the Song featuring our young people’s choir, the Schola Cantorum, directed by Stacey Sunde. We have Christmas cards, including our own Cathedral cards. We also have a fine selection of devotional books for Advent.

YOUNG ADULTS FIRST FRIDAY GATHERING is on December 7th. Our Music Director, Dr. James Savage has agreed to reprise his role as Cathedral Storyteller Extraordinaire and show-all Tour Guide. The evening will start in the Gallagher Center in the rectory basement amid the gallery of photos of Cathedral personalities for stories of our fascinating Cathedral history. Then we will move on to the Cathedral itself for a tour that includes places seen by few… Don’t miss this special event! Information TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018 or Young Adults.

SANDWICHES FOR ST. MARTIN DE PORRES SHELTER may be brought to the coffee hours next Sunday or to the parish office before 3:00 pm on Monday, December 10th. We always welcome new sandwich makers who want to share in this important ministry. Information Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

ST. MARTIN DE PORRES SHELTER TOUR. For many years, St. James volunteers have been making sandwiches and providing an overflow Winter Shelter for the guests at St. Martin de Porres Shelter. On Sunday, December 9th at noon, volunteers and interested parishioners will have the opportunity to tour the shelter. On the tour we will meet knowledgeable shelter staff, see how a shelter operates, and learn more about homelessness in Seattle and the work that Catholic Community Services is doing. Space is limited so please register. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

SACRIFICIAL GIVING 2008

During this season when we give thanks for all God’s gifts, it’s a good time to reflect on how we can share those gifts of time, talent, and treasure with our parish community. Please drop your 2008 commitment card in the offering basket today. If you've never made a commitment to Sacrificial Giving, please consider making one this year, the centennial year of our great cathedral. Our goal this year is not a dollar amount. Instead, we’re going for 100% parishioner participation! Every gift counts.

AIDS AND MS CARE TEAM

Are you interested in joining the St. James Care Team to provide companionship and assistance to persons living with AIDS or MS? CALLED TO SERVE? Are you a pastoral lay leader who feels called to formally serve the Church in collaboration with the Archbishop, priests and deacons? The Archdiocese of Seattle in partnership with the School of Theology and Ministry sponsors a program entitled Christifideles which includes acceptance and participation into the Master of Arts in Pastoral Studies at Seattle University and certification by the Archdiocese as a Lay Ecclesial Minister. If you are interested or would like more information, you are invited to one of two information meetings at Seattle University (12/8), or St. Martin’s in Fife (12/8). Information, Office of Catholic Faith Formation, 206-382-4096 or Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

DO YOU GIVE AT THE OFFICE? If so, you can support work going on right here at the St. James ESL Program. When you make a gift through the United Way or other workplace giving campaign, you can designate where it goes. Visit our website to see what a difference your donation can make. for United Way, simply fill in the line that asks which specific agency you want to designate with “St. James ESL Program.” If you work for Boeing or Microsoft, your gift will automatically be matched, doubling your donation. Washington Mutual and many other businesses will also match employee gifts. Information Christopher Koehler, 206-382-4511.
