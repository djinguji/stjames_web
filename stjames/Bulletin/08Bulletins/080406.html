 Easter III

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? Our Welcome Back program provides a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are offered on Mondays from 7:00-9:00 pm, April 7 through June 30 in Cathedral Place, 2nd floor. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

CATHOLIC 101: SACRAMENTS OF HEALING AND VOCATION. Join us on Tuesdays, April 8, 15, and 29, 1:00-3:00 or 7:00-9:00, Cathedral Place—second floor for an ongoing look at the newly published US Catechism for Adults. Helen Oesterle reviews the foundations of our Catholic faith from our American Catholic experience and facilitates lively conversations among the participants. (Focus on April 8: Sacrament of Anointing of the Sick) Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values. A new inquiry session will begin this Wednesday evening. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

VOLUNTEER CHORE SERVICE ORIENTATION. VCS helps connect parishioners with people who need chore services and companionship. Volunteers help to ensure that elders and individuals with disabilities and/or long-term illnesses can remain in their homes among familiar surroundings rather than going to an institutionalized setting. If you are interested in this rewarding ministry, please sign up for the Orientation to be held on the 2nd Floor of Cathedral Place at 4:00 pm on Sunday, April 6. Information Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

WORLD DAY OF PRAYER FOR VOCATIONS. On Sunday, April 13, the Church throughout the world prays that all the baptized may be faithful to their vocation in life, whether to the vocation of marriage, priesthood, diaconate, consecrated life, or single life. On Thursday, April 24, at 7:00 pm, a special “Come and See” for single men aged 16-30 who are interested in learning more about the priesthood will be held at St. Jude’s Church, Redmond. Information, Rich Shively, Vocations Office of the Archdiocese of Seattle, 206-382-4595.

ST JAMES CATHEDRAL’S THIRD ANNUAL HEALTH FAIR. Sunday, April 20, 11:00 am to 2:00 pm in Cathedral Hall. This year’s focus is on stewardship of our health and stewardship of our environment. Local health, environmental, civic and government organizations will be on hand to demonstrate and speak with you about our health and the health of our environment, and show how these are connected. Join us for a chance to collect valuable information and ask questions. There will be something for everyone, so bring family and friends! Sponsored by St. James Health and Healing Ministry and the Environmental Justice Group.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. On Thursday, April 24, at 7:00 pm in the Rectory Parlors (note date and place change!), you’re invited to gather for a discussion of Henri Nouwen’s Adam: God’s Beloved. This is a memoir about Nouwen’s friendship with Adam, a severely handicapped man he knew at the L'Arche Daybreak Community in Canada. Although Adam could not speak and was wracked with violent seizures, Nouwen called Adam "my friend, my teacher, and my guide.” Adam is available in the Cathedral Bookstore. Information, Maria Laughlin, 206-382-4284.

CATHEDRAL SCHOLARSHIP FUND FOR RAINBOW SCHOOLS’ STUDENTS. Our parish has established a scholarship fund to assist families in meeting the financial obligation involved in sending their elementary school age children to any one of the Rainbow Schools (Sts. George, Edward, Paul, Theresa, and Joseph). Applications are now available for the 2008-2009 school year. Families are required to be registered in our parish, have a Sacrificial Giving card on file (whether they are able to make a monetary pledge or not), and are active in the parish in some capacity. Applications are due April 30. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.
