Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • On this Feast of the Triumph of the Cross, we hear a hymn or canticle from St. Paul’s letter to the Philippians (2:6-11). The first part of the poem focuses on the humility of Christ, who emptied himself to take on our human flesh. The second part speaks of God’s action, raising Christ from death. At the very center, the turning point of the poem, is the word “cross.” When in your life have you encountered the cross? How did the encounter transform you?
  • St. Paul tells the Philippians, “have among yourselves the same attitude that is also yours in Christ Jesus” (Philippians 2:5). What do today’s readings reveal about Christ’s “attitude”? If we had that same attitude, what might be different about the way we live our lives?

From the Desk of the Pastor

September 14, 2008

Dear Friends,

I’m writing this on a beautiful Indian summer day that is considerably nicer than almost any day we had this past summer. In fact, I feel like I should put “summer” in quotation marks! But whether it seemed like summer or not, it’s almost over and fall is taking over nicely.

It’s always nice to be able to share good news. Two weekends ago, Paul Wilkes, a nationally acclaimed writer and filmmaker, was with us to speak about the Home of Hope, an orphanage for poor and abused girls in the south of India. At latest count, your gifts to this wonderful cause came to over $40,000, a remarkable response, but not a surprising one to someone who has been around long enough to know the generosity of the people of St. James Cathedral. And, in case you’re curious, it does look like there will be a cow named Mike. I can’t wait to get a picture!

The Archbishop Hunthausen Charity Golf Tournament is a week from Monday. It’s not too late to sign up to play and, for the non-golfers among you, the dinner afterwards has become a most enjoyable parish social event. Newcastle Golf Club is stunning, the dinner will be delicious, the auction fun, and the cause (our social outreach programs) couldn’t be better. (Registration materials are in today’s bulletin.)

Last week many of you took advantage of the opportunity to contribute to the campaign to defeat I-1000, the ballot initiative that would legalize physician-assisted suicide. On Monday evening, October 7, at 7:00 pm you will have an opportunity to learn more about the Initiative and the Church’s position from Sister Sharon Park, OP, Executive Director of the Washington State Catholic Conference. Sister Sharon is coordinating the response of the Catholic bishops of the State of Washington and is extremely articulate on the subject. Her presentation should be well worth hearing. The following Monday, October 14, Patty Bowman, director of Cathedral outreach ministries, will give a follow-up talk on the “consistent ethic of life,” the Church’s approach to a whole host of important questions that deal with the unique value of each and every human life. Both of these presentations are part of our Respect Life Month observance and should be very timely in light of the coming elections.

And speaking of “life” questions: Sister Helen Prejean, who became famous some years ago for her book (later a movie) “Dead Man Walking,” will present the second annual St. James Cathedral Lecture at 7:30 pm in Cathedral Hall on Thursday evening, October 2. You may remember that Vatican correspondent, John Allen, presented the first of these lectures last year at this time. It is a rare privilege for us to be able to host Sister Helen. Her message is a powerful one and a timely one, too. Because it will take place the same evening as the Vice-Presidential Debate between Senator Biden and Governor Palin (bad timing on our part, I know, but we had our event scheduled long before the debate!), we are going to make a ‘package deal’ of the evening: from 6:00-7:30 pm, those who wish to may gather in the Pastoral Outreach Center to watch the debate on TV. Feel free to bring a brown bag lunch if you like. Following the debate, Sister Helen will present her lecture. Any way you look at it, this will be a memorable evening!

Keep your seatbelts fastened. It’s going to be a busy fall!

Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor
