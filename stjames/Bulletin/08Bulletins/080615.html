Bulletin - June 15, 2008

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS IN THE CATHEDRAL are in honor of the graduation class of 2008 from Seattle University.

SACRED STEPS. Through July 28, in the Cathedral Chapel, St. James Cathedral is privileged to host an exhibit of paintings and photographs of the Camino to Santiago de Compostela. Today, immediately following the 10:00 am and 5:30 pm Masses, you are invited to a short guided tour of the exhibit. Gather in the Chapel after Mass. The tour will take 10-15 minutes.

PRIEST DAYS. During the coming week, June 16-19, the priests of the Archdiocese of Seattle will gather at Ocean Shores with Archbishop Brunett for their annual time of reflection. During this time, there will be just one Mass in the Cathedral each day, at 5:30 pm. At 8:15 am, we will have Morning Prayer in the Cathedral Chapel, and Midday Prayer will be offered as usual at 12:10 pm. The normal Mass schedule will resume on Friday, June 20.

CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE. Father Pat Howell’s powerful homilies on the Seven Last Words are available on CD. Get a deluxe Cathedral tote bag while supplies last… only three left! Or one of our best-selling Cathedral Music CDs — Receive the Song, Mozart’s Requiem, and many more.

CONGRATULATIONS AND GOD’S BLESSINGS. Last weekend we celebrated the Rite of Reception for Full Communion into the Catholic Church with Robert Dahlstrom, Shemaiah Gonzalez, Corinne Guay, John Leen, Sarah Mack, Debra Nagel, Julie Ritterman, and Natalie West. They made a Profession of Faith and celebrated the sacraments of Eucharist and Confirmation, and now join us as full members of our Catholic Christian family. We welcome them with great joy!

DYING TO LIVE. Join the ESL advisory board for this short documentary about migrants along the U.S.- Mexico border. This compelling film helps us come to know who these migrants are, and why they risk their lives to cross the border. Second floor, Cathedral Place, Tuesday, June 17, 6:30 pm, followed by discussion at 7:00 pm. Information, Christopher J. Koehler, 206-382-4511.

NIGHT OF 1,000 CONVERSATIONS. On June 19, thousands of people from across the country will gather in homes, offices, coffee shops, and places of worship to discuss immigration, civil liberties and human rights. Come join a conversation hosted by the ESL advisory board and learn more about Justice for Immigrants, the Catholic campaign for immigration reform. Learn about Catholic teachings that underpin this campaign, as well as proposals from USCCB to achieve reforms in our nations laws and policies that better reflect our values as a nation of immigrants. We will meet on Thursday, June 19 at 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Please RSVP to the ESL Program at 206-382-4511.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. On Tuesday, June 24, at 7:00 pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center, you’re invited to gather for a discussion of Garry Wills’ What Paul Meant. This study of Paul will challenge us to a deeper understanding of the great apostle as we begin a year dedicated to St. Paul (June 2008-June 2009). What Paul Meant is available in the Cathedral Bookstore. Information, Maria Laughlin, 206-382-4284.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action — all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin on Wednesday, July 9. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

SECOND CAR WASH FOR YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT. On July 6, teens from St. James, St. Mary’s, St. George’s, St. Edward’s, and St. Paul’s will head to Lynden, Washington to participate in the Archdiocese’s Youth Migrant Project mission trip together. The teens will volunteer in migrant daycare centers, run a Vacation Bible School and Reading Camp, experience farm work, and have time to reflect on their experiences. In order to help fund their trip, they are hosting a car wash on Sunday, June 29 from 9:30 am-1:00 pm at St. Paul’s (10001 57 Ave S., Seattle, 98178). Please plan on coming to get your car washed and support a great cause! Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

AMERICORPS OPENING! This is an opportunity not to be missed! The ESL Program has one current opening for an AmeriCorps member starting in September. The AmeriCorps will develop job skills in volunteer management and providing services to diverse populations. Specifically, the AmeriCorps member will provide English instruction to 1-3 groups of adult low-income refugees and/or immigrants in Seattle and South King County. Open until filled. To apply, you must first fill out and submit an AmeriCorps application available at http://www.kcls.org/literacyamericorps/index.cfm. Visit the ESL program website at www.stjames-cathedral.org/esl. Information, Christopher J. Koehler, 206-382-4511.

SUMMER FILM FESTIVAL. Join us on the July 8 in Cathedral Place, second floor at 7:00 pm for the return of this popular film series. This month we feature Juno, a smart, funny and ultimately moving comedy-drama with a strong pro-life message about an unwed teen who decides not to have an abortion, and promises the coming baby to a childless couple who long to adopt. Rated PG-13. Popcorn and lemonade will be provided and you are welcome to bring your favorite movie treats. Information, Helen Oesterle 206-382-4514.

YES, THE MARINERS ARE NOT HAVING A GREAT SEASON BUT… Join us anyway for a day of baseball and fun on Sunday, July 20 at 1:05 pm when our Mariners take on the Cleveland Indians. Our very own Jubilaté choir will be scheduled to sing the National Anthem! Tickets are $17. Deadline: June 18. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514, or complete the order form found on our website.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE. Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Thomas More, Lynnwood; and St. Aloysius, Buckley.
