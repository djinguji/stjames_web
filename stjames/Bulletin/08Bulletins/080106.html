WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are—whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

Join us on Tuesdays, January 15, 22, and 29, 1:00–3:00 pm or 7:00–9:00 pm, Cathedral Place — second floor for an ongoing look at the teachings of the Catholic Church. SUPPORT REFUGEES & IMMIGRANTS, ADD YOUR RECIPE TO THE COOKBOOK. (Now accepting recipes through February 2008) The St. James ESL cookbook, celebrating world cultures through recipes, will raise funds to support the St. James ESL Program in providing free English language instruction to low income adult refugees and immigrants in King County. By adding your recipe to the cookbook you also have the opportunity to share your family ethnicity and customs with the community. You can submit your recipe and any related stories or customs you’d like to admin@stjames-cathedral.org or St. James ESL, Attn: Cookbook, 804 Ninth Avenue, Seattle, WA 98104.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values — the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action — all with the support and involvement of the parish community. A new inquiry session will begin on January 9 on Wednesday evenings. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES

ST. JAMES WINTER SHELTER VOLUNTEERS NEEDED. Each Winter, from October through April, St. James operates an overflow shelter for 10-14 men from Catholic Community Services’ St. Martin de Porres Shelter. The men are 50 and over and cannot stay at St. Martin’s because of lack of space in the cold winter months. Pairs of volunteers serve as overnight and/or breakfast hosts once a month. We always welcome more breakfast cooks but are especially in need of volunteers who can serve as overnight hosts on a Friday, Saturday, Sunday, or Monday night. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

TEACH ENGLISH IN GREENWOOD. The St. James ESL Community Mentors Project is looking for an ESL Group Instructors to teach English a small group of Spanish-speakers at Broadview-Thomson Elementary in Greenwood. We provide a pre-service training in English language instruction techniques. Volunteers must be 18 years or older and fluent in English. Information, Mali Main, 206-382-4511.

RCIA HOSPITALITY VOLUNTEERS NEEDED to work one Wednesday evening a month to help set up, welcome, and clean up for our regular RCIA sessions. It is a great way to meet new people and provides important support for persons learning more about the Catholic Church. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

WOULD YOU CONSIDER BEING A RCIA SPONSOR? We continue to have many new inquirers coming to participate in our Christian Initiation process to learn more about the Catholic Church (RCIA). We need St. James parishioners to be companions with our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. All training and support will be provided and the time commitment is reasonable — a minimum of 2 sessions a month (Wednesday evenings). The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, Helen Oesterle, 206-382-4514.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Elizabeth Ann Seton, Bothell; and St. Mary, Castle Rock and Centralia.
