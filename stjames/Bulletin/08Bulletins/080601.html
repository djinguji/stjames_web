Bulletin - June 1, 2008

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

YOUTH MIGRANT PROJECT CAR WASH. On July 6, teens from St. James, St. Mary’s, St. George’s, St. Edward’s, and St. Paul’s will head to Lynden, Washington to participate in the Archdiocese’s Youth Migrant Project mission trip. The teens will volunteer in migrant daycare centers, run a Vacation Bible School and Reading Camp, experience farm work, and have time to reflect on their experiences. In order to help fund their trip, they are hosting two car washes. The first will take place on June 8 from 10:30 am-2:00 pm at St. Mary’s Church (611 20 Ave S., Seattle). Please plan on coming to get your car washed and support a great cause! Information, Jennifer Ibach 206 654-4640.

THE WAY OF COMPANIONSHIP. How can we reach out with love to those who are hurting? How can I see the face of Christ in the face of a homeless person? There are many times when we are called up to listen to another person, to hear their story and to offer some kind of help or support. Craig Rennebohm from the Mental Health Chaplaincy will present a training in the practice of companionship, a way of being gently present to another and sharing a little of the journey together. Tuesday, June 3, 7:00 pm in the Room 1E/W of Cathedral Place. Information and to register, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. On Tuesday, June 24, at 7:00 pm in the Mother Mary Rose Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center, you’re invited to gather for a discussion of Garry Wills’ What Paul Meant. This study of Paul will challenge us to a deeper understanding of the great apostle as we begin a year dedicated to St. Paul (June 2008-June 2009). What Paul Meant is available in the Cathedral Bookstore. Information, Maria Laughlin, 206-382-4284.

Are you interested in exploring the social justice tradition of the Catholic faith? Do you want to know more about the spiritual journey of Francis of Assisi, Dorothy Day, Jean Vanier, Oscar Romero, and the prophets? Consider taking part in our fifth year of the JustFaith program, starting in the fall. The program includes readings, videos, conversations about current issues, face-to-face encounters with the poor, and an investigation into the intersection of spirituality and Christian activism. To learn more, come to the Information Night on Wednesday, June 4 at 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

WINTER SHELTER TOILETRIES DONATIONS NEEDED. As part of their overnight stay at our Winter Shelter for men, our guests are given toiletries that they can use to freshen up before they head out for the day (many head to work in the mornings). We are able to provide these toiletries thanks to the generous donations given by St. James parishioners. Although Winter Shelter has recently ended for the season, we continue to collect donations over the summer. Please consider dropping off any of the following unused items in the bin at coffee hour over the next few months: disposable razors, small sewing kits, cough drops, economy-sized bottles of Tums and Tylenol. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

SUMMER COFFEE HOUR. Summer is coming, and we’ll soon be sipping coffee in the sunshine. Weather permitting, the Sunday morning coffee hours will take place in the Archbishop Murphy courtyard starting on June 1 and continuing through Labor Day weekend. But this can only happen with your help. Hosting coffee hour outdoors requires extra hands, and our regular coffee hour crew is already stretched. If you have enjoyed the summer coffee hour in the past, would you please consider helping us bring it back? We are looking for several outgoing people to help with set-up, serving and clean-up for this very important ministry of hospitality. It’s fun, it’s easy, and you might even get a suntan! Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.
