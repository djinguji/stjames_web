Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • We are so surrounded by turmoil these days – political, economic, social – that the first words of today’s second reading might sound naive. “Have no anxiety at all,” Paul says (Philippians 4:6). Paul does not want the Philippians to ignore or become indifferent to the tensions and trials of life. Instead, he urges them to focus all their energies on prayer: “in everything… make your requests known to God.” What are the sources of anxiety in your life? And what is the “true… honorable… just… pure… lovely… gracious” in you, and in the world around you?
  • Paul summarizes his message to the Philippians in these bold words: “Keep on doing what you have learned and received and heard and seen in me” (4:9). Paul lives so entirely in Christ that his words and actions serve as a model for others – a living Gospel. What changes can we make in our manner of speaking and acting to be more transparent witnesses to Christ?
