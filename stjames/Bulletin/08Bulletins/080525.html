Bulletin - May 25, 2008

 Corpus Christi

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are — whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

THE WAY OF COMPANIONSHIP. How can we reach out with love to those who are hurting? How can I see the face of Christ in the face of a homeless person? There are many times when we are called up to listen to another person, to hear their story and to offer some kind of help or support. Craig Rennebohm from the Mental Health Chaplaincy will present a training in the practice of companionship, a way of being gently present to another and sharing a little of the journey together. Tuesday, June 3, 7:00 pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information and to register, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

Scripture repeatedly calls us to care for the widows, the orphans and the strangers. Who are these people in our world today? What do I know about them? VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES

SUMMER COFFEE HOUR. Summer is coming, and we’ll soon be sipping coffee in the sunshine. Weather permitting, the Sunday morning coffee hours will take place in the Archbishop Murphy courtyard starting on June 1 and continuing through Labor Day weekend. But this can only happen with your help. Hosting coffee hour outdoors requires extra hands, and our regular coffee hour crew is already stretched. If you have enjoyed the summer coffee hour in the past, would you please consider helping us bring it back? We are looking for several outgoing people to help with set-up, serving and clean-up for this very important ministry of hospitality. It’s fun, it’s easy, and you might even get a suntan! Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

HELP CREATE A COMMUNITY OF COMPASSION. Are you interested in joining other St. James parishioners to provide companionship and assistance to a person living with HIV/AIDS? Multifaith Works has an unprecedented number of people waiting for volunteers. To get involved, join them for their day-long CareTeam training at First Baptist Church on June 14. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Blessed Sacrament, Seattle; Holy Disciples, Puyallup; St. Madeleine Sophie, Bellevue; and Visitation, Tacoma.
