HELP END HOMELESSNESS IN KING COUNTY. Seem impossible? Rich Gamble from the Archdiocesan Housing Authority’s Faithful Action in Transforming Homelessness (FAITH) program believes it can be done through ample, appropriate housing and that we have the ability to create enough of it in our county to end homelessness. Come to the Pastoral Outreach Center on January 31 at 7:00 pm to hear more about the FAITH program, King County’s 10-year Plan to End Homelessness, and the upcoming Homelessness and Housing Advocacy Day in Olympia. Discuss the issues and learn how you can get involved. Information, Jennifer Ibach 206-654-4640.

A SPIRITUAL JOURNEY TO TURKEY. Begin the Year of St. Paul by visiting his birthplace and many of the cities he visited in Asia Minor (according to the Acts of the Apostles). We also focus attention on the cities of the Book of Revelation, the cave dwellings and churches in Cappadocia, and the sites of several early councils of the Church. Three professors from the Jesuit School of Theology at Berkeley will offer biblical, historical, and art historical commentary. Regular celebration of Eucharist at holy sites, including Ephesus (House of Mary), Antioch, and Nicaea. For details www.TurkeyJourney2008.com or the Jesuit School of Theology at Berkeley, 510-549-5000, Father. John Endres, S.J.

VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES

ST. JAMES WINTER SHELTER VOLUNTEERS NEEDED. Each Winter, from October through April, St. James operates an overflow shelter for 10-14 men from Catholic Community Services’ St. Martin de Porres Shelter. The men are 50 and over and cannot stay at St. Martin’s because of lack of space in the cold winter months. Pairs of volunteers serve as overnight and/or breakfast hosts once a month. We always welcome more breakfast cooks but are especially in need of volunteers who can serve as overnight hosts on a Friday, Saturday, Sunday, or Monday night. Information, Jennifer Ibach.

VOLUNTEER CHORE SERVICE helps connect parishioners with people who need chore services and companionship. Volunteers help to ensure that elders and individuals with disabilities can remain in their homes among familiar surroundings rather than going to an institutionalized setting. If you are interested in this rewarding ministry, please sign up for the Orientation to be held in the Pastoral Outreach Center at 6:30 pm on Tuesday, January 22. Information, Jennifer Ibach, 206-654-4640.
