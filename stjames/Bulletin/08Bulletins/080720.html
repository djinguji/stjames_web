GLEANING is the traditional Biblical practice of gathering crops that would otherwise be left in the fields to rot or be plowed under after harvest. Monday through Friday, Cathedral Kitchen volunteers also glean. Five days a week we go early in the morning to four local grocery stores and collect the leftover (produce, dairy, deli, etc.) that the stores have been unable to sell. We use these giveaways as key ingredients in our evening meals that we serve daily to 150 needy people. If you would like to be a part of this gleaning tradition, please call Jim or Jill McAuliffe, 206-264-2091. We are also looking for new volunteers who may be interested in being a part of our daily cooking and prep teams.

Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • In today’s second reading, St. Paul says to the Romans, “we do not know how to pray as we ought” (Romans 8: 26). Do you feel that you “know how to pray”? Do you pray with words, or without? Is prayer difficult for you at times? Have you ever felt what Paul describes—the Spirit coming to the aid of your weakness?
  • At every Mass, we pray the General Intercessions, also called the Prayer of the Faithful, in which we bring before God the specific needs of our church, world, and local community, here and now. St. Paul tells us that the Spirit “searches hearts,” our hearts, and understands what we need and what is God’s will for us. What specific intentions do you bring with you today?
