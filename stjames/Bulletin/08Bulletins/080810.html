REGISTER TO VOTE. In their recent document Forming Consciences for Faithful Citizenship, the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops state: “In the Catholic tradition, responsible citizenship is a virtue; participation in the political process is a moral obligation.” If you are not yet registered to vote, you can do so at the coffee hours following the Sunday morning Masses today. To learn more about how we are called as Catholics to bring our consciences to bear on how we vote, visit the Faithful Citizenship website at http://www.faithfulcitizenship.org. Each week during the coming Year of St. Paul, watch this space in the bulletin for reflection questions that will lead us deeper into the writings and example of St. Paul. You are also invited to visit the Place of Prayer in the west aisle and on the website.

  • St. Paul’s letter to the Romans (9: 1-5) speaks of the gifts God has lavished on the Jewish people. Where does St. Paul find God’s presence, in the history and life of his “kindred,” the Israelites?
  • St. Paul speaks of his “great sorrow and constant anguish” at seeing his fellow Israelites “cut off from Christ.” All of us have suffered at not being able to share our faith with those we love, sometimes those closest to us. What is Paul’s response to this dilemma? What might ours be?
