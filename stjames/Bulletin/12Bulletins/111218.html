WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. Cathedral Christmas Cards are still available—but going fast! We also have some wonderful new Christmas books for children. Cathedral Music CDs make the perfect stocking stuffer! Please note that the Cathedral Bookstore will be closed the next two weekends in observance of the Christmas and New Year’s Day holidays.

OUR ST. JAMES “WARM HEARTS AND SOLES” 8th annual Advent sock drive for homeless men continues today, December 18. Your generosity is warming our hearts–we are doing well, but a little short of our goal of 2011 pairs for delivery in January to warm the soles of those in need. Advent is coming to a close, but let’s keep up our efforts by dropping off new socks at the sock collection table in Cathedral Hall after 8:00am, 10:00am, and 12:00 Sunday Masses—AND you can continue to drop off sock donations during the week at 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Such a worthwhile project—let’s keep those hearts and soles warm this winter! Information TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

ADVENT COMMUNAL PENANCE SERVICE will be held on Monday, December 19 at 7:30pm. During this beautiful service of readings, reflection and song, we will open ourselves to experience the gift of God’s gracious and loving forgiveness in the Sacrament of Penance. It has long been a tradition in the church to confess one’s sins before celebrating the great feast of Christmas. This will be the ideal time to do so.

THE SOLEMNITY OF THE DEDICATION OF ST. JAMES CATHEDRAL. St. James Cathedral was dedicated by Bishop Edward O’Dea on December 22, 1907, and rededicated by Archbishop Thomas J. Murphy on December 22, 1994. Each year, the Cathedral’s anniversary of dedication is celebrated throughout the Archdiocese. The solemn celebration of this special day will be at the 5:30pm Mass on Monday, December 21. The Mass begins with a special procession to light the dedication candles. All are welcome.

CHRISTMAS IS ON A SUNDAY THIS YEAR. Did you know that when Christmas falls on a Sunday, it has a major impact on our parish budget? Because we lose a weekend collection, we anticipate a loss of around $25,000 this year. Would you consider making your usual Sunday offering in addition to your Christmas offering next week? Two envelopes were provided for this purpose in your December packet. It will make a big difference!

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, January 11 at 7:00pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

WHAT ARE HOLIDAY BLUES? The holidays are supposed to be a joyful time of good cheer. Yet, it is not unusual for many of us to feel sad or lonely during the holiday period—a condition that has come to be called “holiday blues” or “holiday depression.” For more information, visit the Mental Health Ministry page.

Information, John Marquez, 206-274-3106.

WOULD YOU CONSIDER ASSISTING A PERSON who wants to become Catholic? We continue to have new inquirers coming to participate in our Christian Initiation process to learn more about the Catholic Church (RCIA). We need St. James parishioners to be companions with our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. The time commitment is reasonable–two Wednesday evening sessions a month. Training and support are provided. The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.
