WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. You are most welcome in this holy place.

NEW ON THE WEBSITE. Visit view albums of recent events, including last weekend’s Ordination to the Priesthood and wonderful Corpus Christi Procession.

INTRODUCING… CAMINO SEATTLE! This summer, in the weeks leading up to the Feast of St. James, the whole parish community is invited to embark on a five-week walking and prayer adventure called Camino Seattle. You’re invited to create your own walking and prayer goal and set out on “pilgrimage” on June 24. Participants will receive a passport, which you can fill with stamps each week during the Camino. All are welcome—why not get a friend to walk with you? Visit the Camino Seattle webpage for more information. Sign up after all Masses this weekend!

HELP A NEIGHBOR IN NEED. Many of our neighbors need help with transportation, shopping, laundry, and housework. Advanced age, chronic illness and financial difficulties can keep them from accomplishing these routine daily tasks on their own. By volunteering just a few hours a month with Volunteer Chore Services, you can help someone in our community continue living at own home. Find out more about VCS by attending a brief informational session TODAY Sunday, June 10 from 11:15 to 11:45 am in the Mother Mary Rose Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center (907 Columbia). Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

WILL YOU ASSIST A PERSON WHO WANTS TO BECOME CATHOLIC? We continue to have new inquirers coming to participate in our Christian Initiation process to learn more about the Catholic Church (RCIA). We need St. James parishioners to be companions with our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. The time commitment is reasonable – two Wednesday evening sessions a month. Training and support are provided. The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your own faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

ST. JAMES VOTER REGISTRATION PROJECT 2012. Some graduates of the St. James JustFaith class of 2009 are responding to the call of Catholic Social Teaching to promote participation in civic life by helping people in the cathedral neighborhood, particularly the poor and homeless, register to vote during this presidential election year. We are completely non-partisan and do not support or oppose any political party or issue. We need volunteers who can give 90-minutes of their time to help our neighbors get registered for the first time, confirm their prior registrations, or re-register at their current address. Information, Joseph Roberts or Teresa Acosta.

THE ST VINCENT DE PAUL conferences of St. James thank cathedral parishioners for their generous donations last month. During May we made a total of 57 home visits to our low income neighbors. Your contributions helped us to keep 37 individuals and families in their homes, and to prevent 6 utility shutoffs. We provided food for 17 households and clothes for 4 people. We also gave out 11 vouchers for beds and furniture, and helped 6 people with transportation costs. Members of the St. Vincent de Paul Society follow Jesus through service to those in need. We show our commitment through person-to-person contact, always making our home visits in pairs. Please consider joining our ministry to the poor among us. Information, Rob Millar, 206-954-8902 and Tammy Bendix, 206-595-9158.

The time commitment is once a month from 6:00-9:30pm. It is a great way to meet new people and support an important parish ministry. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Operation Nightwatch is an ecumenical Christian ministry providing hot meals and shelter referrals to 150-200 homeless people every night. St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner at the Nightwatch Center three times a month. We currently need a volunteer to cook dinner once a month. If you own or work at a restaurant, you could even arrange to have dinner cooked there and delivered to the Nightwatch facility in the evening. We also need an additional volunteer to serve and clean up on the third Tuesdays of the month. Information, Mary Nichols, 206-420-8788. To learn more about Operation Nightwatch, please visit the Operation Nightwatch website.

YESLER TERRACE REDEVELOPMENT. The St. James Housing Advocacy Committee is cosponsoring a public discussion of the Yesler Terrace Redevelopment Project with a panel of three City Council Members, Bruce Harrell, Tom Rasmussen, and Nick Licata on Saturday, June 30th from 11:00 am to 12:30 pm at Hilltop House, 1005 Terrace St. Parishioners are encouraged to attend to learn about the issues involved in the biggest affordable housing development in our neighborhood. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

Getting to know Good Pope John

On September 30, thanks to some generous parishioners, a permanent shrine to Blessed John XXIII, the Pope who called the Second Vatican Council, will be established in the north aisle of the Cathedral. Throughout the year, keep an eye on this box in the bulletin to learn more about this great saint of modern times.

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of Sacred Heart in Seattle, Bellevue, and Morton; St. Aloysius in Buckley; and St. Thomas More in Lynnwood.
