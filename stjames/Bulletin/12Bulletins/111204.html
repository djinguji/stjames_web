Advent II

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO. Stop by Cathedral Hall after the 10:00am Mass today for a latte or mocha. All proceeds in the month of December support our parish’s Pregnancy Support Ministry.

ADVENT BAZAAR TODAY. Stop by our annual Advent Bazaar in Cathedral Hall after the morning Masses. Vendors include Jubilee Traders, the L’Arche community, and Catholic Relief Services Work of Human Hands. This is a great opportunity to find alternative gifts from fair trade sellers. A Cathedral Advent calendar is free with every purchase!

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. Celebrate Advent with good spiritual reading for the season. Also in stock: Cathedral Christmas Cards!

OUR ST. JAMES “WARM HEARTS AND SOLES” 8th annual Advent sock drive for homeless men continues today, Sunday, December 4. Your generosity is warming our hearts as we collect socks toward our goal of 2011 pairs for delivery in January to warm the soles of those in need. Advent will be over before we know it, so let’s keep up our efforts by dropping off new socks at coffee hour in Cathedral Hall after 8:00am, 10:00am, and 12:00 Noon Sunday Masses, or during the week at 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Such a worthwhile project–let’s keep those hearts and soles warm this winter! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

SOLEMNITY OF THE IMMACULATE CONCEPTION. Masses for the Holy Day will be Wednesday, December 7 at 5:30pm, and Thursday, December 8 at 8:15am, 12:10pm, and 5:30pm. In addition, you are welcome to join the O’Dea High School community for their holy day Mass at 10:00am.

WINTER SHELTER DONATIONS. Our shelter guests need disposable razors, toothbrushes, chapstick, and new or gently used hand towels and wash cloths. Please bring your donations to Cathedral Hall during coffee hour after Sunday Mass or drop them off at the parish office Monday through Friday, 9am-5pm.

THE WINTER SHELTER NEEDS a few more people who can serve as overnight hosts once night a month through April. We also need one or two volunteers to help organize the toiletries we provide for our guests. This is a good service opportunity for high school students. Information, Suzanne Lee 206-654-4640.

JOYOUS DECEMBER. St. James invites you to four special musical events to celebrate Advent this December. Opus 7 Vocal Ensemble performs Britten’s monumental St. Nicolas with the Cathedral Chamber Orchestra and our Youth Music Program choirs on December 10 at 8:00 pm. Medieval Women’s Choir presents a program of works about light and peace on December 17, 8:00 pm. And our acclaimed Youth Music Program presents the traditional service of Readings and Carols on December 18, at 7:30 pm – free and open to all. For information about these and other events please visit the Cathedral Music website, call 206-382-4874, or email the Music Office.

NEW YEAR’S EVE. Passes are available now for our New Year’s Eve Gala, featuring the music of Bach and Mendelssohn, with the Cathedral’s professional singers – the Cathedral Cantorei, Cathedral Soloists, Cathedral Chamber Orchestra, and two organists, conducted by James Savage. Suggested donation is $25, students and seniors always pay as able. Use your credit card at the Cathedral Music website, or call 206-382-4874.

MENTAL HEALTH MINISTRY: PRAYER PARTNERS. Don’t be anxious about anything, but in everything, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God (Phil 4:6). We are a branch of the new Mental Health Ministry devoted to praying for those living with mental illness and emotional distress. We support the Mental Health Ministry by responding to special requests and needs that sometimes can only be answered with prayers. If you would like to request prayers for yourself, a loved one, or someone you care for go to Mental Health Ministry webpage and submit your prayer requests. All requests are strictly confidential.

THE FOOD BANK AT ST. MARY’S thanks all of the parishioners at St. James who contributed canned and dry goods on Thanksgiving Day. It was an overabundance of generosity and was very much appreciated by many in need.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, January 11 at 7:00pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

CATHEDRAL CONCERTS. Cathedral parishioners are always welcome to attend each and every concert given at St. James. The Cathedral Music Office has special pay-as-able Parishioner Passes available for all Cathedral concerts, including all of the special Advent music concerts in December. For a complete listing of music events throughout the year, please visit the Cathedral Music website. If you would like a Parishioner Pass please call the Music Office, 206-382-4874.

NEW LANGUAGE OF THE MASS. Last Sunday, along with English-speaking Catholics around the world we began using a new translation of the Roman Missal, the prayers of the Mass. If you would like to learn more about the changes, visit the Transitioning to the new Roman Missal page. Information, Corinna Laughlin, 206-622-3559 x3996.
