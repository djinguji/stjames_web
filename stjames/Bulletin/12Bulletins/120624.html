WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. You are most welcome in this holy place! Last week, we had visitors from Ann Arbor, MI; Miami, FL; Sugarland, TX, and many other places. The winner of our drawing was from Bethlehem, PA.

PETER’S PENCE. This weekend we are taking up the annual Peter’s Pence collection which provides support for the many worldwide ministries and programs of the Holy Father. Thank you for your generosity.

CAMINO SEATTLE BEGINS! This summer, in the weeks leading up to the Feast of St. James, the whole parish community is invited to embark on a five-week walking and prayer adventure called Camino Seattle. You’re invited to create your own walking and prayer goal and set out on “pilgrimage” today. Participants receive a passport, which you can fill with stamps each week during the Camino. All are welcome—why not get a friend to walk with you? Visit the Camino Seattle webpage for more information. Sign ups continue after each of the morning Masses today.

TEENS ON THE CAMINO. Young people from 7th-12th grade are invited to form a team and participate in Camino Seattle together. In addition to walking together, teens are invited to pool their miles and challenge other groups in the parish to match them! Information, Christina Buchholz.

MASS FOR DECEASED BISHOPS. On Thursday, June 28, at 5:30pm, we will have our annual Mass for the Deceased Bishops of the Archdiocese of Seattle. This is a time to remember the shepherds who led this local Church for more than 100 years. Archbishop Sartain will preside. All are welcome.

VATICAN II LECTURE SERIES: CHILDREN OF GOD TOGETHER. The third in our Vatican II lecture series will be Thursday, June 28 at 7:00pm. Father William Treacy, a pioneer in ecumenism and interfaith relations in the Archdiocese of Seattle, will speak about the Council’s vision of unity. All are welcome. Information, Vatican II Lecture Series page.

YESLER TERRACE REDEVELOPMENT. The St. James Housing Advocacy Committee is cosponsoring a public discussion of the Seattle Housing Authority’s proposed Yesler Terrace Redevelopment Project on Saturday, June 30 from 11:00 am to 12:30pm at Hilltop House, 1005 Terrace Street. Speakers will include a panel of three City Council Members: Bruce Harrell, Tom Rasmussen, and Nick Licata. Parishioners are encouraged to attend to explore the issues involved in the largest affordable housing development in our neighborhood. Information, Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640.

JUSTFAITH. Do you long to lead an extraordinary life of compassion? Will you help transform the world through love and service – and be transformed in the process? The JustFaith program is a small-group process that offers an opportunity to experience a spiritual journey into compassionate discipleship. JustFaith provides a lively and challenging format to engage and be formed by our Catholic faith tradition that changes lives, inspires compassionate witness and transforms the world through modeling the love and justice of God in Jesus Christ. The program includes readings, videos, conversations about the most difficult topics, face-to-face encounters with the poor, and an investigation into the intersection of spirituality and Christian activism. Come learn more at the Information Night Monday, July 9 at 6:30pm in the Pastoral Outreach Center. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

ST. JAMES VOTER REGISTRATION PROJECT 2012. Some graduates of the St. James JustFaith class of 2009 are responding to the call of Catholic Social Teaching to promote participation in civic life by helping people in the cathedral neighborhood, particularly the poor and homeless, register to vote during this presidential election year. We are completely non-partisan and do not support or oppose any political party or issue. We need volunteers who can give 90-minutes of their time to help our neighbors get registered for the first time, confirm their prior registrations, or re-register at their current address. Information, Joseph Roberts or Teresa Acosta.

FLORAL DONATIONS. Floral donations are a wonderful way to remember a deceased loved one or commemorate an important anniversary, and at the same time enrich the whole parish community! If you would like to offer flowers, contact Elizabeth Falzone, 206-654-4650.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Operation Nightwatch is an ecumenical Christian ministry providing hot meals and shelter referrals to 150-200 homeless people every night. St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner at the Nightwatch Center three times a month. We currently need a volunteer to cook dinner once a month. If you own or work at a restaurant, you could even arrange to have dinner cooked there and delivered to the Nightwatch facility in the evening. We also need an additional volunteer to serve and clean up on the third Tuesdays of the month. Information, Mary Nichols, 206-420-8788. To learn more about Operation Nightwatch, please visit the Operation Nightwatch website.

LIVING SOLIDARITY. The Gospel and Catholic Social Teaching remind us that we are responsible for both ourselves and the common good. One of the ways in which we do this is through government, taxes and the federal budget. What kind of society do we wish to have? It is vital that all of us engage this question by bringing our values–not our opinions—to the many conversations about the economy and the federal budget. Being informed about and engaged with these issues is part of our responsibility as citizens and as people of faith. Living Solidarity: Government, the Federal Budget and the Common Good is an 8-week exploration of these issues, including reading and small group dialogue. Thursday evenings, July 12 through August 30. Registration required. Information, or to register, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.
