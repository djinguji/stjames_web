WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. You are most welcome in this holy place! Last week, we had visitors from Ridley Park, PA; Crystal Lake, IL; Dhaka, Bangladesh, and many other places. The winner of our drawing was from St. John, IN.

SUMMER CHOIR. Come one, come all to support the musical prayer of the assembly at 12:00 Noon Mass each Sunday of August. There is a rehearsal in the Cathedral Choir Room at 11:15am on Sundays before the Mass.

TEACHER BLESSING AT THE NOON MASS. PRAYER WITH MUSIC FROM TAIZÉ. Each Friday at 6:30pm in the Cathedral, all are invited to join in an ecumenical service of readings, silence, candlelight, and the music of the ecumenical community of Taizé in France. This simple, reflective prayer is the perfect way to end a busy week.

THE CATHEDRAL KITCHEN is looking for three new gleaners. The commitment is one morning a week. Each morning, two volunteers take the Cathedral van and go to three PCC Markets and the Madison Market, to collect and sort produce, dairy, deli, and dry goods. These donated food items are used in our daily meal. The food that we are unable to use at the Cathedral Kitchen is passed on to St. Mary’s Food Bank. Nothing goes to waste! If you would like to become involved in this outreach, contact Jill McAuliffe, 206-264-2091.

Must be available Monday, Wednesday or Friday from 1:00 to 4:00 pm. The Solanus Casey Center serves approximately 300 people per month. Your help is essential to continuing and expanding the work of this important outreach ministry. Information, Sr. Peggy Kennedy, 206-223-0907.

HELP FEED THE HUNGRY. Several times every month, teams of St. James parishioners cook and serve dinner for 150-170 homeless people at the Operation Nightwatch Center near Seattle University. We urgently need a volunteer to prepare dinner once a month—on either the 1st or the 3rd Tuesday from 5:00 to 8:30 pm. During October, one of our longtime cooks will be on vacation, so we will be in special need of assistance. Please consider joining this deeply rewarding ministry. Information, Mary Nichols, 206-420-8788 or Suzanne Lee, 206-654-4640. Learn more at the Operation Nightwatch website.

ST. JAMES IS A PLACE FOR CHILDREN. We encourage all of our parishioners who attend Mass with young children, and we welcome them into our midst. Many families find that sitting in the front rows, where the view is unobstructed, allows children to participate more fully. Others are more comfortable sitting at the ends of the aisles, so that they can easily step into the west vestibule or the Cathedral Chapel, if their child needs a quiet place away from the assembly. We have provided a sound system in these areas so you can continue to participate in Mass. We appreciate your effort to be with us and to include your child in our worship. For those who wish, babysitting is available in the Mother Cabrini Room at the Pastoral Outreach Center during the 10:00 am and 12 Noon Masses.

ARE YOU A REGISTERED PARISHIONER AT ST. JAMES? Many who attend St. James regularly and consider the Cathedral their parish have never officially registered. We strongly encourage you to register. Registered parishioners receive important mailings from the Cathedral (including offering envelopes!), are regular recipients of the Cathedral’s quarterly journal In Your Midst, are able to celebrate their wedding at the Cathedral, and their children may attend any of the five Catholic Rainbow Schools at parishioner rates. Registration cards can be found in the vestibule. If you are not sure whether or not you’re registered, call the Parish Office, 206-622-3559.

Getting to know Good Pope John

On September 30, thanks to some generous parishioners, a permanent shrine to Blessed John XXIII, the Pope who called the Second Vatican Council, will be established in the north aisle of the Cathedral.

Once somebody asked Pope John what he would like to do after the Council had finished its labors. He replied, “Spend a whole day tilling the fields with my brothers!”

Recounted in The Wit and Wisdom of Good Pope John
