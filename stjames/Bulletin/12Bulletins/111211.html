WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

NEW IN THE BOOKSTORE. Celebrate Advent with good spiritual reading for the season, including Advent with the Holy Fathers. Also in stock: Cathedral Christmas Cards! We also have some wonderful new Christmas books for children.

HUNTHAUSEN FUND CHRISTMAS CARDS. This year, give a gift that will make a real difference in the life of a homeless family! The Hunthausen Fund celebrates its tenth anniversary this year. This unique fund provides grants and no-interest loans to homeless families to help them move into permanent housing. Since October 2001, the Fund has helped more than 380 homeless families into homes of their own. Give a gift to the Hunthausen Fund in honor of someone special this year. See the order form in this bulletin for more information, or contact Maria Laughlin, 206-382-4284.

OUR ST. JAMES “WARM HEARTS AND SOLES” 8th annual Advent sock drive for homeless men continues today, Sunday, December 11. Advent will be drawing to a close before we know it, so let’s keep up our efforts by dropping off new socks at the Young Adult table in Cathedral Hall after 8:00am, 10:00am, and 12:00 Noon Sunday Masses, or during the week at 2nd floor, Cathedral Place. Such a worthwhile project–let’s keep those hearts and soles warm this winter! Information Young Adults or TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

ADVENT PENANCE SERVICES will be held on Saturday, December 17 at 4:00pm and Monday, December 19 at 7:30pm. During these beautiful services of readings, reflection and song, we will open ourselves to experience the gift of God’s gracious and loving forgiveness in the Sacrament of Penance. It has long been a tradition in the church to confess one’s sins before celebrating the great feast of Christmas. These will be ideal times to do so.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, January 11 at 7:00pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

WHAT ARE HOLIDAY BLUES? The holidays are supposed to be a joyful time of good cheer. Yet, it is not unusual for many of us to feel sad or lonely during the holiday period—a condition that has come to be called “holiday blues” or “holiday depression.” For more information, visit the Mental Health Ministry page.

THANK YOU AND MERRY CHRISTMAS. Last Wednesday PEMCO spoiled us at the Cathedral Kitchen with a special Christmas dinner for our guests. This has become an annual event that we all look forward to. The chicken breasts, mashed potatoes, fresh vegetables, rolls, and beautiful holiday cakes and cookies were delicious. PEMCO employees not only prepared the dinner, they also set the tables with poinsettias and served our guests milk, coffee, and sparkling cider. The McDonald’s gift cards packed into warm gloves were also very much appreciated by all. Thank you, PEMCO!
