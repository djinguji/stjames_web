WELCOME, SUMMER VISITORS! St. James is proud to welcome visitors from near and far throughout the summer months. Each week a drawing for a special Cathedral gift will be made from among all the visitor envelopes used. Please write your address on the visitor envelope provided in the pew and drop it with your offering into the collection basket. Thank you for adding to the power of our prayer by your presence in our midst today. You are most welcome in this holy place! Last week, we had visitors from Jersey City, NJ; Orange, CA; Duncan, BC, and many other places. The winner of our drawing was from Loganville, GA.

CATHEDRAL PAINT AND REPAIR. You may have noticed that a significant paint and repair project is taking place in the west nave of the Cathedral. In the 18 years since the Cathedral was renovated, there has been some water damage at the upper level and normal wear and tear at the lower level. Some gifts from generous parishioners have made it possible for us to take on this project.

CAMINO STORY NIGHT. Join us on Tuesday, July 17 at 7:00pm in the Cathedral Chapel, for an evening with pilgrims who have walked the Camino to Santiago de Compostela. They will share the highlights (and the low points!) of the experience, and talk about the spiritual meaning of the pilgrimage as well as some of the practical ins and outs. If you are considering doing the Camino, or if you just want to travel vicariously, this evening is for you. There will be plenty of time for questions and answers as well.

BOAT TOUR OF DUWAMISH WATERWAY. The St. James Environmental Justice Committee is joining with the Duwamish River Cleanup Coalition and other Seattle faith communities in a prayerful and reflective boat tour of the Lower Duwamish River. The tour will provide an opportunity to learn about the state of Seattle’s major river as well as its people, and to explore ways to live out our call to practice environmental justice. Saturday, August 18, 10:00. Registration required; suggested donation: $20 presale, $25 day of tour. Information and to register: Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session began last week, but it’s not to late to join! Wednesday, July 18 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

CATHEDRAL BOOK GROUP. Join us on Tuesday, July 24 at 7:00pm in the Holy Names Room, Pastoral Outreach Center, for a discussion of Father Kevin Codd’s illuminating account of his pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela, To the Field of Stars.
