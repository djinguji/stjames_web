WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Find out more about VCS by attending a brief informational session Sunday, June 10 from 11:15 to 11:45 am in the Mother Mary Rose Room of the Pastoral Outreach Center (907 Columbia). ST. JAMES VOTER REGISTRATION PROJECT 2012. Some graduates of the St. James JustFaith class of 2009 are responding to the call of Catholic Social Teaching to promote participation in civic life by helping people in the cathedral neighborhood, particularly the poor and homeless, register to vote during this presidential election year. We are completely non-partisan and do not support or oppose any political party or issue. We need volunteers who can give 90-minutes of their time to help our neighbors get registered for the first time, confirm their prior registrations, or re-register at their current address. Information, Joseph Roberts or Teresa Acosta.

THE ST VINCENT DE PAUL conferences of St. James thank cathedral parishioners for their generous donations last month. During May we made a total of 57 home visits to our low income neighbors. Your contributions helped us to keep 37 individuals and families in their homes, and to prevent 6 utility shutoffs. We provided food for 17 households and clothes for 4 people. We also gave out 11 vouchers for beds and furniture, and helped 6 people with transportation costs. Helping our neighbors pay their rent cost us an average of $3.90 per bed night. This is far cheaper than picking up the pieces after people lose their homes. Shelters cost $15-$20 per night, and motels cost $50 or more. Members of the St. Vincent de Paul Society follow Jesus through service to those in need. We show our commitment through person-to-person contact, always making our home visits in pairs. Please consider joining our ministry to the poor among us. Information, Rob Millar, 206-954-8902 and Tammy Bendix, 206-595-9158.

INTRODUCING… CAMINO SEATTLE! This summer, in the weeks leading up to the Feast of St. James, the whole parish community is invited to embark on a five-week walking and prayer adventure called Camino Seattle. You’re invited to create your own walking and prayer goal and set out on “pilgrimage” on June 24. Participants will receive a passport, which you can fill with stamps each week during the Camino. All are welcome—why not get a friend to walk with you? Visit the Camino Seattle webpage for more information. Sign ups will be next Sunday, June 17 after all Masses.

WILL YOU ASSIST A PERSON WHO WANTS TO BECOME CATHOLIC? We continue to have new inquirers coming to participate in our Christian Initiation process to learn more about the Catholic Church (RCIA). We need St. James parishioners to be companions with our new inquirers to help welcome them to our community. The time commitment is reasonable – two Wednesday evening sessions a month. Training and support are provided. The rewards are many, including renewal and update of your own faith and “kudos” in heaven. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

Getting to know Good Pope John

On September 30, thanks to some generous parishioners, a permanent shrine to Blessed John XXIII, the Pope who called the Second Vatican Council, will be established in the north aisle of the Cathedral. Throughout the year, keep an eye on this box in the bulletin to learn more about this great saint of modern times.

“We all like to judge events from the vantage point of the handful of earth beneath our feet. This is a great illusion. We must take our view from the heights and courageously embrace the whole.”
