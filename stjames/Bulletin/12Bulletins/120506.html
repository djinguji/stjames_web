MAY IS MENTAL HEALTH MONTH. One in every five Americans experiences a mental disorder in any given year. Mental illnesses are far more common than cancer, diabetes, heart disease, or arthritis. People living with mental health problems are our neighbors; members of our parishes; members of our families. Mental Health Ministry offers two special events this month: Today, May 6: Informational MHM Table at all coffee hours. There will be brochures and information available, and volunteers standing by to answer questions about mental health or the ministry. Tuesday, May 22: Information Night, 6:30-8:30pm. Learn about stigma and myths related to mental illness. Gain more in-depth knowledge about mental health and illness. Hear personal stories of living with mental illness. To register or for more information, Beth Rose at 206-382-4269.

Getting to know Good Pope John

Later this year, thanks to some generous parishioners, a permanent shrine to Blessed John XXIII, the Pope who called the Second Vatican Council, will be established in the north aisle of the Cathedral. Throughout the year, keep an eye on this box in the bulletin to learn more about this great saint of modern times.

During a visit to the Regina Coeli prison in Rome, the Pope requested that the inmates be allowed to leave their cells so that he might address them in the main courtyard of the prison. “I have come,” he said, “you have seen me. I have looked into your eyes, I have placed my heart alongside your hearts.” Among the prisoners who were allowed to approach the Pope, there were two murderers. One of them, after kissing the Pontiff’s ring, looked up at him with deep sadness on his face. “Are those words of hope you have given us meant for such a great sinner as I am?” In response the Pope bent over the convict and embraced him.

Recounted in The Wit and Wisdom of Good Pope John
