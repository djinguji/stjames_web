September 9, 2012

Dear Friends,

It has been quite a summer at St. James Cathedral, and anything but quiet. Over the past three months or so, we celebrated a lot of weddings and, sadly, a record number of funerals, giving back to God some wonderful parishioners and friends.

We also finished up the Annual Catholic Appeal remarkably well, even though we fell short of the goal we were hoping for. For a number of reasons, it was a difficult time to promote the Appeal, but, thanks to the generosity of more than 950 of you, we did remarkably well. In the end, we were only $20,000 short of our combined archdiocesan and parish goal of $526,676.

During June and July, several hundred of you participated in the Camino Seattle, exploring the beauties of the city and the surrounding area, praying along the way, meeting new friends, and maybe even shedding a pound or two! It all culminated at the annual celebration of the Feast of St. James, when we pulled out all the stops at the Sunday Masses and enjoyed a fabulous parish picnic afterwards on a picture-perfect July Sunday. Special thanks to Trent and Alex Mummery and Stephen Pace, along with an army of great volunteers who made it all happen.

Then there was Choir Camp when the Cathedral and the entire campus echoed with the happy and beautiful sounds of more than 50 young people from ages 9 to 14. If you want to get an idea of what Choir Camp is all about (my words will not do it!), I suggest you check out the Cathedral website. You’ll be mightily impressed, believe me.

And now, the realities of fall -- back to school, and back to work -- are staring us in the face. Here at the parish, this is going to mean a few changes that I am excited to be able to share with you.

After a good deal of praying and planning, and in response to things you told us in parish survey that we conducted back in the fall of 2010, we are reorganizing the way we minister to families. Of course, we have always ministered to families, assisting young couples preparing for marriage, following them through the early years of family life with the arrival of children and the celebration of baptism, and continuing into the years of children’s faith-formation. The idea of the new ministry is to consolidate some work that had been spread among several staff members and to coordinate these existing ministries more effectively while expanding marriage enrichment programs, increasing support for families experiencing difficulties and challenges, and assisting divorced Catholics who seek healing and understanding from the Church.

I am pleased to announce that Suzanne Lee, who has been ministering in our social outreach and advocacy programs with Patty Bowman for the past few years, has accepted the challenge of building and coordinating this new ministry. I am grateful to Suzanne for her willingness – and eagerness – to take on this major set of responsibilities.

Taking up some of Suzanne’s former duties and involvements in the social outreach and advocacy area is the newest member of our team, Caitlin Lanigan. I am delighted to welcome Caitlin to St, James and to our staff. She is a graduate of Holy Cross College and has just completed a year serving at Seattle’s Urban Rest Stop as a Jesuit Volunteer. I know Caitlin will bring energy, ideas, and a strong commitment to our Outreach ministries and I also know that our many volunteers will enjoy working with her. If you’re at the hospitality after one of the morning Masses today, I hope you’ll take a moment to introduce yourself and welcome her to St. James!


Father Michael G. Ryan
Cathedral Pastor

Bishop McElroy is a distinguished scholar, church leader, and pastor, whose writings on contemporary issues facing the Church—including religious liberty, war and peace, and other issues especially relevant during this election year—have deservedly gained national attention. It is a privilege to welcome Bishop McElroy to St. James and I hope many of you will avail yourselves of the opportunity to hear him speak.

WINTER SHELTER NEEDS OVERNIGHT HOSTS AND BREAKFAST COOKS. St. James Cathedral will soon start its 21st season partnering with St. Martin de Porres Shelter to provide overflow sleeping space during the cold winter months. From Friday night through Monday night, from October through April, we provide a warm refuge in Cathedral Hall for 10-15 men. Nearly 100 cathedral parishioners serve as overnight hosts and breakfast cooks. Please consider joining our Winter Shelter Team. Training for new and returning volunteers will be held Sunday, September 23 from 6:30 to 8:30pm in Cathedral Hall. Information, Caitlin Lanigan, 206-382-4235.

COOKIES AND BROWNIES NEEDED. The Cathedral Kitchen prepares anywhere from 175 to 250 sack lunches a week for those who choose not to come in the hall for a hot meal. These sack lunches usually include a sandwich, chips, a piece of fruit, cookies. Recently we have been short on desserts. Would you consider sharing your favorite homemade/bought cookies or brownies with us? Drop donations off a the Cathedral Hall, Monday–Friday, 10:00am-6:00pm. Information, Jill McAuliffe, 206-264-2091.

DO YOU ENJOY SHARING YOUR FAITH WITH YOUNG PEOPLE? Please consider joining the team for RCIA/C (Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults - Adapted for Older Children). Catechists help the children learn about being a Catholic Christian during Sunday morning classes (10:30am–11:45am). Dismissal Leaders accompany the children as they “feast” on the Scriptures during the Noon Mass. Materials and training provided. Information, Brenda Bellamy, 206-274-3108.

THE CATHEDRAL KITCHEN is looking for three new gleaners. The commitment is one morning a week. Each morning, two volunteers take the Cathedral van and go to three PCC Markets and the Madison Market, to collect and sort produce, dairy, deli, and dry goods. These donated food items are used in our daily meal. Information Jill McAuliffe, 206-264-2091.

MENTAL HEALTH MINISTRY PRAYER PARTNERS are devoted to praying for anyone living with mental illness or emotional distress – either in you, a loved one, or someone you care for. The Prayer Partners help support the Mental Health Ministry by responding to special requests and needs that sometimes can only be answered with prayers. You are welcome to submit your requests online.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, September 12 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? Our Welcome Back program provides a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are offered on Mondays, September 17 through December 10, 7:00-9:00pm on 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.
