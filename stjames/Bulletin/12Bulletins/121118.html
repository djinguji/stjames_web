WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS AT THE ALTAR are from the Guido family in loving memory of James Nicholas Guido. If you would like to offer flowers, contact Jane Mueller, 206-654-4650.

CATHEDRAL ESPRESSO. Please come by after the 10:00 Mass. All proceeds for the month of November benefit the Environmental Justice Group. The Espresso Ministry is also looking for volunteers willing to run the cart after the noon Mass. Information, Katherine Berry, 425-772-0897.

CATHEDRAL BOOKSTORE. Sunday Missals for 2013 are now in stock! Also, some wonderful new prayer books for young children.

MASS OF THANKSGIVING DAY. On Thursday, November 22, there will be one Mass in the Cathedral at 9:30am. The Thanksgiving Day Mass is a long and wonderful tradition at St. James Cathedral. Please bring with you bags of non-perishable canned and boxed food items to be shared with needy neighbors. During the Mass, you will be invited to bring your gifts forward to the altar.

DOES YOUR HUNGER FOR THE WORD OF GOD extend beyond Sundays? Do you long to prayerfully engage the Bible in a group setting? Then Bible Study might be for you. Beginning on Tuesday, November 20 at 6:30 pm and continuing on successive Tuesday evenings into Advent, an opportunity to read and reflect on Sacred Scripture will be offered in Cathedral Place, Room 1E-W. After a break at Christmas, we’ll continue in Lent. Information John Marquez, 206-654-4646.

SHRINE HONORING BLESSED POPE JOHN XXIII. A plaque commemorating the blessing of the new shrine will soon be placed in the Cathedral’s west vestibule. It is not too late to make a donation. Donations of $500 or more will be acknowledged on the permanent marker. You can be a part of this important project celebrating the 50th Anniversary of the opening of the Second Vatican Council. The deadline is Monday, November 26. Information, Maria Laughlin, Director of Stewardship and Development, 206-382-4284.

NAMI FAMILY-TO-FAMILY EDUCATION PROGRAM is a free, 12-week course for family caregivers of individuals with mental illnesses on Wednesday evenings starting November 28, 6:00-8:00pm in the Gallagher Center. You must register to attend. Deadline for registration is November 20. The course includes information about mental illness as well as practical tools for living with mental illness and the mentally ill, and guidance on locating services within the community. Information, Rick Martin, Interim Parish Mental Health Ministry Director, 206-382-4269 or sign up at the Mental Health Ministry’s table at today’s Ministries Fair.

HAVE YOU BEEN CONFIRMED? Adults and youth aged 16 and older are invited to complete their Christian initiation beginning in early 2013. Confirmation classes start Thursday evening January 17 and continue weekly through April 11. The Rite of Confirmation will be celebrated in the context of the 5:30pm Mass Saturday, April 13. Information, Brenda Bellamy, 206-274-3108.

DO YOU GIVE AT THE OFFICE? Help refugees and immigrants to learn English, become citizens, and connect to community resources. When you make a gift through the United Way or other workplace giving campaign, you can designate the St. James ESL Program. For United Way, simply write in “St. James ESL Program” for the specific agency you want to designate. If you work for Boeing or Microsoft, your gift will automatically be matched, doubling your donation. Visit the ESL website to see what a difference your donation can make. Information, Christopher Koehler, 206-382-4511.

TOILETRY DONATIONS. We are collecting donations of toiletries for our Winter Shelter and for the Solanus Casey Center. Please bring in donations of shaving cream, razors, soaps, lotions, etc. Drop them off at Coffee Hour after masses or at the parish office M-F 9am-5pm.

MEET OTHER PARISHIONERS. Join with hundreds of St. James parishioners in our parish’s upcoming production of Great Music for Great Cathedrals. We need folks who can volunteer a couple of hours or a couple of weeks and everything in between. We need costume sewers, stage crew, lighting assistants, office support, etc. This is a great way to get to know other parishioners and make new friendships. Information, Music Office, 206-382-4874.

KEEP PERSPECTIVE ON THE CHAOS of the holidays with advent meditation. Join our Advent Meditation group starting either on Tuesday, November 27 at 10:30am-noon or Thursday, November 29 at 7:00-8:30pm–same session offered twice each week for participant convenience. Both offered at Cathedral Place. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

CALLING ALL YOUNG ADULTS.
