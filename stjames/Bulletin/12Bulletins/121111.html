NAMI FAMILY-TO-FAMILY EDUCATION PROGRAM is a free, 12-week course for family caregivers of individuals with mental illnesses on Wednesday evenings starting November 28, 6:00-8:00pm in the Gallagher Center. You must register to attend. Deadline for registration is November 20. The course includes information about mental illness, up-to-date information about medications, and current research related to the biology of brain disorders. In addition, it offers practical tools for living with mental illness and the mentally ill, with techniques for problem solving, listening, and communication, strategies for handling crises and relapse, care for the caregiver, and guidance on locating services within the community. Information, Rick Martin, Interim Parish Mental Health Ministry Director, 206-382-4269 or sign up at the Mental Health Ministry’s table at today’s Ministries Fair.

THE ST VINCENT DE PAUL conferences of St. James thank cathedral parishioners for their generous donations last month. During October we made 65 home visits to our low income neighbors. Your contributions allowed us to help 41 individuals and families pay their rent and stay safely in their homes. In addition, we prevented 5 utility shut-offs, provided food for 17 families, beds and furniture for 3 households, clothes for 2 people, and transportation assistance for 5 individuals. Our priority is preventing homelessness. This makes your contributions go a long way. Helping our neighbors pay their rent is far cheaper than picking up the pieces after they lose their homes. Members of the St. Vincent de Paul Society follow Jesus through service to those in need. Bearing witness to Christ’s compassionate and liberating love, we show our commitment through person-to-person contact, making our home visits in pairs. Please consider joining our ministry to the poor among us. Information, Rob Millar 206-954-8902 and Tammy Bendix 206-595-9158.
