THE ST VINCENT DE PAUL conferences of St. James thank cathedral parishioners for their generous donations last month. During April we made a total of 51 home visits to our low income neighbors. Your contributions helped us keep 32 individuals and families in their homes, and prevent 9 utility shut-offs. We provided food for 22 households and clothes for 4 people. In addition, we gave out 11 vouchers for beds and furniture, and helped 5 people with transportation costs. Our priority is preventing homelessness, which makes your contributions go a long way. Helping our neighbors pay their rent costs us an average of $6 per bed night. This is far cheaper than picking up the pieces after people lose their homes. Information, Rob Millar 206-954-8902 and Tammy Bendix 206-595-9158.

Getting to know Good Pope John

Later this year, thanks to some generous parishioners, a permanent shrine to Blessed John XXIII, the Pope who called the Second Vatican Council, will be established in the north aisle of the Cathedral. Throughout the year, keep an eye on this box in the bulletin to learn more about this great saint of modern times.

John XXIII detested solitude, particularly during meals. Although protocol required that a pope dine alone, he could not resign himself to the rule. “I look like a seminarian under punishment,” complained the Pope. “I have read the Gospel over carefully without finding a single passage which prescribes that one should eat alone. As we know, Jesus loved to eat in company.”

Recounted in The Wit and Wisdom of Good Pope John
