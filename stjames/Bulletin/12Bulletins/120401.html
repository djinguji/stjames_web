Passion (Palm) Sunday

WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

LENTEN PRAYER PARTNERS FOR THE ELECT. The Church tells us that it is the entire parish’s responsibility to support and welcome those preparing for baptism this Easter. Notes and cards will be collected during Holy Week to be given to the Elect before Easter. Please consider writing a note of support and welcome to one or all of our Elect. (You can “meet” them by viewing the Book of the Elect in the Cathedral or on our parish website.) Notes, cards, and gifts may be left at the parish office during the week, or dropped in the special bin in the north aisle beginning on Holy Thursday. Information, TerryAnn Bowen 206-382-2018.

CATHOLIC RELIEF SERVICES’ OPERATION RICE BOWL. As we reflect on the passion of Christ, we remember that more than 44 million people in the United States are living in poverty. While 75% of our gifts to Operation Rice Bowl support Catholic Relief Services’ programs in other nations, 25% of our contributions remain in our diocese to support local hunger and poverty alleviation efforts. Your prayers, fasting, learning, and almsgiving through Operation Rice Bowl make a significant difference in the lives of people in our own community and in communities around the world. Be sure to bring your Rice Bowls to the Evening Mass of the Lord’s Supper on Holy Thursday.

MENTAL HEALTH CAREGIVERS. The St. James Mental Health Ministry is offering a series of biblically-based prayer and support group sessions for parishioners who care for relatives and friends living with mental illness. Getting to know Good Pope John

Later this year, thanks to some generous parishioners, a permanent shrine to Blessed John XXIII, the Pope who called the Second Vatican Council, will be established in the north aisle of the Cathedral. Throughout the year, keep an eye on this box in the bulletin to learn more about this great saint of modern times.

When, following his election as Pope, he was carried on the sedia gestatoria for the first time on November 4, 1958, John XXIII recalled a time in his childhood when he was carried on his father’s shoulders: “Once again I am being carried, carried aloft by my sons. More than seventy years ago I was carried on the shoulders of my father at Ponte San Pietro. The secret of everything is to let oneself be carried by God, and so to carry him to others.”
