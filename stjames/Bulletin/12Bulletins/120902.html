WELCOME! If you are a visitor to the Cathedral, we want you to know how welcome you are – whether you have come from another part of the country, from across the world, or simply from another parish here in the Archdiocese. Thanks, in part, to the generosity of visitors like you, we are able to celebrate the Church’s liturgy here in a fitting and beautiful way. We are also able to offer a wide range of services to needy people living in the central district of our city.

FLOWERS ON THE ALTAR are in loving memory of David Pettigrew from his family. If you would like to offer flowers, contact Elizabeth Falzone, 206-654-4650.

LABOR DAY HOLIDAY. On Monday, September 3, the parish office will be closed in observance of Labor Day. There will be one Mass only in the Cathedral, at 9:00am.

OUR NATIONALLY RECOGNIZED YOUTH MUSIC PROGRAM starts fall classes on September 10. We have two training choirs, our advanced Schola Cantorum, and Jubilate!, our young women’s choir. For information regarding schedule, auditions, tuition, scholarships and performances visit the Cathedral Music website. or contact the program director, Stacey Sunde, 206-941-9450.

THE NEXT SENIOR TRIP, Wednesday, September 12, will take us to the Point Defiance Zoo. This zoo is large enough to offer a wide array of animals yet small enough to let you get really close to them. You'll see seahorses and sharks, peacocks and penguins, wolves and walrus, all in one place. It offers beautiful views of Puget Sound, Mount Rainier and the Olympic Mountains too. Space is limited and priority will be given to registered parishioners! Information, or to reserve your place, John Simpson, Pastoral Care Minister, 206-274-3173.

OPPORTUNITIES TO SERVE

WINTER SHELTER NEEDS OVERNIGHT HOSTS AND BREAKFAST COOKS. St. James Cathedral will soon start its 21st season partnering with St. Martin de Porres Shelter to provide overflow sleeping space during the cold winter months. Four nights a week, from October through April, we provide a warm refuge in Cathedral Hall for 10-15 men. Nearly 100 cathedral parishioners serve as overnight hosts and breakfast cooks. Please consider joining our Winter Shelter Team. Training for new and returning volunteers will be held Sunday, September 23 from 6:30 to 8:30pm in Cathedral Hall. Information, Patty Bowman, 206-382-4515.

DO YOU ENJOY SHARING YOUR FAITH WITH YOUNG PEOPLE? Please consider joining the team for RCIA/C (Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults - Adapted for Older Children). Catechists help the children learn about being a Catholic Christian during Sunday morning classes (10:30am–11:45am). Dismissal Leaders accompany the children as they “feast” on the Scriptures during the Noon Mass. Materials and training provided. Information, Brenda Bellamy, 206-274-3108.

SOLANUS CASEY CENTER, our parish’s drop-in center for homeless and low-income people, is looking for volunteers to help in the areas of hospitality, resource referral, and ministry of listening and presence. Must be available Monday, Wednesday or Friday from 1:00 to 4:00 pm. The Solanus Casey Center serves approximately 300 people per month. Your help is essential to continuing and expanding the work of this important outreach ministry. Information, Sr. Peggy Kennedy, 206-223-0907.

THE CATHEDRAL KITCHEN is looking for three new gleaners. The commitment is one morning a week. Each morning, two volunteers take the Cathedral van and go to three PCC Markets and the Madison Market, to collect and sort produce, dairy, deli, and dry goods. These donated food items are used in our daily meal. If you would like to become involved in this outreach, contact Jill McAuliffe, 206-264-2091.

CALLED TO PROTECT FOR MINISTRIES. On Saturday, September 22 at 10am, St. James Cathedral is offering this important safe environment training. All church personnel are required to complete this foundational training, including all employees of the Archdiocese of Seattle and all volunteers with ongoing unsupervised contact with minors and vulnerable adults. Preregistration is encouraged. Information, Elizabeth Falzone, 206-382-4650.

ARE YOU INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH AND/OR BECOMING CATHOLIC? You are invited to participate in the RCIA, a process of initiation into the Catholic community of faith. It is a process which encompasses the study of Scripture, Catholic teaching, and Christian values—the development and deepening of a life of prayer and action—all with the support and involvement of the parish community. You can learn more by going to the RCIA website. A new inquiry session will begin Wednesday, September 12 at 7:00 pm in Cathedral Place, second floor. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

ARE YOU A CATHOLIC WHO HAS BEEN AWAY FROM THE CHURCH? Our Welcome Back program provides a safe place, in the company of others who are also examining their faith, to ask questions about returning to regular church attendance, returning to the sacraments, and to learn about changes in the church. The sessions are offered on Mondays, September 17 through December 10, 7:00-9:00pm on 2nd floor Cathedral Place. Classes are offered without any obligation. Information, TerryAnn Bowen, 206-382-2018.

Getting to know Good Pope John

On September 30, thanks to some generous parishioners, a permanent shrine to Blessed John XXIII, the Pope who called the Second Vatican Council, will be established in the north aisle of the Cathedral.

“The Council. God knows that I opened my small soul to this great inspiration with the utmost simplicity. Will he grant me enough time to finish it? May he be praised if he does not grant it. I shall see the happy conclusion from heaven, where I hope, and am even certain, Divine Mercy will allow me to enter.”

Recounted in The Wit and Wisdom of Good Pope John

PARISH REMEMBRANCE: Throughout the year, because we are the Cathedral Church, we remember in prayer at Mass and Vespers each of the parishes and missions of the Archdiocese of Seattle on a Sunday near their feast day. This week we remember in prayer the parishes of St. Monica on Mercer Island, St. Augustine in Oak Harbor, and St. Mary of the Valley in Monroe.
